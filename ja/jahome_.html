The Department of Japanese Antiquities houses one of the most comprehensive collections of Japanese material culture in Europe, comprising about 25,000 objects. The collection includes early archaeological material; the arts of Buddhism; secular sculpture; netsuke and inro; lacquerware; swords and sword furniture;metalwork; secular paintings, prints and printed books; textiles; ceramics including porcelain and tea ceremony wares and utensils. For the pre-20th century, the decorative arts are among the finest in the Western world, while for paintings and prints the collection is pre-eminent within Europe, both for scope and quality.

Many of the objects were collected by specialists in the past and either given or bequeathed to the Museum. Important antiquities not represented in other European collections continue to be judiciously acquired wherever possible, often from Japanese sources, and the Japanese Galleries have proved the stimulus for many generous gifts.
