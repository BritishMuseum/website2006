Japanese Prints of the Showa Era (1926-89)
Montague Place entrance Level 2, Corridor

Admission free

The display begins with works by two of Japan's leading 20th printmakers, Onchi Koshiro and Munakata Shiko, exemplifying two contrasting strands of the Sosaku Hanga ('creative print') movement. Other artists, such as Tetsuya Noda with his ongoing diary of highly personal images, demonstrate the later Showa era proliferation of techniques and styles which has won worldwide recognition for modern Japanese prints. Arts of Japan:Selections from the Permanent Collection

There are Japanese Buddhist sculptures on the North Stairs and prehistoric ceramics in the Montague Place Entrance
