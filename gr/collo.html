Cleaning the Parthenon Sculptures
The British Museum XXIIIrd Classical Colloquium
30th November - 1st December 1999

In 1937-38 a controversial cleaning of some of the Parthenon Sculptures took place in the British Museum, while they were being prepared for exhibition in a new gallery given by Lord Joseph Duveen. The methods used had not been officially authorised, and the popular press of the day was therefore much interested in a perceived scandal.

The press scandal was revived in 1998 following a grant of “privileged access” by the Museum to its papers on the subject. Erroneous and confused accounts began to circulate, and so the Museum decided to hold an international conference and to make available all the documents in its archives that it could find on the subject. Copies of these were sent out on 5th November 1999 to all delegates, interested parties and the media and the conference followed at the end of the month. Some 300 delegates attended, including many representatives of the media, as well as an international panel of invited experts in relevant fields. Speakers included a number from Greece, as well as Britain and Germany. The original documents were evaluated and the surface of the Sculptures themselves assessed for evidence of how the cleaning may have affected them. The conference was itself much reported in the press. One distinguished British journalist, for example, described it as ‘a breath of fresh air’.
