Students wishing to work on objects in the Department's collections should apply to the Keeper in writing. Those who are not already known personally to a member of the Department must provide a letter of introduction or other reference from a professor, or tutor or other qualified person and should be prepared to provide proof of identity on arrival.

Applications should be made at least 48 hours in advance, and correct identification of objects by Catalogue or Register number is essential. Students are warned that the number of antiquities that can be brought to the Study Room at any one time may be limited by the availability of storage space. It would be helpful if students could give some indication of the length of their stay. If for some reason a student cannot keep an appointment, the Department should be informed. The opening hours of the Study Room are Monday to Friday, 10.00 - 13.00 and 14.15 - 16.00.

All objects made available to students will be listed in the Issue Book, to be signed by the student on issue and by an authorised member of the Department on return. Objects must be returned and signed for whenever a student leaves the Department for any reason. Students are asked to inform the staff of their intention to leave the Study Room. Rubbings, squeezes and tracings are not normally allowed, but applications may be made to the Keeper or Duty Officer for exceptions. The British Museum can provide squeezes and casts of objects, but a charge will be made. No metal drawing or measuring tools may be used. Permission to take photographs for study purposes is granted only on condition that great care is taken in handling and setting up the objects and that other students are not inconvenienced. Students should apply to the Keeper for permission to reproduce such photographs: the British Museum normally insists that its own photographs are used for publications. In the case of an emergency evacuation of the building, students will be asked to leave the Department as quickly as possible. Staff will give them every assistance to ensure their safety.

Personal possessions left in the Department at any time are at the owner's risk and the British Museum can accept no responsibility. The British Museum reserves the right to search any personal bags or containers that visitors bring into the Department.

1st April 2001 Keeper of Greek and Roman Antiquities
