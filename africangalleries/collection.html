The initial display of over 600 objects has been drawn from across the entire continent. Traditionally under-represented regions such as the Maghrib, Northeast Africa and Madagascar will be strongly featured. In addition to the rich collections from ancient kingdoms such as Benin and Asante, the work of some of Africa's foremost contemporary artists will be displayed, emphasising both their innovative qualities and the dynamism and continuity of many cultural and artistic traditions. There will be sections examining wood sculpture, masks and masquerade, pottery, brass-casting, forged metalwork, textiles and items of personal adornment.

Through the Galleries, the diversity and vitality of the continent's artistic achievements is revealed by focussing on aspects of African cultural life, past and present. Video presentations and slide sequences provide a context for some of the items on display.
