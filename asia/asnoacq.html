Phoenix headed ewer
Stoneware with monochrome emerald-green glaze North Vietnam, 15th century The tradition of producing bird-headed ewers with a green glaze originates in China and dates back at least to the 1st century AD. Sculptural forms were particularly popular for pouring wine.

 

 


 

Shrine with an image of Zhenwu, the God of the North
Stoneware with fahua palette enamels North China, late 16th- early 17th century.

 

 

 

 

 


Pouring vessel in the form of a mandarin fish
Stoneware with underglaze iron decoration North Vietnam, 15th century There is a long tradition of manufacturing ewers and water droppers in the form of fish in Vietnam and Cambodia. This pouring vessel was made to be suspended through the hole in the fin. Its chocolate base and smooth body clay are typical of Vietnamese production.

 

 

Ewer in the form of a crouching mythical beast
Stoneware with moulded and incised details and with underglaze blue decoration North Vietnam, 15th century Vietnamese ewers in the form of elephants are well known but this is unique. It was probably used for pouring potent rice wine into small cups.
