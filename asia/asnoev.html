COURT CULTURE IN QING CHINA : NEW DIRECTIONS IN RESEARCH
Saturday 4 & Sunday 5 March 2006

Sponsored by the RoyalAcademy of Arts, Department of Art and Archaeology and Centre for Chinese Studies, SOAS, and MertonCollege, Oxford.

A major two day international symposium which builds on the achievements of the landmark exhibition China : The Three Emperors 1662-1795 . Leading scholars from China , the United States and Britain debate the pressing new questions this unprecedented exhibition poses and the new directions in research it provokes. How do art and visual culture inflect our understanding of the culture of rulership in Qing China ? Through consideration of other historical and geographical contexts, this conference seeks to expand our definition of ‘court culture’ and provides a significant opportunity for scholars to re-angle research into this important period in Chinese history. Participants include Craig Clunas, Yuan Hongqi, Zhang Hongxing, Regina Krahl, Shane McCausland, Alfreda Murck, Susan Naquin, Jessica Rawson , Jan Stuart, Ren Wanping, Evelyn Welch and Verity Wilson.

Displaying Korea and Japan
A workshop organised by the Department of Asia, British Museum and the Sainsbury Institute
Thursday 10 November 2005
Workshop: 10.00 - 17.00
Documentary film screening: 17.20 -18.50
Stevenson Lecture Theatre, The British Museum
Free admission

Korea and Japan are Asian neighbours with historically interlinked cultures. This one-day public workshop will debate issues relating to the display of Korean and Japanese objects. Topics will include how to define Korea and Japan, both past and present; special considerations when displaying Asian objects outside their countries of origin; and national cultural policies. The workshop marks the designation of 2005-6 as Japan-Korea Friendship Year.

The workshop will be followed by a film screening of the documentary Osaka Story (1994), directed and produced by Tôichi Nakata.
