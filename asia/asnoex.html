PERMANENT EXHIBITION

7000 years of Chinese jade
from the collection of Sir Joseph Hotung
Room 33b

"Soft, smooth and glossy, it appeared to them like benevolence; fine, compact and strong - like intelligence" Attributed to Confucius (c. 551-479 BC).From very ancient times jade has been the material of the highest value to the Chinese. Beautiful and mysterious, it was worn by Chinese kings and nobles in life as well as death, endowed with symbolic as well as material significance. This exhibition illustrates the history of jade in China from c.5000 BC to the present day. The majority of the jades are from the collection of Sir Joseph Hotung.
