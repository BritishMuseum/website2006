PERSONAL STUDY OF THE COLLECTIONS

Tickets (for the viewing of original material from the collections)

(a) Visitors who wish to view objects from the Asia collection, should apply for a Students' Room Ticket on the form supplied by the Department, normally at least one month before the ticket is required.

(b) The Department will contact the referee, and if approved, will arrange an appointment with the applicant. Access to the collections is by appointment only and subject to availability.

(c) A valid ticket of admission must be produced at each visit. This is issued for six months and may be renewed on application. Tickets are not transferable.

(d) Tickets are not issued to anyone under the age of eighteen except by special arrangement.

(e) Ticket Holders wishing to view objects from the collection must make an appointment, Monday, Tuesday, Thursday or Friday 2:15 – 4:00 . Wednesdays are unavailable due to Opinion Day.

STUDENTS' ROOM PROCEDURE

All visitors to the Students' Room must write their name, home address and any temporary address in this country in block letters in the Visitors' Book.

Visitors are requested, where possible to leave all coats, umbrellas and bags in the main Museum cloakrooms at the front and back of the building. Any other bags should not be placed on the table or underneath it. Bags, briefcases etc will be examined before visitors' departure from the Students' Room.

Requests for material should be made on the form provided or in writing, noting the registration number and location of the object(s) wherever possible. No verbal requests will be taken. Advanced notice, of a minimum of 72 hours, is required for all requests.

All work by visitors to the Students' Room must be done in pencil

Objects will be prepared at the discretion of the Students' Room staff. The best efforts will be made to prepare all requested objects but due to other commitments objects may not be made available. A maximum of seven objects will be issued per visit, including no more than two objects from display.

All objects/paintings will be counted out on delivery to the visitor and recounted on their return to the Students' Room staff.

In the case of an emergency, visitors must follow instructions issued by museum staff at all times.

Visitors are not permitted to compare their own objects with those in the collection without prior approval of the Keeper, or to remove any property from the Students' Room.

HANDLING OF OBJECTS

The handling of objects will be monitored by the Students' Room staff at all times, and assistance given where felt necessary.

Visitors are required to wash their hands before handling original material. Visitors may also be required to wear gloves when handling objects. These will be provided by the Students' Room staff.

Visitors are not allowed to touch the surface of any painting, print or drawing. Easels should normally be used for the examination of mounted drawings. However, mounted drawings in solander boxes must be handled separately and be placed singly from one side of the solander to the other.

PHOTOGRAPHY

Visitors are not permitted to take photographs of objects examined in the Students' Room. However, the Museum's Photographic Service undertakes a wide range of public orders. All photographic orders must be made through the Departmental Photographic Officer (0207 323 8416). Details of the service, current prices and order forms are available in the Students' Room. Visitors are advised to place orders well in advance of the required date.
