Medea is the archetypal sorceress of ancient Greek mythology. The granddaughter of the sun-god Helios, she has grown up as a princess in the land of Colchis, beyond the Black Sea. Here she meets the Greek hero Jason who has come to Colchis with the Argonauts to obtain the legendary Golden Fleece. Having fallen in love with him she helps him to fulfil his task and returns with him to his Greek homeland. With her she brings, however, her abilities as sorceress, which she puts to use for Jason by deceitfully killing Jason's rival for the throne Pelias: Taking advantage of his daughters' desire to rejuvenate their father she demonstrates that by dismembering and boiling an old ram it can turn into a young lamb again; the trick of course fails to work for Pelias who loses his life in the process. The episode demonstrates the dangerous nature of women sorceresses, which is further confirmed by Medea's later killing of her children in revenge for Jason rejection of her in favour of a new wife. This tragedy of Medea's abandonment by Jason far away from her own home and her revenge on him was the focus of Euripides' play Medea which stands at the beginning of a long line of treatments of the theme in writing, painting and music up to modern times.
