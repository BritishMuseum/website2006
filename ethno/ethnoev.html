New Seminar Series
2006

The British Museum Centre for Anthropology is organising a new seminar series on current debates in anthropology, broadly focused on ideas of the visual and the material. We are seeking to encourage the use of the resources offered by the Centre and hope that this seminar series will provide a forum for the wider anthropological community.

 Featuring myths, scripting reality: Second Native North American Film Festival
26–28 November
Clore Education Centre
 Admission free by ticket only

www.thebritishmuseum.ac.uk/filmfestival

This festival presents nine historical and contemporary documentaries and features, by and about Native North Americans, including Nanook of the North (1922), The Silent Enemy (1930), The Business of Fancydancing (2002), and The Dreamkeeper (2003). The films will be discussed by panels of Natives, film-makers and scholars. We open with two documentaries of the Netsilik Film Series about traditional Inuit life in the 1920s, discussed by the Hon. Peter Irniq, and film-maker Asen Balikci.

Conference
Marketing Native North America:
The promotion and sale of art and design
4-6 February 2005
A Centre for Anthropology event
Supported by the Eugene V and Clare E Thaw Charitable Trust, Santa Fe

From 4-6 February 2005 the Centre for Anthropology of the British Museum will host an international conference on Marketing Native North America: The promotion and sale of art and design. The conference will explore the marketing of Native North American art and design, and issues regarding copyright and appropriation, with contributors from Native and non-Native professional, scholarly and commercial communities.

The conference will be organised thematically into eight sessions with two or three speakers each. To allow time for discussion and questions, papers must be no longer than 20 minutes. 200 word abstracts should be sent or emailed by 15 November 2004 to Birgit Pauksztat, at the address below. We would welcome all proposals that would fit into the general parameters as delineated by the Call for Papers .

Limited funding is available for travel and accommodation, especially for speakers who are not otherwise supported by institutions.
