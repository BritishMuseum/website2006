With funding from the British Museum Friends, the Department has just acquired 49 felt textiles from central and eastern Turkey, the result of a commissioned collection made on the Museum's behalf by Selcuk Gurisik, a research student at Central St Martin's School of Art / Open University. Selcuk has been working with some of the traditional makers for almost ten years, and made the collection during summer 2001 when he visited the eight different centres where decorated felt textiles are still made, purchasing examples from all the extant workshops. The majority of the pieces were made this year, although one large floor cover is possibly 40 years old. Despite the similarity of technique, the range of work is striking: multi-coloured patterns embedded into the fibres of the felt fabric. The collection comes with contextual documentation; photographs profiling the makers and their workshops; raw materials; felt-making and -decorating techniques; motif terms and meanings, and felt textile use. Many of the textiles collected are large floor covers for special occasions such as weddings and circumcision ceremonies, or large prayer gatherings. Some smaller mats and amulets decorated with appliqué designs, buttons and shells have been developed with the tourist market in mind. Three costume items are included: a one-piece hooded felt shepherd's cloak, and two Dervish hats.
