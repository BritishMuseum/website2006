  IMPORTANT NOTICE:

Centre for Anthropology, British Museum
The Ethnography Department of the British Museum is returning to Bloomsbury and, under the banner of the Centre for Anthropology, services will recommence on 10 May 2004.

The Centre is located inside the north entrance
of the British Museum. Services on offer include the Anthropology Library and Collections enquiries service.

Library
The Anthropology Library of the British Museum's Ethnography Department will re-open to the public as part of the new Centre for Anthropology, in the British Museum, Bloomsbury, near the north entrance, on 10th May 2004.

Fellows of the Royal Anthropological Insititute (RAI) will be able to use the Library from 13th April 2004. >> more

Collections enquiries service

The Collections enquiries service (formerly known as the Study Room) is normally the department's first point of contact for public enquiries or research requests relating to our collections.

Until the 10 May 2004 the Study Room will not, as a matter of course, be staffed, therefore both our telephone numbers, 020 7323 8041 and 8044 will usually not be answered but will generate a message.

Callers are encouraged to consult our website or check with the British Museum information desk, if their enquiries relate to a particular curatorial area, in order that they can direct them to the appropriate person. The Department of Ethnography's pages on the British Museum website also provide details of our staff and their curatorial areas of responsibility.

Similarly, written and E-mail enquiries will be dealt with more quickly if you are able to identify the relevant curator. E-mails to the Ethnography Department address, rather than to specific individuals, and general letters to the Study Room can not be dealt with as comprehensively as previously. We will respond as efficiently as possible but the Study Room curator will not be able to deal with enquiries which entail time-consuming research.

Any updates to this notice will be posted on our website. Information concerning the move of the Anthropology Library and Departmental offices is posted on the notices page.

We apologise for the inconvenience this will cause but it is unavoidable if we want to carry out our move within the proposed timescale and be able to set up our new, improved, Study Room facilities back at the main British Museum.

4 April 2003
