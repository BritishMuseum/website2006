Department of Africa, Oceania and the Americas > research > fieldwork > Romanian masquerade

This research project focuses on a New Year masquerade tradition in Moldavia, northeastern Romania. The study has been developed in collaboration with the Museum of the Romanian Peasant (MRP) in Bucharest. Through ten months of field-based research, carried out in the period 1994-8, Sarah Posey, an anthropologist from the British Museum's Department of Ethnography, worked alongside Gabriel Hanganu, an ethnographic documentary film-maker from the MRP, charting the winter festival of capra in the context of day-to-day life in a village in Bacau county.

Field data, taped interviews, photographs and video have built up a rich record of contemporary life in rural Romania, and at a time of far-reaching political, economic and social change. In the context of fieldwork, Ms Posey has also acquired a small number of contemporary masks and masquerade outfits from a number of centres in Moldavia, and collected textiles and costume items from two villages in particular.

The research is currently being written up for future publication.
