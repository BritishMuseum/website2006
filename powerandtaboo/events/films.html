Film Season

£3, concessions £2 (unless stated)
Stevenson Lecture Theatre (unless stated)

Free lunchtime films are in collaboration with the Royal Anthropological Institute
To book please contact the Box Office:
Telephone: +44 (0)20 7323 8181
Email: boxoffice@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk

The Last Navigator

Friday 17 November, 13.30

An American is taught the skills of traditional Micronesian navigation and attempts to cross dangerous waters.
Director: Andre Singer
USA, 1989, 50 mins

Admission free, booking required

Paradise Bent: Boys will be girls in Samoa

Friday 24 November, 13.00

A study of the Samoan fa’afafine: boys who are raised as girls and play an important role in Samoan culture.
Director: Heather Croall
Australia, 1999, 51 mins

Admission free, booking required

Mutiny on the Bounty (1935)

Friday 1 December, 14.00
BP Lecture Theatre

With Charles Laughton as the scowling Captain Bligh and Clark Gable as Fletcher Christian.
Director: Frank Lloyd
USA, 1935, 132 mins

Samoan Wedding

Friday 1 December, 19.30

Brand new urban romantic comedy set in Auckland.
Director: Chris Graham
New Zealand, 2006, 97 mins

Matai Samoa

Monday 4 December, 13.30

A film based around archive footage shot by anthropologist George Milner in the 1950s which focuses on the traditional Samoan way of life.
Director: George Milner
Samoa, 1989, 65 mins

Admission free, booking required

Moana

Friday 8 December, 17.00

Flaherty’s film attempts to capture the traditional daily life of the Pacific islanders.
Director: Robert J. Flaherty
USA, 1926, 85 mins

Tabu

Friday 8 December, 18.30

Murnau’s last film is a simple Pacific Islands folk tale of two young lovers who violate a local taboo and have to flee their village with tragic consequences.
Directors: F.W. Murnau and Robert J. Flaherty
USA, 1931, 84 mins
