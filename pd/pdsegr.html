services > group visits          
 

Many teachers and lecturers wish to teach parties of their students in front of works in the Study Room, and we make facilities available on the centre table in the Department for them to do so. We cannot provide Departmental staff to do any teaching, but we can put out whatever selection of works the tutor wishes to talk about.

The way this works is as follows:

There are a limited number of slots available either morning or afternoon during our normal opening times (weekdays 10.00-13.00 and 14.15-16.00). There is a considerable demand, and places are usually taken quickly. So the sooner a booking is made, the better. This can be done by letter to the Superintendent of the Study Room, by phone on +44 (0)20 7323 8408 or fax on +44 (0)20 7323 8999, or by email to aroche@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk.

Parties are limited by the space in the Study Room to a maximum of 10, including the tutor. Larger groups can be brought into the Study Room in shifts.

The centre table can hold approximately 24 works (fewer if the works are large, more if small). A list of the precise objects that are needed must be supplied at least a week in advance to the Superintendent by post or fax. Please provide as complete references as possible, including register numbers and references to standard catalogues. The Superintendent can give advice on what forms of reference will enable us to locate the works with the greatest ease.

We ask that the group tutor keeps the party together, and makes sure that members do not start wandering round the Study Room. Pencils, not ink, should be used for taking notes.

No fee is charged for this facility, though we are always pleased if donations are made in recognition of our help.
