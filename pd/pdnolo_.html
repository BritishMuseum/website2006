Due to the size and high quality of the collection, the Department of Prints and Drawings receive numerous requests for loans every year. At present we are lending over one thousand works a year to between seventy and eighty museums and galleries. Loans can consist of a single work, or an entire exhibition.

An exhibition sponsored by "la Caixa" Foundation is currently on show in Spain:

La coleccíon de estampas de Hernando Colón (1488-1539): Coleccionismo en la Era del Descubrimiento

The Print Collection of Ferdinand Columbus (1488-1539): Collecting in the Age of Discovery

4 October - 19 December 2004
Seville, Real Alcázar

The print collection of Ferdinand, son of Christopher Columbus, is the earliest known to historians. The prints themselves were dispersed long ago, but an inventory preserved in Seville describes 3200 engravings, woodcuts and maps. The exhibition presents a partial reconstruction of this collection with 120 prints by all the most important Renaissance printmakers. Included are works from Italy by Antonio Polllaiuolo, Marcantonio Raimondi and Giovanni Battista Palumba; from Germany by Albrecht Dürer, Albrect Altdorfer, Hans Baldung and Hans Weiditz; from the low countries by Lucas van Leyden and Jost de Negker. Many are large format prints such as maps that have rarely been exhibited. A highlight of the exhibition is a stencil coloured genealogical tree of the House of Charles V by Robert Peril that is 7.3 metres long.

The exhibition will be shown at the British Museum from 10 February to 5 June 2005

In collaboration with Hayward Gallery / National Touring Exhibitions we have run a programme since 1993 of entire exhibitions of prints from the collection which have been circulated to many regional galleries. These have included Piranesi: The Imaginary Prisons (2003-04), Followers of Fashion (2001-02), The Popular Print in England (2000-01)

For those planning exhibitions a sheet of advice for potential borrowers is available from the Department.
