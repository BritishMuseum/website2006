How many prints and drawings are there in the collection?
There are about 50,000 drawings and over two million prints. It is one of the greatest collections of its kind in the world.

Do you have Japanese prints?
The Department collects prints and drawings made in Europe from the end of the 14th century to the present day, and in other countries that are culturally linked to Europe such as the United States. Japanese prints are in the Department of Asia, together with Islamic, Indian and Chinese prints and drawings.

Do you have manuscripts and illustrated books?
The national collection of manuscripts and illustrated books is in the British Library. The Department of Prints and Drawings only has a small collection of historic printed books, with particular strengths in the German Renaissance and British publications of the 1860s.

Do you have reference books on prints and drawings?
Yes, there is an excellent reference library with over 50,000 titles which can be read in the Study Room.

Do you still collect?
Yes, through purchases and gifts we add about 600 items a year to the collection.

Do you have modern prints and drawings?
Yes, we collect up to the present day.

How much of the collection is on show?
Only three works are on permanent display. Selections are made from the rest of the collection for exhibitions in Room 90, which are changed three times a year. Exhibitions are chosen to show highlights of the collection, to give an idea of its range, and to advance knowledge in neglected areas. We also lend over a thousand works a year to exhibitions held in other institutions throughout the world.

Can I see what is not on display?
Yes, by coming to the Study Room at any time during opening hours. Since the collection is so huge, it is essential that you have a clear idea of what you want to see if we are to be able to help you.

Can I see the catalogue?
There are printed catalogues for a few areas of the collection, especially the old master drawings and the satirical prints. We have so far catalogued on computer the entire collection of drawings and well over 100,000 prints. Plans are underway to make the database available on the web; in the meantime, it can be seen in the study room.

How quickly can work be got out for visitors?
All drawings and most prints can be produced in a matter of minutes, if they can be properly identified. However through lack of space certain portfolios, including the series of engraved portraits, are kept on another site in West London. They can be seen there by appointment on Tuesday mornings. The only restricted item is a sketchbook of architectural and figure drawings by the Renaissance artist Jacopo Bellini, which is extremely fragile.

Can I buy a print?
No, none of the collection can be sold. But you can order a photograph of anything in the collection.
