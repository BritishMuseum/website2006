Upper floors Room 90
Exhibitions of prints and drawings are changed several times a year because long exposure to light causes works on paper to fade and discolour.


Three works from the Department of Prints and Drawings are normally on display in the Museum:
Albrecht Dürer, The Triumphal Arch, woodcut, 1515, in Room 12
Jacopo de' Barbari, View of Venice, woodcut, 1500, in the Study Room lobby off Room 90 Michelangelo, Epifania, black chalk, around 1550, Room 90
Other prints and drawings can be seen in the Study Room
