Due to the size and high quality of the collection, the Department of Prints and Drawings receive numerous requests for loans every year. At present we are lending over one thousand works a year to between seventy and eighty museums and galleries. Loans can consist of a single work, or an entire exhibition.

Michelangelo Drawings: closer to the master will be on exhibition at the Teyler Museum, Haarlem, from October 2005 to January 2006 before being shown at the British Museum from March to June 2006.

Clouet to Seurat: French Drawings from the British Museum will be shown at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York, from November 2005 to January 2006 before moving to the British Museum.

In collaboration with Hayward Gallery / National Touring Exhibitions we have run a programme since 1993 of entire exhibitions of prints from the collection which have been circulated to many regional galleries. An exhibition of prints by Rembrandt is planned for 2006. Previous exhibitions have included Piranesi: The Imaginary Prisons (2003-04), and Followers of Fashion (2001-02).

For those planning exhibitions a sheet of advice for potential borrowers is available from the Department.
