Three great gifts
Room 90
11 December - 25 April 2004
Admission free

The British Museum is increasingly dependent on the generosity of private individuals or bodies. These displays pay tribute to three such benefactors.

FRED WILLIAMS: AN AUSTRALIAN VISION
Fred Williams (1927-1982) was one of the outstanding Australian artists of the 20th century. His work is still little known outside Australia. This is the first significant exhibition of his graphic work in Britain and is made possible by the most generous gift of Lyn Williams, the artist's widow. A catalogue accompanies the exhibition; both have been supported by News International Limited.

Lecture: Irena Zdanowicz `Fred Williams and the tradition of Australian landscape painting' on 12 December at 13.15 in the Stevenson Lecture Theatre, Clore Education Centre. Irena Zdanowicz, formerly Senior Curator of Prints and Drawings at the National Gallery of Victoria, Melbourne, is co-author of the principal essay in the catalogue.

MODERN BRITISH ART: THE DAVID BROWN BEQUEST
David Brown (1925-2002) was a unique combination of veterinary scientist, art historian and curator. A much-loved figure in the British art world, he bequeathed to the British Museum over two hundred works on paper from his important art collection. On display is a selection, including works by Roger Hilton, Gilbert and George and Ian Hamilton Finlay.

100 YEARS OF THE NATIONAL ART COLLECTIONS FUND
The National Art Collections Fund, celebrating its centenary in 2003, has supported the acquisition of works of art on paper by many artists including Bosch, Holbein, Rembrandt, Van Gogh, Courbet, Tiepolo, Picasso and Jake and Dinos Chapman.
