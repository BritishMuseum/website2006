research > Columbus Print Collection Project          
 

The Department of Prints and Drawings is hosting a project, funded by the J. Paul Getty Trust, http://web.getty.edu/grants/, that is transforming our understanding of the history of Renaissance printmaking, collecting and markets. The aim of the project is to reconstruct the print collection of Ferdinand Columbus (1488-1539), the son of Christopher Columbus. Ferdinand's collection of 3200 engravings, woodcuts and maps was built up during his journeys throughout Europe while on diplomatic missions for the Spanish monarchy. The prints themselves have been lost but they are described in great detail in an inventory which survives in the Columbus archive in Seville. Dr Mark McDonald leads a team of international experts on Renaissance printmaking in this exciting project that combines interactive computer technology with traditional art historical methods. One of the prints that Columbus owned was Albrecht Dürer's Rhinoceros woodcut, AD 1515. The results in two volumes with a CD-ROM are being published by British Museum Press in April 2004.
