teaching > MA course          
 

The Department of Prints and Drawings in the British Museum and the History of Art Department of University College London offer every year a course on the History of the Print. The course is one of the special options within the MA degree in the History of Art at University College London, and results from a unique collaboration between the two institutions. The teaching is done by the staff of both departments and as many classes as possible are given in front of original prints, either in the Study Room of the British Museum or the Strang Print Room in University College London. The aim is to give a grounding in the history of European prints and printmaking from the 15th to the 21st century.

The course is intended to appeal to recent graduates with a background in Art History, History, Cultural Studies or Fine Art, as well as to mature students and to museum curators who wish to develop their expertise in this area. Candidates should normally have a good BA degree or overseas equivalent with History of Art as a component in the degree, though other qualifications, including relevant experience will be considered in exceptional circumstances.

The course can be taken full-time in one year, or part-time over two years, the year being the academic calendar year from October to September. Assessment is made on the basis of essay work, written examinations (in June) and a dissertation (to be handed in by mid-September).
