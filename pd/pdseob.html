We do what we can to help identify any prints, drawings and watercolours that belong to members of the public.

Please note the following tips:
We only deal with works on paper. Paintings should be taken either to the National Gallery (if foreign and before 1900), or the Tate (if British or after 1900).

We only deal with Western works. Works on paper from non-Western countries should be taken to the Department of Asia, or to the Department of Africa, Oceania and the Americas.

Please make sure that you bring the original work with you, preferably unframed. It is very difficult, and often impossible, to say anything useful from a photograph.

We are not permitted to give valuations or express any views about value. If it is a valuation that you need, you should take the work to an auctioneer or dealer.

Study Room, Wednesday, 10.00 - 13.00, 14.15 -16.00 except when closed for cleaning or bank holidays (see noticeboard)
This service does not provide valuations or certificates of authentication; advice of this nature should be sought from a dealer or auctioneer.
