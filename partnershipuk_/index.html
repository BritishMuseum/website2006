The Museum in Britain

The Museum in Britain together with The Museum in London and The Museum in the World is an expression of the British Museum’s core purpose to hold in trust for the benefit of humanity a collection representative of world cultures. Central to this purpose is sharing the Museum’s collections and expertise as widely as possible.

A principal component of The Museum in Britain is our Partnership UK scheme. It covers Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland as well as England, where the partnership has been extended to represent all the hubs formed as part of Renaissance in the Regions, administered by the Museums, Libraries and Archives Council (MLA). There are thirteen principal partners, which serve as conduits to a wider network of relationships.

The criteria for inclusion are:

• geographic and demographic spread of the group of partners as a whole, with representation of those covering predominantly urban areas and those with a greater preponderance of rural population

• synergy of collections between the partners and the British Museum

• relationship to cultural and educational infrastructure of the region

The British Museum is committed

 • to being a national museum for the nation by making its collections visible throughout the U.K.

• to a fully collaborative partnership with the museums concerned. They will mediate the BM’s role in the cultural life of the regions, including both Hub and non-Hub museums in England, and the development of links with education at all levels throughout the U.K. The British Museum in turn will provide a conduit for the partners to its metropolitan and international cultural and scholarly activity.
