1. Aims of the Programme
The British Museum is committed to working in partnership with other museums and galleries throughout the United Kingdom. The partners will collaborate together in various activities, including touring exhibitions and training courses. The purpose of these collaborations is to enable exhibitions and collections to be seen by a wider section of the UK population, and to contribute to the development of those who work in UK museums. An important element of this partnership is the sharing of skills and expertise between institutions of all sizes and from all parts of the country. The British Museum therefore is establishing a programme of staff secondments to and from the Museum.

2. Host Organisations
Secondments, inward and outward, will be between the British Museum and any of museums and galleries who form part of the collaborative partner network.

3. The Secondments
The secondments will normally last between two and four weeks and should focus on a project of demonstrable benefit to both partner institutions. Such projects might include loan exhibitions, joint education programmes and collections mapping, and could act as a catalyst to longer-term partnerships.

4. The Bursaries
 There will be up to 4 bursaries per annum (2 inward and 2 outward). Each bursary will be to a maximum of £3,000 to cover transport, subsistence and accommodation expenses. All secondments (inward and outward) will be funded by The British Museum. During the period of the secondment salaries will remain the responsibility of the respective employer.

5. Candidates
 Inward - staff from any of the partner museums currently undertaking any type of museum work who have 2 years of museum (or associated cultural heritage) experience.
Outward - any member of staff undertaking any type of museum work who has been at the British Museum for at least 2 years
