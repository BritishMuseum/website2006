Long-term loan to British Empire and Commonwealth Museum, Bristol

Opening in 2002, the award - winning British Empire & Commonwealth Museum is the first major British institution to present the 500-year history and legacy of Britain's overseas empire. The British Museum has contributed a long-term loan of 41 major objects from its African, Asian, American and Oceanic collections to enhance the programme of preserving, exploring and studying Britain's cultural heritage associated with the former Empire and today's Commonwealth.

A British Museum tour, supported through the generosity of the Dorset Foundation
The oldest objects in the British Museum, and amongst the oldest artefacts in the world, are stone chopping tools from Olduvai Gorge, Tanzania, reminding us that human life, technology and art are thought to have begun in Africa. Three of these remarkable pieces, accompanied by a handling collection, are now on tour within the UK after being exhibited to great acclaim at the British Museum in 2005 and at the opening of the new Museum of African Diaspora in San Francisco in early 2006.

Rembrandt as Printmaker

The 400th anniversary of the birth of Rembrandt (1606-1669) is the occasion to celebrate the work of one of the greatest artists of all time . Rembrandt was the first modern etcher, producing more than 300 prints over a period of 40 years. A selection of 60 prints  from the outstanding collection at the BM has been made for this new Hayward Gallery Touring exhibition to highlight the whole range of his work, including self-portraits, biblical scenes, landscapes and character studies.

In partnership with Hayward Touring.

The Emperor's Terrapin
A British Museum tour, supported through the generosity of the Dorset Foundation

'Turtles are marvellous sculptural pieces and as such clearly inspired the Mughal artist working with a spectacular jade boulder. It is exciting that this wonderful object that has been in London for two centuries, should be shown at last more widely around the country' (Sir David Attenborough). The terrapin of c.1600 was found in the grounds of the fort at Allahabad in northern India in 1803 and is associated with Crown Prince Selim, later the Emperor Jahangir, son of the great Mughal emperor, Akbar. It is visiting seven places in the UK, its first excursion in this country since it came to the BM in 1830.

Art at the Rockface
Millennium Galleries, Sheffield
Until 7 January 2007

This exhibition conceived by Norwich Castle Museum and funded through the Esmée Fairbairn Regional Museums Initiative, examines the principal ways by which mankind has explored its relationship with the geology of the land, focusing on artistic expression from the earliest times. The BM is contributing material from almost every part of its collection - from Easter Island to medieval Europe.

Ringlemere Cup
Dover Museum
16 October   26 February 2007

Acquired under the Treasure Act, this rare Bronze Age cup, c1700-1500 BC was purchased by the British Museum in 2003 with assistance from the Heritage Lottery Fund, the National Art Collections Fund and the British Museum Friends. It was found at Ringlemere in East Kent in November 2001, only the second example of its type to come from Britain.
