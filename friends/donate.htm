Help purchase a new Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM)

Over the last 20 years scientific analysis at the British Museum has relied heavily on one
fundamental piece of equipment: a Scanning Electron Microscope, or SEM for short. It is an essential piece of equipment in enabling scientific research. Unfortunately, the current instrument is well beyond its life expectancy and overdue for replacement. A modern, advanced SEM would help us to make great leaps forward, opening a wide array of new scientific possibilities for our Conservation and Science Department.

Please help us to purchase a new Scanning Electron Microscope, by donating online now.
Every penny will count in acquiring this vital piece of equipment.

 

I would like to support the Museum's important research work,

In 2005 the Museum purchased the Sedgeford torc terminal which, through work conducted using our SEM, we discovered to be the missing terminal ring from an Iron Age torc already in the collection. Found at a site close to the original by an amateur archaeologist, SEM examination work of the terminal and its broken wires allowed detailed records of the construction, workmanship, composition and condition of the terminal to be compared to the original.
