The British Museum has always been reliant upon the help of its supporters to maintain its existing collections and provide funds for new acquisitions.

Membership subscriptions help to fund a wide variety of activity, including acquisitions and research programmes throughout the Museum. The constant need to deepen our knowledge and promote understanding of the collections drives an active programme of research, including collaboration with curators and institutions worldwide.Acquisitions both complement existing collections and uniquely grow our holdings of contemporary objects, held for the benefit of future generations.

Recent British Museum acquisitions that have been supported by memberships include the ‘Queen of the Night’ relief, the Coenwulf coin, the Mycenaean terracotta group of three dancers in a ring, and the Warren Cup.
