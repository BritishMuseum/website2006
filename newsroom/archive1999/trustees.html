Appointment of New Trustees

The Prime Minister has appointed Hasan Askari, Christopher McCall QC, Eric Salama, David Lindsell, The Countess of Dalkeith and Dr Anna Ritchie to the Board of the British Museum for a term of five years.

Hasan Askari is Managing Director of Barclays De Zoete Wedd Ltd. He is a Fellow of the Royal Asiatic Society and a Member of the Royal Society for Asian Affairs. He brings to the Museum strong overseas connections and proven fund-raising talents as well as particular interests in the art and artefacts of South Asia and the Far East.

Christopher McCall is a senior Chancery Barrister specialising in private trusts, charities and taxation. His expertise will be especially valuable to the British Museum. Mr McCall has particular interests in Egyptian art and architecture and design.

Eric Salama is the main board director of WPP Group plc, one of the largest marketing services groups in the world, and a former Managing Director of the Henley Centre. His extensive expertise in marketing and retailing and his strong commitment to making museums better experiences for people will be invaluable.

David Lindsell is a Senior Audit Partner of Ernst & Young and is a member of the Auditing Practices Board.. His experience in corporate governance and organisational change will enable him to play a central role in re-shaping the British Museum to meet the challenges of the 21st century. Mr Lindsell has a keen interest in the arts.

The Countess of Dalkeith is a producer of talks and documentaries for BBC radio. She is a former Trustee of the National Museums of Scotland and a former Chairman of the Scottish Ballet. She brings to the British Museum considerable experience of successful fund raising.

Dr Anna Ritchie is a distinguished archaeologist. She is a Trustee of the National Museums of Scotland, a Trustee of the Ancient Monuments Board for Scotland and member of the Heritage Lottery Fund's expert panel for Historic Buildings and Land. She brings to the British Museum extensive expertise both in the field of archaeology and in corporate governance.

October 1999 32/99
