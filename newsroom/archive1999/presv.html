The Presveis Project - An Exhibition on the Internet
www.culture.gr/nm/Presveis

The Presveis Project: A Currency for Europe has been launched on the Internet. This 'cyber exhibition' is designed to inform the general public of the many and varied occasions in antiquity, and during the medieval period, when different states in the region of modern Europe shared common currencies or entered into monetary agreements. Its aim is to provide an historical background to the current attempt by the European Union to create a single European currency.

The PRESVEIS Project (the name is Greek for 'ambassador') is a cooperative venture between the Athens Numismatic Museum and the British Museum and the exhibition is composed exclusively of coins from the two museums. The project's co-sponsors are The European Commission (Raphael Programme 1996-8) and the Hellenic Ministry of Culture. The exhibition is hosted on the website of the latter.

Among the exhibits are 'panels' dealing with the design of the new European currency, the invention of coinage, the international currencies of Athens and other early Greek states, the coinages of Philip II and Alexander the Great, the spread of Roman coinage, the coinage of the Byzantine empire, and modern international currencies such as the Maria Theresa thaler, the French franc, the British Sovereign and the American dollar.
