Bukhara and Beyond
Artefacts of Uzbekistan and Photographs by Edgar Knobloch
10 September 1999 - 9 January 2000
Room 33b
Admission free

This exhibition of photographs and artefacts of Uzbekistan concentrates on the Islamic history of the region from the 8th to the 19th century. Included in the exhibition are over forty photographs of major monuments and archaeological sites in Uzbekistan taken by Edgar Knobloch, author of Beyond the Oxus, a seminal English-language survey of Central Asian art and architecture. Until the 1990s some of the sites in Knobloch's photographs, such as Tirmiz, were all but inaccessible to tourists and since the independence of the former Soviet Central Asian republics the appearance of many buildings in Samarkand and Bukhara has changed as a result of restoration. Thus, these photographs, loaned to the British Museum by Edgar Knobloch, provide a crucial record of the historical monuments of Central Asia before recent restoration. In addition, Edgar Knobloch has revisited Central Asia in recent years, taking new photographs, which are also on view. These will appear in a new, revised edition of Beyond the Oxus, which will be published in 2000.

The artefacts from the British Museum's permanent collection include a striking jade cup inscribed with the name of Ulugh Beg, who served as the Timurid governor of Samarkand for many years prior to being crowned emperor in 1447. Tiles from 14th century buildings in Samarkand and an 7th-century silver bowl with a Khwarazmian inscription are included in the exhibition. Several 16th-century Bukhara school paintings on show reveal a style distinct from Persian painting of the same period and some of these paintings demonstrate the influence of the Bukhara school on Mughal painting of the 16th century.

One case contains Knobloch's photographs from Merv in neighbouring Turkmenistan and a selection of material excavated there by a joint Turkmen-British team which includes archaeologists from the Institute of Archaeology and the British Museum.
