Life and Ceremony in Urban Algeria
Sponsored by BP Amoco as part of BP Ethnography Showcase
Until 30 January 2000
Room 35
Admission free

This loan exhibition presents, for the first time at the British Museum, superb examples of textiles, costumes, jewellery, domestic and ceremonial equipment from urban centres in Algeria. The display is the result of a unique collaboration between the Museum of Art and Popular Tradition in Algiers, the National Museum of Ahmed Zabana in Oran and the British Museum and has been made possible through the generous support of BP Amoco.

The display focusses on three key celebrations in Algerian life: birth, circumcision and marriage, which are marked by the production, purchase or exchange of specific ceremonial and domestic items. Many of these objects illustrate the impact of numerous foreign invasions and occupations which have introduced a wealth of Andalusian, Ottoman, Jewish and European styles, techniques and materials to Algerian towns and cities. The abundant use of precious metals and luxury fabrics such as silk, velvet and brocade in this display also represents one of the most immediate visual contrasts between town and country.

Domestic objects such as brass and copper vessels, painted wooden chests, shelves and cradles, as well as silver jewellery and personal accessories, demonstrate the technical expertise and creative skills of professional specialist male artisans. Their acquisition and use in ceremonies establishes status, wealth and prestige within the community. In contrast, the array of finely embroidered furnishings and textiles were produced in the home by well-to-do girls and women for use in family celebrations. These practical skills, which were passed from mother to daughter, play a significant role in strengthening kinship ties and obligations during life-cycle ceremonies. Today, fine examples of these crafts continue to hold a prominent place in Algerian ceremonial and daily life.

BP Amoco, in a significant initiative, is supporting the work of the Ethnography Department through BP Ethnography Showcase. The programme, which includes exhibitions, acquisitions, lectures, publications and fieldwork, will enable visitors to gain a greater insight into the history and development of some of the world's most fascinating cultural traditions.
