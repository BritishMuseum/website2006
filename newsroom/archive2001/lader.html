Appointment of New Trustee: The Hon. Philip Lader

The Board of the British Museum has appointed the Hon. Philip Lader, former U.S. Ambassador to the Court of St. James's, as a Trustee. Lader succeeds Sir David Attenborough and will serve for a term of five years commencing 1 June.

Lader is Chairman of WPP Group, the world's largest advertising and communications services company, and a Senior Advisor to Morgan Stanley International. He is also a director of RAND Corporation, the international public policy institute.

A businessman, lawyer and educator before entering government service, Lader served in President Clinton's Cabinet as Administrator of the U.S. Small Business Administration and previously was White House Deputy Chief of Staff and Deputy Director of the President's Office of Management and Budget.

He had been Executive Vice President of the late Sir James Goldsmith's U.S. holdings and President of Sea Pines Company, the award-winning developer/operator of large-scale American recreation communities, including Hilton Head Island and Kiawah Island.

Formerly the president of universities in South Carolina and Australia, Lader is an Honorary Fellow of Pembroke College, Oxford, where he studied law, and London Business School. He has been awarded honorary doctorates from 12 British and American universities.
