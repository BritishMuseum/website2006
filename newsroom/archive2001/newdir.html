New Directions at The British Museum

The British Museum will advertise for a new Director on Sunday 9 September as the Trustees seek a dynamic figure to lead the Museum and to build on its achievements and international reputation in the approach to the 250th anniversary in 2003. The new Director will take unitary responsibility for both academic and managerial matters.

The current Director, Dr Robert Anderson's contract expires in June 2002 and the Trustees have accepted the resignation of Managing Director Suzanna Taverne with six months notice.

Consultants Saxton Bampfylde Hever have been charged with undertaking an international search to ensure that a wide range of possible candidates is considered.

The election of Sir John Boyd, as chairman designate of the Museum's Board of Trustees was brought forward so he could chair the search committee prior to taking over from Graham Greene as Chairman on 1 July 2002.

"The Trustees are seeking a Director who combines cultural authority and managerial capability," said Sir John. "We want strong and active leadership together with strategic vision. Applications from a variety of relevant backgrounds are being encouraged.

"The British Museum has a broad mandate and multiple responsibilities to the public as a visitor experience and a centre for education and research. The new Director will build on the success of the Great Court to develop our role as a major international institution as we look towards and beyond the 250th anniversary in 2003."

Suzanna Taverne said, "My resignation clears the way for the new appointment and I shall be working out my notice with full commitment.

"I have thoroughly enjoyed working at this great institution, managing the Museum through a challenging period and playing my part in the opening of the Great Court, which has received wide public acclaim," she added.

"Suzanna Taverne has played a critical role in ensuring effective management in the final phase of our millennium project," said Graham Greene. "The dual management was necessary during this period leading up to the opening of the Great Court: its success is a tribute to all concerned."

Note to Editors

1. Advertisements will run in The Sunday Times and The Economist.
2. Closing date for applications will be 3 October. An appointment will be made through interview process and it is anticipated that the new Director will start by 1 July 2002.
3. The salary is expected to be in the region of £120,000 per annum with on-site accommodation. 4. The members of the Trustees search committee are: Sir John Boyd (Chairman), Graham Greene (current Chairman of the Board of Trustees), Dame Gillian Beer, Lord Browne of Madingley, Dr Jennifer Montagu, Alex Stewart (Department of Culture, Media and Sport) and John Tusa.

September 2001
