The Big Draw at The British Museum

The First Big Draw at The British Museum, Saturday 29 September, 10.00 - 17.00
This is a trailblazer for the Big Draw, a UK wide initiative aimed at getting everyone in the country to draw. A programme of events, organised jointly by the Campaign for Drawing and the British Museum, will take place in many of the Museum's galleries and the Great Court. Well known artists, cartoonists, illustrators, architects and scientists such as Quentin Blake, Gerald Scarfe and Posy Simmonds, Will Alsop and Sir Roger Penrose will be among those leading by example. Visitors can try drawing from life, architecture or sculpture, or explore the various aspects of archaeological illustration, printmaking and digitalised imagery. This is an open invitation for all visitors to explore visual expression - regardless of age, ability or previous experience.

The day's events begin at 10.00 when Quentin Blake's 'dream team' will create a surprising banner, revealing some unusual views of the Great Court and The British Museum. This will be unfurled, with a trumpet fanfare, at 12.30. Easels and drawing materials will be available throughout the Great Court and the Museum's galleries, with an army of volunteers on hand to offer encouragement. Participants can then take their artwork down to the Drawing Lab and turn their sketches into mono-prints and posters. Champion of the Pre-Raphaelites, John Ruskin (played by James Heard), will give a practical lesson based on his famous 'Elements of Drawing'. At a lunchtime lecture at 13.15, Quentin Blake, Peter Brookes and Linda Kitson will explain what drawing means to them. At 16.15 a colloquium of distinguished architects, including Sir Richard MacCormac and Edward Cullinam will draw 'on their imagination'. During the afternoon there will be a visit to the Prints and Drawings Students Room, to view work by famous 'drawers' from the past such as Matisse and Picasso. A complete list of artists involved, and events for the day, is available on request.

The Big Draw at The British Museum, Saturday 20 October, 10.00 - 17.00
This is the Campaign for Drawing's national focus, when groups and individuals in hundreds of museums, galleries, schools, hospitals and libraries come together to draw. The British Museum will again be offering free events and activities to encourage drawing in the Great Court and many galleries. A special lecture by Gerald Scarfe and a series of guided talks will highlight drawing across different cultures - from Africa and North America to Europe and Asia. It is hoped that every British Museum visitor will leave with a drawing they made in some medium - be it pencil, crayon or electronic imagery.

For further information on either of the Big Draw events or pictures please call Rebecca Chetley on 020 7323 8583 or email rchetley@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk or visit www.drawingpower.org.uk

Drawing Events at The British Museum

Art & Architecture - Open Evening
Tuesday 4 September, 18.00 - 21.00
West Door, Great Court
Admission free for BM Friends, otherwise £5

David Gaimster from The British Museum will open the evening with a lecture on the Renaissance architecture of London to be followed by a series of gallery talks focussing on the art and architecture of London. Gallery talks will feature the Prehistoric and Early European galleries, the Egyptian Sculpture Gallery and the Medieval and Modern European Galleries. Medieval music will provide a backdrop for the evening.

Art in Focus: Neoclassicism and Modernism
Thursday 13 September, 14.30 - 16.30
Clore Education Centre
£10, £8 BM Friends, concessions £6
25 places

A seminar which starts the ' Arts in Focus ' series with a chance to look in depth at a particular work of art from the Museum's collections while studying the context in which it was made. Two drawings will be studied, Jacques-Louis David's Study for the Head of Brutus and Picasso's Study for Les Demoiselles d'Avignon. Both are featured in the special exhibition Paper Assets: Collecting Prints and Drawings 1996 - 2001. Led by Hilary Williams, the series runs until May 2002.

Drawing in Galleries
Wednesday 19 September, 10.30 - 13.00, repeated 14.00 - 16.30
Hotung Gallery, room 35
£10, £8 BM Friends, concessions £6
12 places

Unleash your creativity and develop your drawing techniques by observing and drawing objects from the Museum's inspiring Chinese collections. There will also be the opportunity to experiment with different materials and media under the supervision of Rebecca Jewel. All materials are included and all levels of participant are welcome including beginners.

Theory and Practice of Drawing
10 Monday mornings, 11.00 - 13.00 starting 24 September
Clore Education Centre
£36 (concessions £30)
15 places

This course is held jointly with the Workers Education Association. There will be an opportunity to learn about the drawing techniques of the great masters and put knowledge into practice by drawing the variety of artefacts on show in The British Museum. The tutor for this course is Helen Lewis. For further information and to book, please call Liz Carlin at the WEA on 020 7613 7550 or come along to the first class.

Scarfe on Scarfe - Gerald Scarfe
Saturday 20 October, 13.15
Clore Education Centre
Admission free

"Scarfe on Scarfe" brings caricaturist Gerald Scarfe to The British Museum to talk about aspects of his life and work. Many public figures have come under the Scarfe scrutiny, notably Margaret Thatcher and Edward Heath through his characteristic assassination of being "Drawn and Quartered".
