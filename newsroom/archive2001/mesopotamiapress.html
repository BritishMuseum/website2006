Mesopotamia Press Release

Online Learning
Wednesday 28 March 2001, 10.15am
Great Court

Discover a culture as ancient as Egypt
ziggurats as old as pyramids
royal death pits richer than mummies
Tiamat and Marduk, more terrible than Isis and Osiris

The British Museum is launching Mesopotamia, the second educational website in its Ancient Civilizations series.

Building on the success of Ancient Egypt (www.ancientegypt.co.uk), currently running at over half-a-million page hits per week, Mesopotamia presents information about life, culture, beliefs and practices in Mesopotamia: Sumer, Babylonia and Assyria, using animations, 3D models and objects from The British Museum's collections.

The website supports the National Curriculum for upper Key Stage 2 schoolchildren and their teachers. Each topic is presented in three ways: a narrative ‘Story', a self-guided ‘Explore' and an animated ‘Challenge'. The site also contains a substantial Staff Room area, where teachers can find a range of background information, discussion topics, printable worksheets and follow-up classroom activities.

The Ancient Civilizations series is generously sponsored by Nippon Telegraph and Telephone East Corporation (NTT East), Japan. NTT East will be translating the website into Japanese and making it available to Japanese schools.

Come along to the press and VIP launch and take part in an exciting series of events, together with children from a local Camden school, Museum curators and educators and an epic story-teller:

  • the Royal Game of Ur, one of the oldest known board games in the world, blown up to 30 times its normal size, with teams of children racing for the finish
  • re-telling of the Epic of Gilgamesh with epic story-teller Ben Haggerty
  • see the classroom of the future, with a website presentation in The British Museum's new ICT Room in the Clore Education Centre using the latest in interactive whiteboard technology

Press and VIP launch Wednesday 28 March 2001, 10.15 am, The Great Court.
