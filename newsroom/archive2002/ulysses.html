Imaging Ulysses: Richard Hamilton's Illustrations to James Joyce
2 February - 19 May 2002
Room 90
Admission free

Richard Hamilton, one of Britain's most innovative and original artists who first came to international attention as the founding father of British Pop Art in the late 1950s, has been preoccupied for more than fifty years with James Joyce's modernist epic Ulysses, first published in 1922, the year of Hamilton's birth. Joyce's revolutionary novel provoked a storm of controversy upon its publication in Paris and was banned in the English-speaking world until 1933 on account of its sexual frankness, scatological themes and vulgar language.

The idea of illustrating this complex, experimental novel occurred to Hamilton while doing his National Service in 1947. As a student at the Slade he made numerous preliminary drawings and studies with the view to producing etched illustrations to Joyce's text but for technical and practical reasons the project was put aside in 1950. After a break of more than thirty years Hamilton resumed illustrating Joyce in a series of large-scale independent etchings during the 1980s. Some of these are reworkings of the earlier studies, others are completely new treatments. In the 1990s, with the computer as an increasingly important tool in Hamilton's image-making, the Iris digital print has recently joined the sequence of illustrations. Evolving technically and intellectually over a lifetime Hamilton's images represent an odyssey through the themes and ideas of his own career as well as those of Joyce.

This exhibition assembles all the studies and prints produced since the project began in 1948, most of which come from the artist. The exhibition, commissioned by the British Council, is part of a European tour which includes Ljubljana (Cankarjev Dom Galerija, 10 June-16 September 2001), Tübingen (Tübingen Kunsthalle, 10 November 2001-13 January 2002) and Dublin (Irish Museum of Modern Art, 1 June -1 August 2002). For The British Museum venue, there will be a special display of first edition Joyce books and ephemera from a private collection.

The exhibition opens at The British Museum on 2 February 2002, the 80th anniversary of the publication of Ulysses. The BM venue coincides with Richard Hamilton's 80th birthday on 24 February 2002; it is the first solo exhibition given to a living artist at the British Museum since 1974 when Henry Moore's illustrations to Auden's poems were exhibited. A fully illustrated catalogue written by Stephen Coppel, the curator of the exhibition, and designed by Richard Hamilton has been published by the British Council and will be available at the exhibition.
