Governance Review

At their regular meeting of 28 September, the Board of Trustees of The British Museum agreed far-reaching reforms to the Museum’s system of governance.

The changes are designed to ensure a better alignment between the respective responsibilities of the Trustees and the Director and enable the Trustees to focus fully on their broad strategic and supervisory role. They will also enable decisions to be taken more rapidly and effectively when necessary.

The Board, which has since June been chaired by Sir John Boyd, has 25 Trustees including 15 appointed by the Prime Minister, 4 representing learned bodies and appointed by the Department for Culture, Media and Sport and one appointed by the Crown. The Board will in future have three committees – Executive, Audit and Nominations – and a number of panels to advise the Director on key issues facing the Museum. The Governance Review was chaired by Lord Browne of Madingley, Group Chief Executive of BP.

The changes were agreed at the first meeting since Neil MacGregor, the new Director, and Dawn Austwick, Director of Resources, joined the Museum.

“It is essential that the Museum has an effective governance structure for the new era,” said Sir John Boyd. “These changes will ensure that the Board can provide the policy framework and strategic guidance necessary to support the Director and his management team.”
