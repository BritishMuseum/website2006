Appointment of new Trustee: Lord Powell of Bayswater KCMG

The Trustees of The British Museum have appointed Lord Powell of Bayswater KCMG as a Trustee, in succession to Lord Moser.

Charles Powell was born in 1941 and educated at King's School, Canterbury, and New College, Oxford, where he obtained a first class honours degree in History.

Lord Powell was for many years Private Secretary and adviser on foreign affairs and defence to Lady Thatcher when she was Prime Minister, and held the same position in the early part of John Major's time as Prime Minister.

Since 1992 he has been an international businessman, serving as a Board member of several major international companies. He is currently Chairman of Sagitta Asset Management Limited, Chairman of Phillips Fine Art Auctioneers and Chairman of LVMH, Louis Vuitton Moet-Hennessy in the UK.

He is a Board member of, among others, the Textron Corporation, Caterpillar Inc., LVMH and Mandarin Oriental Hotel Group. He also holds a number of other positions, in particular President of the China-Britain Business Council, Chairman of the Singapore-British Business Council and Chairman of the Trustees of the Oxford Business School.
