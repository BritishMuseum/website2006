History comes to life with children's COMPASS
Made possible by Ford Motor Company Fund
www.thebritishmuseum.ac.uk/childrenscompass

Following the success of The British Museum's collections website COMPASS, a parallel site has been developed specifically for children, introduced by Alfred the British Museum lion. children's COMPASS has been developed by curators, teachers and education officers and is aimed at 7-11 year olds and their teachers. The website has been fully evaluated, with input coming from over 100 children and staff from local Camden primary schools.

children's COMPASS contains information on over 800 key objects in the Museum's collections, linked to both printable and online activities. Children will be able to solve the hieroglyphs' spell and open the mummy's tomb, try to piece together the Sutton Hoo helmet, or design their own mythical creature. There are also online Tours on subjects of general interest such as Animal Mummies, others based on the National Curriculum, and some written by the children themselves.

Children are also encouraged to contribute to the site in other ways. There is a monthly competition, with the prize winners' work displayed on the Home page, and Noticeboards show off children's artwork and writing. With the 'Ask the Expert' facility, children can email their questions to curators, and a selection of answers are displayed each month.

Some Tours are specially written for younger children, others for families. For teachers there are adaptable classroom resources with teaching suggestions explaining how to alter them for children with special needs, including extension activities. Like the main site, children's COMPASS has been written with accessibility in mind, with a text only version of the site in production for those using screen readers.

Some of the main features of the site:

  • Tours, such as Animals, Toys and Games and Anglo-Saxons
  • Themed Search with topics like 'Death', 'Warfare' and 'Daily Life'
  • Online games and 'print and do' activities
  • A monthly competition
  • Noticeboards displaying children's artwork, stories and comments
  • 'Ask the expert' where children can email questions to curators
  • Feedback forms for children and teachers

Additional features specifically for teachers and parents:

  • A curriculum search facility, enabling the quick and easy location of resources
  • Links to further information, including websites and bibliographies
  • Tours written especially to cover aspects of the National Curriculum, to include Art, Literacy and Numeracy.
  • Curriculum based activities, including those developing the 'knowledge, skills and understanding' Programmes of Study of the National Curriculum for History.

  • Ford Motor Company
    Ford's support of children's COMPASS is part of a larger initiative by the company to forge long-term partnerships with leading cultural institutions, such as The British Museum. Ford's longstanding commitment to supporting innovative educational initiative has resulted in the development of numerous programs and facilities at cultural institutions throughout the world. In addition to children's COMPASS and the Ford Centre for Young Visitors at The British Museum, Ford has sponsored Ford Free Days at museums across the United States and the Ford Learning Center at the Nelson-Atkins Museum in Kansas City. Ford has also partnered with the Henry Ford Museum & Greenfield Village to establish the Henry Ford Academy. The academy serves a diverse student body that learns and studies in a setting dedicated to invention and innovation. Other programs supported by Ford, such as the African American Reference Collection Project in New Orleans ensure that all aspects of American culture are preserved and accessible to future generations. Additionally, Ford's sponsorship of nationally touring exhibitions always features educational components that draw together the museum and the classroom.
