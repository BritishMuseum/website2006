The British Museum Faces Hard Choices

The British Museum is developing a package of measures to reduce an impending £5 million deficit in the face of the fall in tourism to London. Museum senior staff are modelling projected cuts of at least 15 per cent to ensure that the finances of one of the world's largest and most important cultural institutions are placed on a firm footing for the future.

The Museum currently has a revenue budget of some £45 million. £36 million comes from government grant, a figure that has not increased for a number of years; other income includes sales and sponsorships. There are just over 1000 staff and the salary bill is some £29 million.

"We've been hit hard", underlined Christopher Jones, the Museum's Accounting officer. "It is not just the British Museum's problem; museums and galleries both in the UK and around the world are finding their financial position difficult, with tourism sharply down.

We obviously need to ensure that we live within our means. The present situation calls for adjustment to provide for long-term sustainability. At this stage no cuts can be ruled out including job losses. We're looking at some painful choices including reducing the number of galleries open and reducing the number of exhibitions we mount. We will, however, be making absolutely sure that all our core values are maintained. We also want the Museum to be in the best possible shape as we enter our 250th anniversary year in 2003."

Museum staff were told at open meetings that plans were being developed for approval by the Board of Trustees by the end of April.

Notes to Editors

The British Museum, which holds some 8m artefacts in trust for the nation, received 4.6m visitors last year, 45% of whom are from the UK and 17% from the USA.

The British Museum has doubled income per head since the opening of the Great Court through increased retail, catering and other services.

Opening hours of the Great Court were reduced with the agreement of the Millennium Commission on 2 January 2002 to reflect the pattern of evening visitors. The Great Court now opens everyday at 9am and closes at 6pm on Sunday-Wednesday and 11pm on Thursday-Saturday.


Mr Christopher Jones was appointed Accounting Officer by the DCMS upon the departure of Managing Director, Suzanna Taverne, on 31 December 2001. Mr Neil MacGregor, currently Director of the National Gallery, has been appointed as Director and will take up his post at 1 August 2002.
