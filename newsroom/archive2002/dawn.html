The British Museum Appoints New Director of Resources

Dawn Austwick OBE, formerly project director for Tate Modern, is to join The British Museum in September as Director of Resources.

The post forms part of a new management structure supporting the incoming Director, Neil MacGregor, who takes up post on 1 August. The Director of Resources takes responsibility for support functions incorporating finance, information systems, human resources, building management and operations with an annual budget of £25 million.

Dawn Austwick (41), who has an MBA from London Business School and a BA Honours degree in English and Drama from the University of London, began her career with the Association for Business Sponsorship of the Arts. After two management roles at the Half Moon Theatre, she worked for 8 years as a principal management consultant leading the Leisure and Tourism Group for KPMG.

Dawn became Project Director for Tate Modern in 1995, assuming the role of Change Director in 1998. Since the opening of Tate Modern in 2000, she has advised a range of organisations on governance issues and led the quinquennial reviews of both English Heritage and the National Gallery/National Portrait Gallery for the Department of Culture, Media and Sport.

Dawn is also Vice Chair of the Management Council of The Women's Library, a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts, a director of London International Sport, a member of the Arts and Business London Advisory Council and a trustee of both the Woodland Trust and the Stoke Newington Festival.

April 2002/14
