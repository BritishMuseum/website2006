Scultures from the Parthenon

“The British Museum is the best possible place for the sculptures from the Parthenon in its collections to be on display”, says Neil MacGregor, Director.

Earlier today the Chairman of Trustees, Sir John Boyd, and Mr Neil MacGregor, received Mr Venizelos, Greek Minister for Culture, in the Director's Residence at the British Museum for a meeting concerning the sculptures from the Parthenon within the Museum's collections.

After cordial discussions, Sir John and Mr MacGregor affirmed the view of the Museum's Trustees that the Parthenon sculptures are among a select group of key objects which are indispensable to the Museum's core function, which is to tell the story of human cultural evolution and civilization; and that, as such, the sculptures cannot be lent to any museum, in Greece or elsewhere.

The British Museum, they said, is the best possible place for the Parthenon sculptures to be on display to 5 million visitors a year, entirely free of entry charge; here the worldwide significance of the sculptures can be fully appreciated, where the visitor can move under one roof from Egypt to the Aztecs, from ancient Britain to Africa, from ancient Greece to Japan.

They also discussed two recent requests for generous loans of British Museum objects to exhibitions in Athens being organised in association with the 2004 Athens Olympics, and looked forward to many such collaborative ventures in the future, and to a continued strengthening of the existing close relations between The British Museum and museums in Greece.
