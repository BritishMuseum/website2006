30 June – 11 September

Kabuki Heroes celebrates the devotion surrounding Japanese urban theatre between 1780 and 1830, the intense rivalries among actors, poets and artists and the colourful memorabilia, from scrapbooks to songbooks, they produced. Bringing together some 300 prints, books and paintings from Britain, Europe, USA and Japan, this is a vivid reminder that the drama of pop culture runs through the centuries and across the globe.

£5, concessions £3 
 Booking Information

The British Museum and the BBC invite you to
7 action-packed hours of African music, dance, storytelling, talks and workshops.

  • Meet the stars of Ground Force as you wander round the Africa Garden
  • Enjoy the best in live African music on the forecourt stage and around the Museum
  • Listen to special performances by BBC celebrities in the famous Round Reading Room
  • Join in the construction of a fantastic African inspired sculpture made from recycled materials in the Great Court

Africa Live will mark the lead-up to BBC One’s week of programming about Africa in the first week of July and forms an important part of the British Museum’s contribution to the Africa 05 cultural season.

7 July – 25 September

Introducing the work of one of Canada’s most acclaimed and original painters.

David Milne Watercolours: “Painting toward the Light”  traces a visionary journey through the bustle of New York City, the scarred terrain of war-torn Europe, and upstate New York and Ontario, where Milne discovered a simplicity that informed his finest compositions. Featuring more than 80 watercolours and colour drypoints, including Dreamland Tower, Coney Island (1912) and The Boarding House, Boston Corners (1916).
 
Admission Free 
  

Organised by the Art Gallery of Ontario with the assistance of the Canadian High Commission, London

Thursday 30 June, 18.30

MASAI DOUBLE BILL

Masai Women
The Masai are cattle herders living in the East African Rift Valley. Men have exclusive control over rights to cattle and women are dependent throughout their lives on father, husband or son for rights to access to property. This television film, part of the Disappearing World series, uses extensive interviews to detail key moments in the women’s lives.
Director: Chris Curling 1974, 53 mins

Masai Manhood
A vivid view of the lives of Masai men, especially of the moran (warriors) and the dramatic eunoto ceremony which marks their transition from warriorhood to the responsibilities of elderhood. A fascinating insight into rituals and ceremonies which have now disappeared.
Director: Chris Curling 1975, 53 mins
