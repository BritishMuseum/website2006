WELCOME

September at the British Museum sees the beginning of the new exhibition, Power & Taboo: Sacred objects from the Pacific , in Room 5. Immerse yourself in the Museum's unique collection from the eastern Pacific through this special exhibition exploring the power of the gods. Discover famous objects from this region that had a lasting influence on 20th century artists such as Henry Moore and Pablo Picasso, and find out more about the vibrant and contemporary art scene of the region, inspired by continuing tradition.

This month also sees the start of two of the exhibitions in the Voices of Bengal season, Myths of Bengal in Room 91 and The art of peace: Paintings by the poet Tagore in Room 3. These exhibitions are also complemented by a series of events and films which highlight the rich cultural heritage of Bengal .

A Durga sculpture in Calcutta.
Photo: John Williams

12 August – 27 September 2006

The Great Court will be the venue for the creation of a spectacular image of the goddess Durga, fashioned onsite by craftsmen from West Bengal, using straw, clay and paint.

This is a rare chance to witness the incredible skills of the Bengali craftsmen, before the image transfers to the Camden Centre for worship, ending with its immersion in the Thames.

Great Court
Admission free

 Myths of Bengal
14 September 2006 – 7 January 2007

Explore Bengal's rich oral tradition in this exhibition which focuses on the region's diverse myths and stories, through the British Museum's superb collection of Bengali artefacts, including beautifully painted scrolls.

Room 91
Admission free

The art of peace:
Paintings by the poet Tagore
14 September – 12 November 2006
This is the first chance to see a selection of rare paintings by Rabindranath Tagore, that have never before been exhibited. An insightful introduction to the legendary Bengali poet and polymath.

Room 3
Admission free

 


The Royal Game of Ur

Play the Oldest Board Game
in the World: The Royal Game of Ur
Until 3 September 2006

The Royal Game of Ur is over 4,500 years old and is the oldest surviving board game in the world. This exhibition shows you how to play and gives you a chance to try out your skills on replicas of the ancient game.

Room 3
Admission free

Middle East Now family events
1, 3, 8, 10, 15, 17, 22, 24 August, 11.00–16.00

Enjoy a feast of Middle Eastern entertainment. Pit your wits against the opposition by playing the Royal Game of Ur and backgammon, or be enchanted by gripping tales of heroes, genies, riddles and kings. On the same dates there will also be storytelling sessions for adults.

Rembrandt: A 400th anniversary display
 Until 3 September 2006

Rembrandt van Rijn (1606–1669) was born in the summer of 1606 – exactly 400 years ago. The British Museum has one of the finest collections of Rembrandt's prints and drawings in the world, and this 400th anniversary display reflects the artist's interest in people of other cultures, including those from the Middle East, to tie in with the Museum's current Middle East Now season.

Room 90
Admission free
