WELCOME

This July at the British Museum we have a fantastic selection of events and exhibitions to enhance your summer. Immerse yourself in the rich culture of the Middle East, and experience the diverse range of displays and events surrounding the exciting reopening of the Prints & Drawings
exhibition gallery.

Highlights of the Middle East Now programme include dance demonstrations and workshops from Egyptian, Arabic and Sufi traditions,
in-depth explorations of contemporary art from the Arab world, and a selection of powerful films from the region.

French Drawings: Clouet to Seurat
Part I: 1500–1700 Clouet to La Fage
Until 1 October 2006
 The exhibition displays nearly 100 highlights, representing four centuries of French master drawings, from the renowned holdings of the British Museum. There are masterpieces from the Renaissance to Post-Impressionism, including works by Jean and François Clouet, Claude Lorrain, Antoine Watteau, Eugène Delacroix, Edgar Degas and Georges Seurat. The exhibition is divided into two parts.
Part 2 covers the years 1700–1900,
from Watteau to Seurat and opens on
3 October 2006.

Avigdor Arikha from life:
Drawings and Prints 1965–2005
Until 7 January 2007
An exhibition honouring Avigdor Arikha and his gift to the British Museum of 100 works on paper. The exhibition explores the artist's movement away from abstract work to drawing from life.

Rembrandt (1606–1669):
