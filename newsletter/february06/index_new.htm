The Bible as History?
A view from the exile
Thursday 9 February, 18.30

Jonathan Tubb, British Museum, considers the issues and examines the Biblical narratives against some of the most recent archaeological studies of the Holy Land.

Pandora's Breeches: women, science and power in the Enlightenment
Thursday 16 February, 18.30

Challenging conventional histories of the age, Patricia Fara, Cambridge University, gives voice to the friends, sisters and wives whose work informed a scientific revolution. Introduced by Silke Ackermann, British Museum.

What did the Enlightenment do for women?
Thursday 23 February, 18.30

Karen O'Brien, University of Warwick, explores 250 years of paradox and possibility. Introduced by Kim Sloan, British Museum.
