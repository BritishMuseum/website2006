Lecture: We Won't Budge
Friday 20 January, 18.30–20.00

Manthia Diawara's lyrical, layered memoir, We Won't Budge, explores the complexities of being 'an African exile in the world' based on his own experience as a Malian in Europe and America.

Here, in conversation with Museum writer-in-residence, Diran Adebayo, the NYU professor and film maker explains why 'the black man in America bears the curse of Cain'.

Admission free, booking required
Call the Box Office on +44 (0)20 7323 8181
to book tickets

The Ones We left Behind
Friday 27 January, 18.30–20.30

Full Spectrum Productions return with more original drama and discussion.

The Ones We Left Behind explores the cultural, emotional, and economic experiences of moving away from one's country of origin and leaving behind friends, family and a known way of living.

From Henan province, northern China Ming dynasty, dated AD 1486.
Room 33

Maitreya Buddha, the future Buddha, made his first appearance under the Song dynasty (960-1279), and came into his own as one of the most popular gods in East Asia.

In terms of religious porcelain, he represents a bold departure from the normal ‘delicacy’ of the craft. Elsewhere, he is often shown surrounded by children, who sometimes catch him in a net, only to set him free. He holds a sack and is always laughing.
