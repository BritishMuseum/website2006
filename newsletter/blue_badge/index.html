National Treasures: icons to mark 25 years of the National Heritage Memorial Fund
14 October – 28 November 2005

NHMF has been saving heritage for the nation for 25 years, providing grants to museums and heritage organisations and assisting the purchase of some truly extraordinary objects.

To celebrate, the British Museum is exhibiting a selection of objects in the Great Court from across the country, from clever clocks to special cars and a ‘golden glimpse’ into Roman life in Britain.

 
 


Enamelled bronze pan

The Staffordshire Moorlands Pan
Roman England, 2nd century AD

Partnership UK (the British Museum’s cross-regional approach to sharing heritage) recently initiated the purchase of this 2000-year-old masterpiece of Romano-British craftsmanship with Tullie House Museum, Carlisle, and the Potteries Museum, Stoke-on-Trent.

    

The Pan beautifully preserves one of the mostly finely decorated of ‘souvenirs’ from Hadrian’s Wall. It was probably bought by a soldier in commemoration of his service at the world-famous site and is in fact the only extant object to explicitly connect Hadrian’s personality with the wall.

It will be on display at the British Museum until 2006, it will then move to the Potteries Museum and, in 2007, to Tullie House.
