WELCOME

Be inspired by a range of
diverse temporary exhibitions
and displays at the Museum
this December. Power &
Taboo, Myths of Bengal,
Encounters: Travel and
Money in the Byzantine
World, Avigdor Arikha from
life: Prints & Drawings
1965–2005, and the second
part of the French Drawings
exhibition (exploring Watteau
to Seurat) all close on
7 January 2007, so make
sure you don't miss them!
Admission is free.

Buy your tickets now for
The Past from Above:
Through the lens of Georg
Gerster and journey over the world's greatest
archaeological sites
(Admission £5).

Public events, study
opportunities and lectures
also continue throughout
December. Highlights include
the evening lectures on
Etruscan bucchero and the
Aztec turquoise mosaics,
both great opportunities to
really engage with some of
the Museum's objects. From
Christmas Carols to
Japanese Tea Ceremony demonstrations, a visit to the
Museum is the perfect way to
usher in the festive season.
