There is something for
everyone to explore during
the Middle East Now season
at the British Museum until
3 September.

For daytime visitors, free

in more depth. The Learning programme also includes
full day events on faith and

Middle East and workshops
on calligraphy.

whole family can participate
in special events and

Game of Ur.

Book tickets now for the
Museum's Middle East
Evenings – a series of six
events led by well known
artists and experts on
subjects ranging from
literature and debate to
music, food and film.

There is also the opportunity
to relax and enjoy some
wonderful dance, music and
poetry performances by
contemporary artists and
performers or take in a film
during June and July.

'I follow the religion of love'
by Ibn Arabi (d. 1240),
illustrated by calligrapher
Hassan Massoudy

This exhibition is based largely on the
collections of the British Museum
complemented by a number of loans.
It demonstrates the imaginative ways in
which artists across the Middle East
and North Africa are using the power
of the written word in their art today.

The exhibition includes wonderful
examples of calligraphy transforming
writing into art, books of poetry and
works which reflect current issues facing the modern Middle East.

Admission free, Room 35

Daily 10.00–17.30, open late
until 20.30 on Thursdays and Fridays

Audio guide £3.50, concessions £3
