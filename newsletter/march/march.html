Illegitimate son of Christopher Columbus, advisor to Emperor Charles V and friend to Erasmus, Ferdinand assembled the earliest and by far the largest known print collection of the Renaissance. Including work by all of Europe 's leading printmakers, including Marcantonio Raimondi , Albrecht Dürer and Lucas van Leyden , this is a unique glimpse into one man's pursuit of new worlds.

Through a wonderful mix of books, paintings and paraphernalia, Kabuki Heroes reveals the passionate public devotion fuelled by Japanese urban theatre between 1780 and 1830. Examine the intense rivalries played out between actors, poets and artists. With a particular focus on the personalities themselves - an inspiration to generations of pop culture enthusiasts - Kabuki Heroes brings together works from a number of institutions in Britain and Japan.

14 March - 23 April

To commemorate the state visit of President Ciampi of Italy in March 2005, the British Museum will be exhibiting the legendary Spinario (thorn-puller), on loan from the Capitoline Museums in Rome.

This renowned 1st century BC Roman bronze is the earliest and most classicising survivor of a long tradition, inspired by Greek art, which celebrated the contrast between a humble subject and the rich form of its representation. It will be displayed alongside the British Museum 's own marble Spinario of the 1st century AD which reflects the earlier, strikingly naturalistic Hellenistic tradition.
