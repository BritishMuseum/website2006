"The Queen of Sheba heard of Solomon's fame through the name of Yahweh and she came to test him with hard riddles. She arrived in Jerusalem with a very large retinue and with camels bearing spices, a great quantity of gold, and precious stones"
Bible, 1 Kings 10:1-2

This exhibition links an eloquent legend with its probable historical context. The Queen of Sheba features in European art from medieval times: she was an exotic subject on which artists could drape their oriental fantasies. Try your hand at mask-making, listen to Arab stories or marvel at Hollywood's fascination with the Queen, to follow up this aspect of the exhibition.

The Queen's homeland, Sheba or Saba, was a powerful incense trading-state in present-day Yemen. It is first recorded in inscriptions from the 8th century BC, but may be even older. Recent archaeological discoveries are gradually shedding light on Sheba and its neighbouring states, which together formed an important civilization in pre-Islamic South Arabia. Come to one of the study days or handling sessions to find out more.

And you can make your own stop on the incense route to find out more about frankincense and myrrh at the Yemen suq in the Great Court throughout the run of the exhibition.
