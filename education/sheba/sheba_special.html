National Archaeology Days - Rome Goes East

Saturday 20 and Sunday 21 July, 10.15 to 16.30
After the success of the Saturnalia weekend in 2000, Legio II Augusta (www.legiiavg.org.uk) and The Colchester Roman Society will parade and display their wares on the Forecourt of The British Museum. To complement the exhibition Queen of Sheba, Treasures for Ancient Yemen (9 June - 13 October, 2002) there will be an Eastern flavour with many participants in Palmyrene dress. Both days have a programme running from 10.15 to 16.30.

For further information please telephone 020 7323 8511/8854 or email learning@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk

 

Dining with the Queen of Sheba

Thursday 4 July, 19.00.
Cost: £49 (£45 BM Friends)
Exhibition curator St John Simpson unravels the mystery of the legendary Queen of Sheba. Enjoy a Yemeni supper and a private view of the special exhibition Queen of Sheba: Treasures from Ancient Yemen.

Tickets available from the British Museum Box Office on 020 7323 8181

 

Discover the Queen of Sheba

Friday 26 July, 10.00.
Cost: £100 (£90 BM Friends)
A behind-the-scenes day at the British Museum, including an introductory slide lecture by exhibition curator St. John Simpson in the Ancient Near East seminar room, a guided walk through the exhibition by a member of the team that put it together, an object handling session, lunch in the Court restaurant, a drinks reception in the Arched Room and a complimentary copy of the exhibition catalogue.

Tickets available from the British Museum Box Office on 020 7323 8181
