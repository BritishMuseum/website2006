Educational Access to the Museum's collections and programmes

We try to ensure that all events are held in accessible locations. Where this is not the case, we will state in the publicity.


New British Museum E-bulletin for Deaf Visitors

We have a wide range of events organised for deaf visitors over the next couple of months. Our new e-bulletin provides information on what is on offer plus by a details of our special exhibitions, a diary and contact information.

To subscribe (free) please email learning@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk putting e-bulletin as the subject heading.


Sign Interpreted Gallery Talks

Signed Gallery talks once a month. They are a mixture of eyeOpener tours and gallery talks by Museum staff and visiting lecturers. We also arrange Sign Interpretation for special events.

If a group has a particular interest in an area of the collection a private talk can be arranged. This must be booked through the Learning and Information Department at least six weeks in advance. We regret we cannot guarantee to be able to provide an interpreter.

We can also arrange for Sign Interpretation for Study Days. This must be requested at the time of booking and to guarantee an interpreter should be booked at least six weeks in advance.


Touch Tours

The Parthenon Introductory Gallery has Braille labels and plaster cast reliefs of the Parthenon sculptures which can be touched. A trained volunteer can be made available to accompany visitors around the museum. This service should be requested in advance by contacting the Education Department.


Handling Session

Group and Individual sessions where you can handle objects from the Museum's collection can be arranged through the Learning and Information Department. These sessions are targeted at visually impaired visitors (both adults and children) although consideration will be made for groups which would benefit from learning about objects through touch.

For further information about Intellectual and Educational Access to our events programme and provision, please contact 020 7323 8506 (voice), 020 7323 8855 (fax), or jsamuels@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk.
