This new permanent display explores ideas of well-being and misfortune throughout the world. In particular, it makes connections between the everyday lives of people from different cultures.


Carved wooden headrest. Shona, 19th century AD. From Zimbabwe
  How do we think about Africa and its peoples, their history, their culture and their place in the world? Material from Africa is held in several different departments of the Museum (Ancient Egypt and Sudan; Africa, Oceania and Americas; Asia; Prehistory and Europe); the Education programme aims to draw linking themes across this material.
