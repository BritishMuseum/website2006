   We hope you will enjoy your visit to the British Museum. Please read the following to help you plan your visit.

Please do not leave your group unsupervised AT ANY TIME whilst they are in the museum.

Booking
When booking your visit please inform us of the following:

  • The date and time of your visit
  • The age and number of people in the group
  • The full group name and address, including country, along with a fax number
Please let us know if you wish to visit any area of the museum in particular, for example the Egyptian galleries. Many groups book for the Egyptian, Greek and Roman galleries: these need to be booked up to three months in advance. Groups can also book for general visits: this means that you can visit most areas of the museum, but will not be able to concentrate on specific rooms. Please book at least 2 weeks in advance.

We recommend that, if possible, you visit the museum in the afternoon when most schoolchildren have left. The galleries are far less crowded after 2pm. It is more helpful if you book by fax. Please allow 5 days for confirmation.

Group size
We are only able to accommodate groups of up to 90 people. If your group is larger you will need to split your group and visit at different times. It is a good idea to have a specific plan for your visit: large groups of people using the galleries without a clear purpose cause congestion. Please ensure you have a minimum of 1 adult for every 10 children.

Entry
Please arrive at the main entrance to the museum on Great Russell Street. If you are visiting the Oriental, Japanese or Prints and Drawings galleries please use the north entrance on Montague Place.
There is space for coats and bags in the cloakroom to the left of the main hall, and limited space in the cloakroom at the north entrance. The cloakroom charges £1 per item.
There is no entrance charge for the museum, but donations are very welcome. There may be a charge for some temporary exhibitions.

Photography and sketching
Visitors may use hand held cameras with flash, and video cameras in most areas of the museum. Tripods are not allowed without prior permission. Drawing using dry media (ie pencils, crayons, chalk etc) is allowed in most galleries. For other media and for further information please refer to the 'Rules on Sketching' leaflet which is available from the Learning and Information Department.

Particular access requirements
It is recommended that you inform us if anyone in your group has particular access requirements. There is lift access to all floors of the Museum. Wheelchairs and magnifying glasses can be borrowed on request.

During your visit
Organise your party into small groups, each with an adult in charge. Please remind all students: not to rush about or shout; not to eat or drink in the galleries; not to touch objects or lean on the cases; to stay together in their groups; to ensure that all chewing gum is deposited in bins before entering the museum.
