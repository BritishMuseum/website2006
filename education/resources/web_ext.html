External websites: KS2

Time Pirates
New website from National Maritime Museum/ Port of London. Explore the River Thames through key stages of its history.

Target the Tudors
The Museum of London's site offers an exciting glimpse into the world of the Tudors through the stories of people from the time and objects in the Museum.

Noisy Paintings
From the National Gallery, London. Bring classic pictures to life by giving them sounds

The Art Zone
Fully interactive site from The National Gallery of Art, Washington. Make your own picture, mobile, 3D twirler or collage. For all ages KS1

External websites: KS3

World myths & legends
Attractive award-winning site listing myths from around the world and how they have inspired works of art.

The Renaissance Collection
Allentown Museum, Pennsylvania offers children a quirky and entertaining introduction to the Renaissance.

Ancient Rome
BBC's comprehensive site. Explore the buildings of Pompeii and look at Hadrian's Wall, or try your luck as a gladiator.


External websites: Further/Higher

Activehistory.co.uk
The site contains interactive lessons, games and tests for students and teachers of history at 16 +.

HistoryWorld
An interactive world history site with facility for users to make their own selection of events and images by year, place and theme.

Learning Curve
This site from the Public Records Office contains online material for the study and teaching of history from KS2 to A level, using original sources such as documents, photographs, video and sound recordings.
