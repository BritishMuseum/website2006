Stone bust of the Maize God, Maya, Late Classic period (AD 600-800). From Copán, Honduras

   The kind of cultural material held in the British Museum often speaks eloquently of the religious beliefs of those who made or used it. The cultures of the ancient and pre-modern world, such as Egypt, Rome or Mexico, are all good sources for material illustrating religious practices. Of the great world religions which still continue as living faiths today, most are well represented in the Museum's collections and the Museum sees it as a priority to encourage faith communities, particularly in Britain, to own this material and work with the Museum in deepening public understanding of it.

To find the best explanatory displays for living faiths, try:
Buddhism: Room 33 (S, SE Asia and China) and 33b (Tibet);
Christianity: Room 49 (Roman Britain) 41 (Early Christian) 42 (Medieval)
Hinduism: Room 33 (S, S.E. Asia)
Islam: Room 34
Jainism: Room 33
Judaism: Room 51 (Persia: look for Cyrus cylinder)
Zoroastrianism: Room 51 (Persia: look for Zoroastrian symbols on coins)
