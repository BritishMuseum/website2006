   The British Museum has played a major role in archaeological survey and excavation since the 19th century. It still leads excavations both within Britain and overseas. Over time, information about ongoing excavations will be made available on The British Museum web pages. This web page is really intended to provide people with general information about archaeology. The Museum hosts a variety of events relevant to archaeology, and a special emphasis is being placed on the provision of resources for A-level Archaeology. A Checklist of 'Things to Do' for those wanting to learn more about Archaeology:
  1. Join the Council for British Archaeology (which also runs the Young Archaeologists' Club) and subscribe to Current Archaeology.
  2. Read a general book on archaeology.
  3. Visit your local museum, an English Heritage site and/or a National Trust site. You will probably find out more about archaeological/historical museums, sites, societies and events in your area. The Council for British Archaeology's handbook, Teaching Archaeology, has an excellent listing of all local museums, sites, societies and archaeological units.
  4. If you wish to excavate, you might be able to gain experience through a local archaeological society. You can also pay to go on a Training Excavation - many are organised all over the country and are advertised in Current Archaeology and the CBA's magazine Archaeology.
  5. Join a county or local archaeology society. They often run lecture programmes, publish journals and even undertake fieldwork.
  6. If you wish to gain a qualification in Archaeology, the CBA's handbooks Teaching Archaeology and Archaeology in Higher Education provide information on a wide range of courses from GCSE to Degree level. Many colleges offer A-level and other diploma/certificate courses.
