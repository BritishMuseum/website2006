There are a variety of paths open to Asian Art Diploma graduates. During the course, we encourage students to speak informally with the wide range of people they will meet through the class. Course tutors and lecturers are available for discussion, as are a range of academics, museum curators, dealers, and auction house people. A number of students in the course come from the Asian art world, and often are a source of useful contacts.

 
The Academic World

A number of students from previous classes have decided to pursue further degrees in the Asian art world at both masters and doctorate level. We can provide personal introductions to a number of degree programmes both at the University of London, Royal Holloway as well as SOAS, De Montfort University and University of East Anglia.

 
Museum Positions

As the course is held within The British Museum, students are able to see many behind-the-scenes workings of a museum. Students are also encouraged to take part in a museum exhibition project during the course; the completed project work is displayed within the museum. Graduates of the course have taken museum positions in both the UK and the US. Stacey Pearson, a tutor for the China module and now curator for the Percival David Foundation, is a course graduate. The Head of Volunteers for The British Museum visits the class, and discusses opportunities for people who are interested in exploring museum work at first hand. The Museum is normally able to offer serious students intern experience.

Museum job information can also be found at www.museumjobs.com and www.globalmuseum.org.

 
Auction Houses

We visit London's major auction houses as a part of the programme, and students often have opportunities to meet with heads of Asian art departments. A number of our graduates have gone to work for principle auction houses, both in this country and abroad.

 
Dealers

London has a rich network of Asian art galleries, and some dealers are invited to lecture on their specialities. Class visits are arranged to galleries during each module, and there is often an opportunity for informal questions. Class members have gone on to work for various dealers.

 
Non-Profit Organisations

There are a number of non-profit organisations that specialise in Asia, including Asia House in London and the Asia Society in New York. Graduates of the course have gone on to work for both these organisations. Students on the course are encouraged to attend appropriate events arranged by London based Asian organisations. Graduates of the programme have gone onto a wide range of activities such as working for the Wellcome Foundation and cataloguing manuscripts.

 
Writing

A number of our graduates have done freelance journalism, and contributed to various Asian art papers and journals.
