Featured Book

Ming Ceramics in The British Museum
The British Museum's outstanding collection of Ming ceramics is now published for the first time in its entirety. Nearly a thousand items are illustrated, identified, dated and discussed, incorporating the most up-to-date archaeological discoveries and new scientific research.

Jessica Harrison-Hall, The British Museum Press, 2001
ISBN: 0 7141 1488 X

   Highly recommended:
  • J. Portal, Korea: Art and Archaeology (BMP,2000)
    Written to coincide with the opening of the new Korea Gallery at the British Museum, this book provides an excellent introduction to the art and archaeology of Korea from the Neolithic period to the present day. Themes of religion and belief are discussed in relation to the social and political structure of Korean society.

  • M. Orban-Szontagh, Traditional Korean Designs (Dover Publications, 1991)
  • B. McKillop, Korean Art and Design (V&A, 1992)
  • J. Smith (ed.), Arts of Korea (Metropolitan Museum of Art, 1998)
  • R. Whitfield (ed.), Treasures from Korea (BMP, 1984)
  • The Arts of Korea: A Resource for Educators (Metropolitan Museum of Art 2001) Includes C-D rom and slides
