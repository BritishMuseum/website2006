ESOL tours at the British Museum

The British Museum is delighted to introduce free 90 minute group tours for students studying English for Speakers of Other Languages.

ESOL Students, ideally working at Entry levels 1 and 2, are introduced to the history, purpose and collections of the British Museum by guides who are experienced in working with students where English is a second or other language. After a short, pre-tour classroom discussion, a general tour of the museum is given, exploring some of the major rooms and galleries: the King's Library, the Reading Room and Great Court, the Egyptian and Assyrian galleries and the African galleries.

Students are given the chance to view a variety of images and written resources, such as photographs, drawings, sculptures, historical exhibits, maps and charts. In the African galleries students can engage in language activities, using Entry level 1 and 2 handouts with basic text and accompanying images. These activities are generally designed to develop students' vocabulary of both historical and everyday words, within a welcoming and stimulating environment.

The downloadable documents, Pre-visit information for tutors and Materials for students and tutors will enable you and your students to get the most from your tour. Alternatively use the information and materials to plan your own tour.
