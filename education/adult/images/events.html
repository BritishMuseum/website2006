Study sessions

Art in focus

Each seminar takes an in-depth look at an art historical subject related to the collections of the Museum and offers a chance for discussion. All levels welcome.

Wren's St Paul's Cathedral

Saturday 27 March
14.00-15.30
Clore Education Centre
£10, concessions £8

The British Museum holds a preparatory drawing by Wren for the famous dome of St Paul's along with drawings by Thornhill for the decoration of the inner dome. This session discusses the development of Wren's designs and how he brought his design from the drawing board, via models into reality after the Great Fire of 1666.

25 places
Tutor: Hilary Williams

 

Wedgwood's Pegasus Vase

Saturday 15 May
14.00-15.30
Clore Education Centre
£10, concessions £8

This is the story of the large beautiful blue jasper vase which in 1786 Josaiah Wedgwood thought of as his masterpiece and presented to the British Museum. Why and how was it made and how does Flaxman fit into this story? How does the vase relate to both the publication produced by Sir William Hamilton and to his collection of Greek vases?

25 places
Tutor: Hilary Williams

 

Jacopo de' Barbari's View of Venice

Saturday 19 June
14.00-15.30
Clore Education Centre
£10, concessions £8

How and why was such a spectacular and enormous aerial view or map made? We will discuss the commission of the German merchant Anton Kolb and how the painter-printmaker Jacopo de' Barbari created in woodcut a representation of this enigmatic city. It shows Venice in 1500 on the verge of its Golden Age but what does it tell us about life in the city then? The map can be viewed in Room 90.

25 places
Tutor: Hilary Williams

The Etruscans

Limestone cippus with relief showing musicians 490-470 BC. From Chiusi, Tuscany, Italy
Saturday 24 April
10.00-13.00
£14, Friends £12, concessions £10

The Etruscans are often portrayed as an enigmatic people of whom we know little. On the contrary we now know a vast amount about this fascinating and influential civilisation. In ancient times they were famous for their love of music, religious devotion and precious metalwork. They also had a great love of feasting, dance and sport which feature on their exuberant tomb paintings, a highlight of any visit to Tuscany.

50 places
Tutor: Judith Swaddling

Destination Libya

Saturday 10 July
10.00 - 13.30
£18, concessions £15

Libya can boast some of the most dramatic monuments in North Africa. In the west the temples and buildings of Cyrene bear witness to the importance of its oracle of Apollo and its site as the terminus of ancient desert trade routes. To the east in Tripolitania lie the phenomenal ruins of Leptis Magna, birthplace of the Roman emperor Septimius Severus, and, overlooking the Mediterranean sea, the city of Sabratha where the writer Apuleius defended himself against charges of Black Magic. We will visit the Greek and Roman galleries to see sculptures from ancient Cyrenaica.

eyeOpener

free daily gallery tours and talks Leaflets giving details of the weekly schedule are available at the Information Desk. The tours and talks are free, but donations are welcome. eyeOpener tours are an enjoyable way to get to know the Museum. They last approximately 50 minutes and are given by trained volunteers. There are ten tours a day, from 11.00 - 15.30, each of which introduces you to a different area of the collection:
  • The Enlightenment Gallery
  • The Classical World
  • The Americas
  • The World of Asia
  • Ancient Egypt
  • Prehistoric/Roman Britain
  • Treasures of the Islamic World
  • Europe: Medieval/Modern
  • The Ancient Near East
  • The Arts and Cultures of Africa

Drop in at lunchtime and you can join an eyeOpener Spotlight talk. These take place daily at 13.15, and are 20 minute talks that focus on one particular aspect of the Museum and its collection. A wide variety of topics are covered, from Greek drinking parties to the Silk Route.
