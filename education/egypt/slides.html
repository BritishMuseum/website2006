Schools: Slide Presentations

Egyptian Mummies: Primary Spotlight

This Key Stage 2 slide presentation runs on set dates throughout the school year. Please see the 2005/6 edition of the Information and events for schools and teachers brochure for further details.

Unwrap the science of mummies in this lively slide show. What is a mummy? How were they made? How can modern technology help us find out about life in ancient Egypt? All these questions and more will be answered as the pupils explore the process of mummification and how archaeologists can investigate mummies to find out about the past.

Curriculum Links

This slide presentation links to the National Curriculum for History at Key Stage 2
Programme of Study - A world history study: A study of the key features, including the everyday lives of men, women and children, of a past society.
Attainment target - themes covered include: sources of information about the past, similarities and differences between different periods of time and recognize why people in the past acted as they did.

Daily Life in Ancient Egypt: Primary Spotlight

This Key Stage 2 slide presentation runs on set dates throughout the school year. Please see the 2005/6 edition of the Information and events for schools and teachers brochure for further details..

Find out how we can use objects to help us find out about the past. This slide presentation will take as its starting point the famous tomb painting of Nebamun hunting in the marshes and lead the pupils on an exploration of different aspects of life in ancient Egypt. The slide presentation will include many objects that the pupils will be able to encounter in the museum galleries.

Curriculum Links

UK National Curriculum for History at Key Stage 2
Programme of Study - A world history study: A world history study: A study of the key features, including the everyday lives of men, women and children, of a past society.
Attainment target - themes covered include: using sources of information to find out about the past and showing knowledge and understanding of people and events in the past.
