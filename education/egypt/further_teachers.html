The books below have been selected to provide teachers with a broad range of information and sources of reference to support the teaching of ancient Egypt.

For Teachers

Aldred, C. Jewels of the Pharaohs. Thames and Hudson, 1978

With colour plates, black and white photographs and line drawings, Art is a basis for explaining the richness of Egyptian adornment. Sources of the semi-precious stones, tools and techniques are concisely covered.

 

Baines J. & Malek, J. Atlas of Ancient Egypt. Phaidon, 1980

A comprehensive survey of Ancient Egypt offering a great deal more information on social and historical themes than its title suggests. The geography of the Nile Valley and sites of importance are supplemented with illustrations of objects and monuments. Some of the special topics covered include everyday life, scribes and writing, women in society and religion and burial customs. A full chronology is given and a useful checklist with statistical data on the pyramids.

 

Erman, A. Life in Ancient Egypt. Dover Publications, 1971

This is a translation of a German book first published in 1886. Its age shows in the wordy and turgid phraseology, but its value is immense. The information on social life is lavish in details covering the family, the home, costume and recreation, agriculture and trade. Numerous line drawings will provide the teacher with opportunities to bring out points about wigs and hairstyles, boats and games, building and numerous other topics. Dates and some rendering of ancient Egyptian names should be checked against the Atlas of Ancient Egypt.

 

James, T. G. H. (ed) An Introduction to Ancient Egypt. British Museum Press, 1979

This is the definitive general handbook to the British Museum's collections of Egyptian antiquities. The most useful sections cover the land and resources, scribal material and art and crafts.

 

James, T. G. H. Pharaoh's People. Bodley Head, 1984

Subtitled 'Scenes from Life in Imperial Egypt', this book gives a view of society particularly drawn from the wealth of material left by the scribes of villages like Deir el-Medina.

 

Lichtheim, M. Ancient Egyptian Literature. University of Carolina, 1973-1976

Volume 1: Old and Middle Kingdoms
Volume 2: The New Kingdom
By far the best up-to-date translations of a wide variety of religious and secular texts, of immense use in introducing pupils to the stories of adventure and fantasy that survive on scrolls of papyrus.

 

Manniche, L. An Ancient Egyptian Herbal. British Museum Press, 1989

Possibilities for classroom projects will come from the pages of this book, which covers the use of over 90 kinds of plants and trees in bouquets, at parties, recipes for banquets, cosmetics and medicine. Line drawings of great clarity make this book visually attractive.

 

Ruffle, J. Heritage of the Pharaohs: An Introduction to Egyptian Archaeology. Phaidon, 1977

A well illustrated account of Egyptian civilisation throughout its major kingdoms (from before 3000 BC to the Roman period) is followed by detailed chapters on government, life on the farm and in the home, crafts and religion.
