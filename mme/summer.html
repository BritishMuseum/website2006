Medieval Summer
Partnership between The British Museum and Firststepnw8.com

Description:
The project will be an opportunity for students from Firststep.com to visit the British Museum and receive a personal tour through the Medieval and Modern Europe Collection. The students will record the visit through digital media and will work together to produce a web page for inclusion on the Compass website/ and or the department website.

The programme will run for the duration 1 day per week over the 4 weeks of the summer holidays.

Dates: 5 -31 August 2002

Programme of student activity
Week one :
Familiarisation of using digital filming equipment and navigating through the Compass Website at FS.c. (Compass staff could be involved in this?)

Week Two:
Visit to the British Museum - AM; talk and discussion 'Authurian Legends and Medieval romance' PM; students to tour museum independently and record visit with digital equipment

Week Three:
Students return to FS.c to create group or individual webpage for posting on Compass site

Week Four:
AM Students complete work. PM visit from British Museum staff to centre to view students work (optional)

Objectives:
To increase local participation in a joint digital media and culture initiative
To promote the work and community inclusion of the Partnership through the museum/centre website
To use the project as a pilot for possible future partnership working with other community based ICT projects

Target Group :
8x Over 50's - Silver surfers
8x Young People - 8-12's

Evaluation:
The success of the Project will be monitored by the production of the webpage and written student evaluation

Co-ordination:
James Robinson; Curator, British Museum and Jo Higgs; Project Manager, Firststep.com
