Department of Medieval and Modern Europe > research > excavations > Projects in the UK

The Department has a traditionally strong involvement in the excavation and study of early Anglo-Saxon burial sites, mostly in collaboration with regional archaeological units and other bodies. Past and current projects include the excavation, conservation, and publication of cemeteries at Mucking, Essex;  Garton Station, Yorkshire; Dover Buckland, Kent; Lakenheath, Suffolk and at the royal burial ground at Sutton Hoo, Suffolk.

Staff are increasingly involved in initiating and/or supporting the investigation of other types of sites, especially rural settlements of the middle and later Saxon periods.  This has included work on settlement and market sites at Netherton, Hampshire; Barham, Suffolk; and Middle Harling, Norfolk, and current collaboration with the Norfolk Archaeological Service at Burnham, Norfolk, on what seems to have been an early regional and long-distance trading centre.

We are continually on the lookout for new opportunities - for example, sites with waterlogged levels, where well-preserved organics may survive, or high status religious sites.

From the later medieval period,  local archaeological society excavations on a substantial moated manor house, Southchurch Hall, Essex during the1970s and 80s have generated a research and publication programme in partnership with Essex County Council and English Heritage.

The results of this extensive work on the material culture of a medieval manorial complex will be published shortly in the monograph series, East Anglian Archaeology.
