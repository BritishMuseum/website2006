Performance

  

Middle East Art Now: words, images, music
Friday 19 May 19.00–20.30  
Room 35
Admission free, booking required
60 places

An evening in the special exhibition Word into Art: Artists of the Modern Middle East. With poets Etel Adnan and Fawzi Karim, artist Said Farhan, performers Mai Ghoussoub, Tanacross and Tanya el-Khouri and harpist Tara Jaff.

  

Persian poetry and music
Friday 2 June, 19.00–20.30
Reading Room
£5, concessions £3
150 places

Celebrate the enduring appeal of Persian poetry, ancient and modern. Readings in Persian and English include works by Hafez, Omar Khayyam and Forough Farrokhzad.

  

Berakah
Saturday 10 June, 18.30
BP Lecture Theatre
£5, concessions £3

This innovative new band features virtuoso musicians of Jewish, Christian and Muslim heritage who will to entertain you with their showcase of the interplay between jazz, classical and Middle Eastern musical traditions.

  

The Rhythm of the Heart – Middle Eastern
music and dance
Sunday 2 July, 14.00–17.00
Admission free, just drop in
Great Court

Internationally renowned belly dancer and choreographer Mia Serra leads a day of public performances, with audience participation and Arabic percussion workshops for all ages.

  

Hilal Dance workshops
Saturday 22 July and Sunday 23 July
BP Lecture Theatre
Talk/demonstration
12.00–12.30
Admission free, no pre-booking 
Workshop
13.00–16.00
Admission free, booking required

Dancer and choreographer Suraya Hilal explains Hilal dance, a dance form which expresses the depth of Egyptian/Arab culture by transforming age-old dance traditions into a holistic modern form. After the talk, join a workshop to learn for yourself. This weekend is part of the Big Dance – a London wide initiative.

Word into Art: music and performance
Tuesday 25 July, 19.00–20.30
Room 35
£5, concessions £3
60 places

An evening of music, dance and art performance set amongst the stunning works in the exhibition Word into Art. Features music and images from artist and singer Houria Niati, performance artist Ninar Esber and stories from 1001 Nights and traditional Arabic music.
