Art and calligraphy (for all ages)

  

Sunday 21 May, 14.00–16.00
Great Court
Admission free, just drop in

  

On this special day a gathering of artists and practising calligraphers, Fou'ad Kouichi Honda, Nassar Mansour, Taha al-Hiti, Lassaad Metoui Ali Saeedy, Mooneer al-Shaarani, Hassan Massoudy and Behman Keryo, a practitioner of the Aramaic script, demonstrate their diverse arts. Try your own hand at Arabic calligraphy and meet other artists who talk about their work in the exhibition Word into Art: Artists of the Modern Middle East.
 

 

Faith in the Middle East (Sign interpreted)

 

Saturday 10 June, 09.30–16.30
BP Lecture Theatre
£18, concessions £12  

  

Experts on the faiths of the Middle East, including Malise Ruthven, Keith Ward, Ulrike Al-Khamis, Moncef Ben Abdeljelil, Dan Cohn-Sherbok and Anthony O’Mahony come together to investigate the variety and richness of religious faith in the Middle East today and reveal how influential Middle Eastern religious thought and practice have been worldwide for thousands of years. Please click here to download a PDF of the full day's programme.
Admission includes free entry to an evening concert by Berakah at 18.30

  

Contemporary art of the Arab world

  

Saturday 1 July, 10.00–13.00
Room 35
£15, BM Friends and concessions £12
50 places

  

Join British Museum curator Venetia Porter, Suzanne Cotter, Museum of Modern Art Oxford and other speakers for a special tour of the Word into Art exhibition and an introduction to the work of contemporary artists from the Arab world.
