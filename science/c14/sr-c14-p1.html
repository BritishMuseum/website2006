Radiocarbon Dating

The discovery of an adult man, in his grave, at Barnack in Lincolnshire posed scientists with a number of questions. One of the first questions to answer about such a discovery is how old it is. The objects found in the grave give us the first clues about its date. The pot is a very unusual shape. Archaeologists call these pots "Beakers", and they have been found in similar graves all over Europe. The metal object on the man's wrist is an archer's wrist guard, used to stop the bowstring bruising his arm every time he fired an arrow. It is also a special shape, which is typical of the beaker period. Altogether, the shape of the grave, and the things in it, tell us that this man lived in the Beaker period, the earliest part of the British Bronze Age. But this still doesn't tell us exactly when this was. By using science, and a process called radiocarbon dating, we can find out just how long ago this man died.
