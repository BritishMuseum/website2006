Whistling Pots from Peru

Some of the most unusual pottery produced in the Americas was made by people who lived along the coast and in the river valleys of Peru, like this one in the form of an owl. Some of the pots 'whistle' when air is blown into the open-ended, hollow tube (to the right of the owl).

 

Whistling pots were made in many different shapes and designs - animals, birds and human-like figures with the different pots making different noises. But how were they made and what makes them whistle?
