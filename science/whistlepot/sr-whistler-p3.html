Whistling Pots from Peru

By carefully examining the blue radiograph in the animated picture, you can see a hollow sphere of clay, about the size of a walnut - this is a whistle. You can also see that the potter attached a clay tube to the whistle and positioned it so that when air is blown into the hollow body of the macaw, through the blowing tube, it is forced over a hole in the sphere. It is this that makes the 'whistle sound', rather like an old-fashioned 'penny whistle'. The diagram shows how the air flows from the blowing tube, past the whistle.
