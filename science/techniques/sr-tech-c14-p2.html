Radiocarbon Dating

By measuring how much 14C remains in ancient
organic materials, it is possible to calculate how long ago they died. To do this requires extensive chemical processing, carried out in laboratories like that of the British Museum To convert the carbon in the ancient objects to a form in which the very low level radioactivity (way below background levels) can be measured. Most radiocarbon dating is carried out on bone or charcoal, as these are the organics that most frequently survive from the past, but many other materials can also be dated using this technique.
