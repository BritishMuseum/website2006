A Roman Wall painting

This Roman wall painting, of two poets in Greek dress comes from a villa at Castelemane di Stabbia, Italy, and dates to around 30 BC. You can see it in Gallery 69 in the Museum.

Recently, it was taken to the Conservation Department because some of the paint, particularly the yellow/green material on the central border, had started to flake. Detailed examination with a microscope showed evidence that this paint layer was, in fact, later repainting over the original Roman surface and that there was an undamaged layer of bright blue paint, possibly the ancient pigment known as Egyptian Blue, underneath it. On close examination it was also possible to see that where was a crack across the lower half of the left figure, which had been painted over. The whole panel was also covered in a later coat of varnish, which had become very discoloured and yellow. To look at how much of the original painting had survived, a small area of the painting was very carefully cleaned.

To see the effects of this work run the mouse over the picture; the cleaned area is on the panel on the right.
