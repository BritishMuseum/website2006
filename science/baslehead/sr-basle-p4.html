What are the Gems on the Basle Head Reliquary ?

Eighteen gemstones decorate the band of the Basle Head Reliquary. They come in a variety of colours including white, purple, orange, black, red and blue. The gemstones were first looked at using a small hand lens. This often allows the gemmologist to identify a particular gem without the need for further analysis. However, as the stones are fixed into the crown, it was not possible to take them out and examine them. This restricts how much a gemmologist can discover. Instead we used a Raman Microprobe, a method that allows us to identify the gemstones without removing them from the crown or taking a sample. We are able to position the gemstone we wish to analyse directly in the instrument beam. Each of the gemstones could then be identified from its Raman spectrum.
