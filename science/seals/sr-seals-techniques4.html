Mesopotamian Cylinder Seals

The most advanced method of engraving was the rotating wheel, powered, like the drills, by a bow. This allowed the designs to be cut more quickly but only came into widespread use around 1300 BC.

The design of the lion was mostly carved into the hard stone seal using a rotating wheel. The curved cuts of the wheel can be seen clearly in the magnified picture of his mane (right). The wheels must have been very small, and the engraver very skilled, because this lion is only 21mm tall.
