Mesopotamian Cylinder Seals

The earliest carvings on hard stone seals were chipped out and this was the main method of engraving cylinder seals prior to around 1750 BC.

Notice the rather rough 'pitted' surface of the design, as seen in the scanning electron microscope. The figure (left), which is only 21 mm high, on this seal was chipped out using small chisels. The same technique was used to engrave the cuniform sign (right). This sign, which translates to 'god', is only 4mm high.
