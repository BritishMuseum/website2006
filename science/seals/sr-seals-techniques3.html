Mesopotamian Cylinder Seals

The use of rotating drills as a form of engraving occurred throughout the history of seal making, although the period between 1750 BC and 1300 BC saw most activity.

The circular and semicircular shapes that form the animals in the design on the left were made with small drills worked with a bow. Some of the drills were hollow, others were solid. On another seal, a bird, probably a falcon (right), was engraved in minute detail. Although the widest part of the falcon's wing is only 5mm across, a solid drill has been used repeatedly to cut some of the feathers.
