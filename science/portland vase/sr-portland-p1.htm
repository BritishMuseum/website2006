The Portland Vase

The Portland Vase is arguably the most famous and certainly the most influential piece of ancient glass in the world. Its blue and white cameo appearance served as a source of inspiration for the potter Josiah Wedgwood, who made several copies in jasper ware, a ceramic which is still popular today. It was not so easy to copy the vase in glass, however, and in the Victorian era a prize of £1000 was offered to anyone who could reproduce it. The challenge was taken up by Philip Pargeter, who blew the blank, and John Northwood, who engraved the frieze, a task which, begun in 1873, was not completed until 1876. The copy was highly acclaimed, and gave rise to a fashion for cameo glass which lasted until the end of the century.
