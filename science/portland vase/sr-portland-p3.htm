Analysing the Portland Vase

In 1845 the vase was catastrophically damaged by a visitor to the Museum who, brandishing a "Persepolitan monument of basalt", dashed it to the floor, breaking it into over one hundred and fifty pieces. The vase has been restored on several occasions since, but a small number of minute slivers remain, one of which was used to obtain an analysis with the scanning electron microscope.
