Organic Chemist

This is a new post, offering the opportunity to develop an area identified as one of potential growth. The post holder will initiate, implement and publish research projects on organic substances in the Museum's collections, and their cultural significance. Duties include the analysis, in the Museum's laboratories and elsewhere, of pigments, dyes, food residues, embalming materials, medicines, cosmetics etc.. The successful candidate will collaborate with the Museum's curatorial and scientific staff, provide advice and evaluate external applications to analyse materials in the collections. S/he will be involved in planning the organic analysis facilities in the Museum's new Study Centre in Central London, activities and in associated outreach, educational and fund-raising activities.

The successful individual will have a relevant post-graduate qualification, preferably with experience in the analysis of organic materials of archaeological origin. Proficiency is particularly sought in the use of gas chromatography-mass spectrometry but experience of high pressure liquid chromatography, FTIR or Raman spectroscopy would be advantageous. The candidate should possess a knowledge of the relevant literature and show evidence of the ability to generate and publish research of high quality. The ability to communicate scientific concepts to a non-specialist audience is essential in this work which deals with materials from many cultures and periods.

Salary in the range £21,000 - £25,000. Benefits include a non-contributory pension scheme.
