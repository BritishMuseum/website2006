The Winchester Hoard

The Winchester hoard is an important recent discovery of Iron Age gold jewellery. There are two sets, possibly a his and hers, each comprising an unusual necklace torc and two gold brooches joined by a chain to secure a cloak. There are also two gold bracelets.

The objects are expertly made and weigh over 1kg in high purity gold, but more intriguing is their historical and political significance. Scientific examination and analysis using a scanning electron microscope shows that these unusual torcs were made of Roman gold by a Roman jeweller, yet they ended up in southern Britain shortly before the Romans invaded these shores. Do we have here a gift from Rome to keep a sympathetic British chieftain sweet, bribery to ensure local support for a Roman takeover?
