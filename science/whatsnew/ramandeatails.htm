RAMAN SPECTROSCOPY IN ARCHAEOLOGY AND ART HISTORY

An international one-day meeting organised by The British Museum,
with the Applied Mineralogy Group of the Mineralogical Society
and the Molecular Spectroscopy Group of the Royal Society of Chemistry
Sponsored by Bruker Optics Ltd and Jobin Yvon - Horiba Ltd

20 November 2001
The British Museum, London


We are able to present an international programme, with over thirty contributions including papers from many of the leading groups in Europe. The day will include oral presentations, a poster session and an exhibition by leading equipment manufacturers.
Topics offered include:

general introduction to the technique
gemstones and jades
ceramics
minerals and mineral pigments
corrosion products on metal artefacts and on glass
oil paintings and paint media
manuscripts
archaeological biomaterials materials such as resins and ivory
dyes
fluorescence suppression/avoidance techniques


The versatility of the technique, the non-destructive nature of the assay and the ability to locate and select areas of interest with very fine spatial resolution make Raman Spectroscopy a powerful technique in the investigation and conservation of archaeological and art historical artefacts.

The meeting will be held in the new Clore Education Centre, below the Great Court of The British Museum.
