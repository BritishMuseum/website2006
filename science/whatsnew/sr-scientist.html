Museum Scientist

A vacancy has arisen for a Museum scientist. The post holder will apply scientific techniques to artefacts in the collections and also use statistical techniques for processing scientific data. In addition, the job holder will have a key role as the principal liaison with Information Services, co-ordinating the IT needs of the Department and also contributing to the further development of the Department web site. The duties will involve the examination and analysis, mainly in the Museum's laboratories, of archaeological materials and artefacts, particularly of metal and ceramics, using a range of scientific techniques such as x-ray fluorescence and inductively coupled plasma spectrometry. The successful candidate will have a relevant science degree, or equivalent, with practical experience of chemical analysis, preferably on archaeological materials. Excellent IT skills and familiarity with Windows-based data presentation and processing packages are also sought, ideally with experience of the application of statistical techniques and web design. The ability to communicate scientific concepts to a non-specialist audience is essential in this work which deals with materials from many cultures and periods.

The salary for this post will be in the range £18,000pa - £20,000pa according to qualifications and experience. Benefits include a non-contributory pension scheme. Please apply with a CV and covering letter (including the names of two professional referees and quoting reference 2001/1) to: Human Resources, The British Museum, Great Russell Street, London WC1B 3DG.

Further information can be obtained by e-mail from recruit@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk or from the Museum Website www.thebritishmuseum.ac.uk
