Were the pots made locally?

Geological maps show us what rock types are present in a particular area. We can look at the rock and mineral inclusions of the Hengistbury Head pottery to see if they match the local rock types around the site. If the pottery was made locally, the inclusions in the pottery will be found in the rocks of the local area. If the pottery contains rock and mineral inclusions NOT found locally to the site then it must have been made elsewhere.
