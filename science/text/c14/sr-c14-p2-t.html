How Old was the Barnack Burial?

This is an x-ray of the leg bone from the burial. It is a femur, from the top part of the leg. One of the problems with radiocarbon dating is that it is a destructive technique, to do it we have to destroy a piece of bone. When something has survived from so long ago it is important to get every scrap of information we can from it, so first we X-rayed it, measured it and had an exact replica made. This copy has been put into the display case instead of the original. If you visit the Museum you can try to decide which is the real bone and which is our replacement.

The x-ray shows a good strong bone with no sign of illness, which tells us that the man was healthy and well fed.
