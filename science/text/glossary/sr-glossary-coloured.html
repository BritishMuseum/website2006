Coloured Glasses

Just as water can be coloured by dissolving in it some crystals of sherbet powder, so molten glass can be coloured by dissolving in it metal oxide powder. Dark blue glass is usually due to the presence of small amounts of cobalt oxide. Turquoise blues and greens are due to copper (think about the blue and green stains on corroded copper piping). By combining a coloured glass made in this way with an opacifier such as antimony, coloured opaque glass can be made.
