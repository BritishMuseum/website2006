The Writings of Theophilus

Theophilus, a monk writing in Germany in around 1100 AD, tells us how enamels were made. He says: 'In the ancient buildings of the pagans, various kinds of glass are found in the mosaic work........They are not transparent but are opaque like little square stones. From these enamels are made....'.

What does Theophilus mean? It sounds as if he is saying that the coloured glass came from mosaics in the ruins of old Roman (pagan) buildings. Mosaics are made up of small cubes (called 'tesserae'). Glass tesserae are usually coloured and opaque, appearing like stones. But was Theophilus correct?
