Was Theophilus Correct?

Is the story told by Theophilus likely to be true? By taking tiny samples from enamels like the Naaman plaque we can measure their chemical composition using the scanning electron microscope.
Look at the chemical composition of a glass from a medieval enamel compared to the glass that was used in church windows of the time.

Graph summary -Chemical composition:
Silicon dioxide in Enamel glass = 67%
Silicon dioxide in Window glass = 53%
Sodium oxide in Enamel glass = 16%
Sodium oxide in Window glass = 3%
Calcium oxide in Enamel glass = 6%
calcium oxide in Window glass = 12%
Potassium oxide in Enamel glass = 0.5%
Potassium oxide in Window glass = 17%
Magnesium oxide in Enamel glass = 0.5%
Magnesium oxide in Window glass = 17%

The two glasses are very different in composition. The enamel is rich in sodium oxide, while the window is rich in potassium oxide. There is also a big difference in magnesium contents. So the enamels are not typical medieval glass. Now compare them with Roman glass.
