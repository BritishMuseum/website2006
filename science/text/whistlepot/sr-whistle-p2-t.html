Whistling Pots from Peru

The ancient Peruvian potters did not uses a traditional potter's wheel to throw their pots, instead they made them by hand, often using moulds. This may have allowed relatively unskilled workers to make vessels, such as the Whistling pots, which have complicated shapes. Whistling pots were first made in about 1000 BC.

X-radiography (the technique used to take 'X-rays' at the dentist and in hospitals) has shown us the secret of what makes these pots 'whistle'.
