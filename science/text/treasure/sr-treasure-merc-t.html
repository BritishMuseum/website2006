Mercury Silvering

Biringuccio writing in the early 16th century about goldsmiths says "... They also make things appear to be of good and fine silver when they are not...." before going on to describe how mercury is used to apply a thin layer of a precious metal (silver or gold) on a base metal object. Most of the mercury would then be driven off by heating and the object "...if it is silver on brass or copper, is thrown in oil and heated with bran flames." Mercury is highly toxic and some metal workers may well have suffered and died as a result of carrying out metal plating using mercury.
