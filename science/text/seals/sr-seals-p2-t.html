Mesopotamian cylinder seals

The seals were carved with miniature animals, gods and other figures, and also cuneiform writing. By rolling the seal across soft clay the design came out as a 'panoramic picture', but the design had to be carved 'back-to-front', so that it would come out the right way round. The person carving the seal must have been very skilled (try writing your name in reverse and carving it into a potato print).
