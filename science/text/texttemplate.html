Scanning Electron Microscope

The scanning electron microscope (SEM) allows us to see things at magnifications hundrEds of times greater than the light microscope. It can focus on both the "hills" and the "valleys" of an object at the same time, so that sharp images of very fine details are produced. However, because the object is viewed in a beam of electrons, rather than light, it appears in shades of grey, like a black and white TV image. False colour can be provided electronically, to emphasise features of interest.
