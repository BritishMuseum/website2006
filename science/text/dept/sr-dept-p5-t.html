Department of Scientific Research: About the Department

The Department housed the first computers in the British Museum and, until recently, remained responsible for all computer-based systems in the Museum. In the 1970s, the Museum launched a programme to computerise the written record of the millions of objects in its collections. The Collections Documentation project continues today and new, web-based interfaces are being developed, one of the aims of which will be to make more information widely available. The Department is also home to COMPASS, (Collections Multimedia Public Access System), a database designed and written with the non-specialist visitor in mind, containing information about key objects from the collections, images, background information and links between them.
