Ceramic provenance studies

Knowing where and how commodities and artefacts were traded in the past adds to our knowledge of the lifestyles and activities of past societies. Unfortunately artefacts rarely have their place of origin marked on them, but we can use scientific methods to find this out. This usually means using some distinct, even unique, feature or ‘finger-print' of the materials from which the artefacts are made. Scientifically, this includes chemical composition, such as elemental and isotopic analysis, and petrology, using mineral and rock identifications. First, it is necessary to show that the various potential sources can be distinguished from each other. An artefact can then be compared with these sources to find a match.
