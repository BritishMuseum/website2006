Metal Microstructure


If a solid lump of metal is hammered, to shape it into a piece of jewellery for example, the microstructure is deformed. The metal soon becomes hard and can take no more hammering without cracks appearing. To overcome this the metal has to be heated before it can be hammered more. This heating and hammering process is repeated when a goldsmith shapes an object. It causes the dendrites to recrystallise, losing their branching shapes.
