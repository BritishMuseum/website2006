Metal Microstructure

We can look at the microstructure of an ancient metal object to find out how it was made. It is usually too small to be seen with the naked eye, but if prepared properly, can be clearly seen with a Microscope.

Metal is cast by heating it until it melts, then pouring it into a mould. As it cools, tiny crystals grow in the liquid and form a special microstructure. The crystals form branching shapes, called dendrites, like those in the photograph. The photograph was taken in a scanning electron microscope and shows an area only half a millimetre across.
