Enamel and Window Glass

Is the story told by Theophilus likely to be true? By taking tiny samples from enamels like the Naaman plaque we can measure their chemical composition using the scanning electron microscope.
Look at the chemical composition of a glass from a medieval enamel compared to the glass used in church windows of the time.

The chart compares the amount of each oxide in the glass. The two glasses are very different in composition. The enamel is rich in sodium oxide, while the window is rich in potassium oxide. There is also a big difference in magnesium contents. So the enamels are not typical medieval glass. Now compare them with Roman glass.
