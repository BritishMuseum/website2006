During the later prehistoric period, objects show clearly a quest for beauty and perfection. The existing techniques of flaking, grinding and polishing were used to create fine flint and stone implements such as knives, mace-heads and battle-axes. The introduction of metalworking provided a fresh medium and new opportunities to demonstrate our mastery over raw materials. The bronze horn from Dunmanway(7), beautiful in itself, was surely destined for some ritual or ceremonial function.

Treasure the Questions

This exhibition may help you appreciate the power of the object in our prehistoric past. The precise function, significance and meaning of many of the objects will always remain a mystery, but with new discoveries archaeologists will reappraise the evidence and will provide revised interpretations. Our uncertainty about much of our prehistoric past is probably best highlighted by the enigmatic Folkton Drums(8) whose function remains a mystery.
