Special Events



 An evening with Lindsey Davis
Friday 9 July 18.30
Reading Room
£7, concessions £3


Historical novelist Lindsey Davis, author of The Course of Honour and the acclaimed Falco detective series, talks about her work. Research has taken her from Palmyra to Benghazi via Anchorage and the Caledonian Road. Lindsey has survived the hazards of faulty equipment and many testing feats of endurance: the water bottle (which leaked), the Universal Survival Tool (which broke), a near-lethal grain of rice and a descent into
a working sewer. Join her in the Reading Room where she will be pleased to take questions.

glass of wine included.

Family Ancient Greeks Day
Sunday 11 July
 Admission free

Hear the story of the Trojan War, listen to ancient Greek music, meet some real Greek hoplite warriors and see how well you would do in the Ancient Olympic Games. Decorate Greek clothes, make gifts for the goddess Athena and then take part in a great procession to celebrate her birthday. Finish your day by settling down for our family film Ulysses starring Kirk Douglas.

 Family activities will be taking place throughout the day from 10.30 to 16.30.
Procession at 14.45. Film at 15.15

All ages welcome

Places may be limited. Please check in the Reading Room for more information on the day.
