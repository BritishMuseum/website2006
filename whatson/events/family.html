Changing, eating and shopping facilities for families  


Babies

Baby-feeding rooms: Ford Centre for Young Visitors and near the East stairs

Baby changing-facilities: Ford Centre for Young Visitors, The Great Court, East stairs, North stairs (Level 0), North Stairs (Level 3) _______________________________________________________________________________________

Toilets

There are toilets on every floor of the Museum. See the Museum plan for details.

_______________________________________________________________________________________

Bags and buggies

If you need to leave bags or other equipment while you visit, there are £1 deposit lockers in the Ford Centre for Young Visitors. Please note, this closes at 17.00 daily.

_______________________________________________________________________________________

Eating and drinking

The Ford Centre for Young Visitors is great for a picnic. It has high chairs, vending machines for sweets and drinks and water fountains. You can also find child-friendly food at the Gallery Café next to Room 12 and at the cafés in the Great Court.

_______________________________________________________________________________________

Shopping

Choose from hundreds of books, games, puzzles and pocket-money souvenirs from the Children's Shop and Bookshop in the Great Court.

What is there for blind and visually impaired children?  
Hands on tables, with the chance to handle real objects from the Museum's collections. Ask at the Information Desk.

The Parthenon Information Room (Room 18) with casts of the Parthenon frieze, raised line drawings and a model of the temple to touch.

_______________________________________________________________________________________

Touch a wooden model of a Native North American Thunderbird (Room 24).

_______________________________________________________________________________________

Touch the facsimile of the Rosetta Stone (Room 1).

_______________________________________________________________________________________

Children's COMPASS is fully accessible to visually impaired children. Ask the Reading Room staff for more information.
