Eternal Egypt organised by the American Federation of Arts in conjunction with the British Museum, is a major exhibition of masterpieces from the Department of Egyptian Antiquities which will be travelling to eight venues in the United States between March 2001 and January 2004.

The Walters Art Gallery
Baltimore Maryland
19 September 2003 - 4 January 2004

No fewer than 144 objects from the Department's collections will form this exhibition. Many of these pieces have never before left the Museum, and while some are on display to the public, many others are little known treasures from the Study Collection which have not been exhibited for many years. The guest curator for the exhibition is Edna R. Russmann of the Brooklyn Museum, one of Egyptology's leading historians of art. The choice of pieces is intended to illustrate all the major periods of Egyptian art from the First Dynasty down to the Roman Period, with particular concentrations of objects from the Middle Kingdom, the New Kingdom and the Late Period. Certain areas of the exhibition will follow particular themes, such as 'The mummy as statue' and 'Gold, substance of the Gods'.

The exhibition is accompanied by the publication of a major catalogue of the exhibits, written by Dr Russmann and other scholars; the catalogue, a joint venture between the American Federation of Arts and the British Museum Press, will also contain introductory essays on the history of the Egyptian collection by T.G.H. James, former keeper of the Department, and on aspects of Egyptian art represented in the exhibition, by Dr Russmann.

The objects in 'Eternal Egypt' are a mixture of pieces which are otherwise on display in the public galleries and those from the Study Collection. Pieces include many which normally are exhibited in the Egyptian Sculpture Gallery (Room 4); while it is regretted that these pieces will not be visible to visitors to London, the opportunity will be taken to replace them with a number of lesser known pieces from the Study Collection in the course of 2001.
