Objects on loan around the world
______________________________________________________________________

The Museum has thousands of objects on loan to several institutions around the world.
Below are some of the places where you can view British Museum objects.

EUROPE


 Essen
Crown and veil
Ruhrlandmuseum and Domschatzkammer
17 March to 3 July

Convents of the middle ages are the subject of this comprehensive exhibition, which includes the British Museum’s ivory seal-die of Godwin and Godgytha (below).
Ruhrlandmuseum and Domschatzkammer, Essen
Ferrara
Reynolds and the Invention of Celebrity
Palazzo dei Diamanti
to 1 May

The first exhibition in Italy devoted to the art of Joshua Reynolds to assess his talent for communicating the fame and notoriety of his influential sitters.
Palazzo dei Diamanti, Ferrara



Hamburg
War & Peace in Ancient Egypt
Helms-Museum
to 17 April

Weapons, stelae, sculptures (see the ivory sphinx holding a figure of a captive, below) and many other objects reveal the art of war in ancient Egypt.
Helms-Museum, Hamburg


Magdeburg
Magdeburg 1200
Kulturhistorisches Museum
8 May to 4 September

The history of this medieval metropolis and modern-day
regional capital is retold through objects dating from 805 to the present day.
Kulturhistorisches Museum, Magdeburg
Rimini
Constantine the Great
Castel Sismondo
12 March to 3 September

A vast array of objects, including the gold plaque with lion-hunt, celebrates the life and achievements of the Roman emperor who founded the city of Constantinople in 330.
Castel Sismondo, Rimini
 

NORTH AMERICA






Houston
Gold! National Treasure, Cultural
Obsession
Houston Museum of Natural Sciences
to 7 August

The role of gold is explored in history, science, art and culture. Objects include the British Museum’s mummy mask of gilded cartonnage.
Houston Museum of Natural Sciences, Houston



Los Angeles
All the Mighty World: The Photographs of Roger Fenton,
1852–1860
Getty Museum
to 24 April

This international touring exhibition, to be shown at Tate
Britain later this year, is the first American survey of the work of one of the most important 19th-century photographers.
Getty Museum, Los Angeles

Montreal
Eternal Egypt: Masterworks of
Ancient Art from the British Museum
Montreal Museum of Fine Arts
to 15 May

Drawn exclusively from the British Museum collection of Egyptian antiquities, the exhibition reaches the final venue of a North American tour.
Montreal Museum of Fine Arts, Montreal

New Haven
William Hodges, 1744–1797: The Art of Exploration
Yale Center for British Art
to 24 April

Retrospective of the work of
landscape painter William Hodges, who travelled with the renowned Captain James Cook to the South Pacific and played a central role in communicating visual knowledge of distant lands and cultures.
Yale Center for British Art, New Haven

San Francisco
The Kingdom of Siam
Asian Art Museum
to 1 May

87 rare works from international collections display the superb but little known art of the Thai kingdom of Ayutthaya, including images of Buddha, sculptures of Hindu deities, jewellery and manuscripts.
Asian Art Museum, San Francisco

Washington
Asian Games: the Art of Contest
Arthur M. Sackler Gallery
26 February to 15 May

This exhibition explores the role of games in Asian culture and their transmission to other cultures from prehistoric times to the present.
Arthur M. Sackler Gallery, Washington

ASIA

Kuala Lumpur
Iznik ceramics
Islamic Arts Museum
long-term loan

A special display of eleven pieces of Iznik ceramics, a loan from the British Museum which follows the showing of the Museum’s exhibition Mightier than the sword in 2004.
Islamic Arts Museum, Kuala Lumpur



Seoul
Treasures from the World’s Cultures
Seoul Arts Centre
11 April to 10 July

Already shown in Japan, the exhibition celebrates 250 years of the Museum and its collections.
Seoul Arts Centre, Seoul
