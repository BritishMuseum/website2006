what's on > dining with...

Enjoy an evening to remember and a lecture by one of The BM's highly knowledgeable curators who will offer fascinating and entertaining insights into the Museum's collections and exhibitions. The lectures will be followed by a themed supper served in the Great Court's spectacular Court Restaurant. The evening will conclude with an exclusive private view.

Schedule for each event
 7.00 pm - Lecture in the Clore Education Centre
8.00 pm - Supper in the Court Restaurant
9.00-10.00 pm - Private gallery view

What's included
Cloakroom facility, lecture, welcome glass of champagne, three course themed supper (drinks are extra), coffee, late view

Please let us know if you are vegetarian, have special dietary requirements or would like to share a table with other diners.

The British Museum Press publishes a range of cookbooks which are the inspiration for the suppers. To learn more, check the online store or telephone 020 7323 8584.

2003

Dining in London in 1753
Thursday 7 August 2003

Go back in time to when The British Museum was called Montagu House, a stately home surrounded by gardens and open fields. Celebrate the Museum's 250th Anniversary with this visit to the past, sampling cuisine of the era. The evening features a visit to the special exhibition, London 1753, and a talk by its curator.

Lecturer: Sheila O'Connell
