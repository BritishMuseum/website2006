Power & Taboo:
Sacred objects from
the Pacific (until 7 January)
explores the power of the
gods in the eastern Pacific
through part of the British
Museum's remarkable early
collections from the region.

A range of lectures,
events and performances complementing the themes
of the exhibition will provide
fascinating insights into
Pacific Islands culture.

Family highlights include
demonstrations by Maori
artist-in-residence George
Nuku in the Great Court,
culminating in Pacific
Islands day on 4 November.

The exhibition's themes and
ideas will be discussed in
more depth in the two-day
Pacific Islands Public
Conference on 1 & 2
December and in journalist
Charles Montgomery's
exploration of how myths
are born, when he discusses
his new book, The Shark God.

Combined with a specially-themed film season, now is the
time to explore the cultures of
the Pacific Islands and the
vibrant and powerful objects
that influenced artists and
thinkers as diverse as
Sigmund Freud, Henry Moore
and Pablo Picasso.

Power & Taboo promotional
partners: Audley Travel,
Tourism New Zealand

Friday 8 December, 11.00–14.30

indigenous and Western-trained artists,
