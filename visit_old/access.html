  Visit home > Access information

This page provides information for disabled visitors about access to the Museum and its collections.

 Main Entrance Great Russell Street

Access (stairs and lifts). The Main entrance (South side, via Great Russell Street) has 12 steps with handrails at each side. There is a self-operable lift on both sides of the steps where a bell is available for visitors requiring assistance.

  Montague Place/ North Entrance

The Montague Place entrance (North side) provides a level entrance to the Museum and a lift taking you to levels in the Museum building. Please note that access from the North lift to the Main floor of the Museum is on level 0.

 Internal access

Access to the Clore Education Centre and the Ford Centre for Young visitors is by the South self-operable lifts in the Great Court.

Access to the Upper floors can be gained by using either the North lift or from the lifts in the Great Court which are self-operable lifts located in the South portico and East and West of the Great Court. Please note that the Upper floors from the Great Court lift are on level 6.

Access to the Joseph Hotung Great Court Gallery, Room 35 level 3, is by the East and West lifts in the Great Court.

Access to the Africa Gallery, on level 0 is by 37 steps in Room 24 or by the East and West lifts in the Great Court.

Access to the Reading Room is at the South side of the Great Court on the Main floor.


 Accessible Toilets

Accessible Toilets are located in the Great Court, in the Ford Young Visitors Centre and the Clore Education Centre.

 Audio Information

A CD is available in the Reading Room library which gives audio information.

 The First Aid Room

The First Aid Room is on the West Side of the Great Court.


  Guide, hearing and assistance dogs

Guide, hearing and assistance dogs are welcome. Dog bowls are available from the Information Desk.

  Large Print Versions

Large Print Versions of What's on, the Free Plan and selected gallery guides are available from the Information Desk and the Department of Learning and Information.

  Magnifying Glasses

Magnifying Glasses may be loaned for a £5 deposit from the Information Desk. Magnifying glasses are also available from the Box Office for special
paying exhibitions.

  Personal Luggage Restrictions

Rucksacks
There will be no admission of large rucksacks or suitcases. Some limited provision for storage of these items will be available at the North Entrance Cloakroom accessed via Montague Place. These items may not be carried through the Museum precincts.

Personal Hand Luggage
Visitors hand luggage will be limited to an item measuring no larger than 39cm x 33cm x 13 cm. Any items larger than this, but not falling into the category of rucksack or suitcase, will have to be deposited at one of the Museum's cloakrooms.

The Museum reserves the right, at its sole discretion, to vary or alter these conditions without prior notice.


 Limited Parking for Disabled Users Only

Limited Parking for Disabled Users Only is available in the Museum's forecourt. This can be arranged by telephoning the Information Desk on +44 (0)20 7323 8299. Please have your vehicle details ready.

  Sign-Interpreted Talks

Sign-Interpreted Talks are an ongoing programme and details are available in What's on, from the Information Desk.

  Sound Enhancement System

A Sound Enhancement System with portable induction loops is available for most gallery talks and to support sign-interpreted tours.

  Sound-Guide System

A Sound-Guide System for the Parthenon Galleries is available in Room 18, which includes a touch section for all visitors, the Tiresias Project.

  Wheelchairs

Wheelchairs are available at both entrances, free of charge. A puncture repair kit is available from the Information Desk.
