Latest news

On 1st April 2003 the departments formerly known as Prehistory & Early Europe and Medieval & Modern Europe were united under the name of the Department of Prehistory & Europe. This forms the largest department in the museum and assumes responsibility for material from world prehistory, Roman Britain and Medieval, Renaissance and post-industrial Europe.

A new website, Virtually the Ice Age - www.creswell-crags.org.uk - has been launched jointly by the British Museum, Creswell Heritage Trust and Derby Museum to explore the archaeology and palaeontology at Creswell Crags, Derbyshire. The website provides views of life and the changing environment during the Stone Age, together with the history of exploration of the site and a detailed catalogue of its finds.

Part of the Department (Later Prehistory and Roman Britain) is currently housed in temporary accommodation, and as a result there is restricted access to many of the collections.
