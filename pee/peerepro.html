Ancient Human Occupation of Britain (Nick Ashton with colleagues from the Natural History Museum, University of London and Durham University)
A five year project funded by the Leverhulme Trust to examine the changing occupation history of Britain from at least 0.5 million years ago to the advent of farming, 6000 years ago. visit website.

Middle Thames Population Project (Nick Ashton and Simon Lewis - Queen Mary College, London)
A survey of artefacts on the Middle Thames river terraces suggests a population decline and absence of humans in Britain during the Middle Palaeolithic. The project assesses the reasons for absence with publication in 2002.

Klemm Collection (Grazyna Orlinska and Wojciech Brzezinski - Pa stwowe Muzeum Archeologiczne, Warsaw)
The publication of the large collection of Neolithic to Post-Medieval artefacts from Poland and Germany collected by Gustav Klemm (1802-67) to be published in 2002.

Modelling and Theorising Metal ‘Flow' (Stuart Needham) A project challenging the received wisdom on the life-cycle of metal, from initial distribution and flow through the networks to the question of ritual versus utilitarian deposition. Several papers published.

Embossed Goldwork (Stuart Needham) The Lockington armlets with earlier finds (e.g. Mold and Rillaton) stimulated the first explicit recognition of an embossed goldwork tradition discrete from the well-known Wessex tradition of the Early Bronze Age. Published in 2000.

Armorican Early Bronze Age (Stuart Needham)
A re-evaluation of the internationally important Armorican grave series, and of the extent and significance of contacts across the Channel with southern Britain. Long-term research that resulted in a substantial paper in 2000.

Bronze Age Fleshhooks (Stuart Needham and Sheridan Bowman - Scientific Research)
A very rare artefact type peculiar to Atlantic Europe. The British Museum examples are for the first time the subject of thorough analysis and technological examination, with significant results. Conclusion of research and submission of paper expected in 2002.

Bronze Age Value Systems (Stuart Needham)
Leverhulme-sponsored research 1998-9 initiated a project aimed at synthesising all major artefact categories of the Copper and Early Bronze Ages in north-west Europe. Value systems and mechanisms of cultural transmission will be central. Publication of a book is expected in 2002/3.

Iron Age pottery and Society in East Anglia (JD Hill)
Long term research on Iron Age societies in East Anglia closely tied to rescue excavations in Cambridgeshire and surrounding counties. This project has already produced 3 major papers and reports due for publication in 2002/3.

Swords and Scabbards (Ian Stead)
The first detailed study since the 1950's of Iron Age swords and scabbards known from Britain. A catalogue and study is expected to be published in 2002.

Roman Medical Instruments (Ralph Jackson)
A long term study of Roman medical instruments from the Roman Empire, currently involving a major catalogue of the Roman and Greek medical instruments in the British Museum. Publication already includes several papers and the book Doctors and Diseases.

Cosmetic Grinders (Ralph Jackson)
A catalogue and study of a group of Late Iron Age and Romano-British cosmetic grinders. Publication is expected in 2003.

Roman Glass (Jenny Price - University of Durham)
A catalogue of the glass objects from Roman Britain held by the British Museum due for completion in early 2003.
