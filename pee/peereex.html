The Department has a long tradition of carrying out new excavations and fieldwork. Many of our excavations are part of long term projects aiming to provide new information. Other excavations are carried out when important new discoveries are made by chance. For most of our ongoing excavations we work with universities, professional archaeological field units and English Heritage. Excavation is clearly focussed on work that enhances our understanding of existing collections, and on projects that make important additions to the collections.

Wetwang Chariot burial
In March 2001 archaeologists and conservators from the British Museum excavated the grave of an Iron Age woman buried with a ‘chariot' at Wetwang in East Yorkshire, England. The burial is about 2300 years old. BBC Television's Meet The Ancestors filmed the excavation and later study of the finds from the grave. Meet The Ancestors also commissioned a ‘revolutionary' full size working reconstruction of the Iron Age chariot. The grave was discovered during construction of a small housing development by Hogg the Builders of York. It was excavated by a team of archaeologists from The British Museum and The Guildhouse and funded by English Heritage. Since the excavation was completed, Conservators and Illustrators at the British Museum have spent many months carefully conserving and analysing the finds. This has led to new, and sometimes controversial, evidence of life in the Iron Age. The finds from the burial have been generously donated to the British Museum by Hogg the Builders.

Elveden Palaeolithic Research Project: 1995 - 1999
(Nick Ashton)
A project in Suffolk examining 400,000 year old flint knapping scatters on the edge of a relict river channel, in collaboration with Queen Mary, University of London.

Hoxne Palaeolithic Project - 2000 - ongoing (Nick Ashton)
 A sampling and coring programme using micro-fauna to date the Lower Palaeolithic site at Hoxne, Suffolk, in collaboration with Queen Mary, University of London and the Natural History Museum.

Glaston Early Upper Palaeolithic site: 2000 (Jill Cook) Excavation of a 28,000 year old ‘leaf point' site in Leicestershire with a rich fauna in collaboration with the University of Leicester Archaeological Service and English Heritage.

Troina Project, Sicily, Italy - 1997 - 2001 (Caroline Malone) Excavation of Copper Age settlement site (c. 3500 - 2500 BC) in collaboration with Cambridge University, University of Catania, Soprintendenza Archeologica - Enna, Oasi Institute, McDonald Institute.

Milton Keynes Treasure - 2000 (Stuart Needham)
A unique hoard of Bronze Age gold objects were found in 2000 unusually contained in a pot. With local archaeologists, BM excavated the pottery vessel.

Winchester Hoard - 2000/2001 (JD Hill and Tony Spence)
A unique hoard of Iron Age gold objects were found by a metal detectorist in 2000. The British Museum investigated the find spot working with Winchester Museum Service. The hoard will be published in 2002.

Roman Shipwrecks Project 2001-ongoing (JD Hill)
A three year project with the Centre for Maritime Archaeology, University of Southampton, searching for Roman shipwrecks in British waters. The current focus of the research is the Pudding Pan Rock/Pan Sands Samian cargo(?) in Herne Bay, Kent.

Als Archaeological Project, Denmark 1989 - ongoing (JD Hill)
Joint project with the Universities of Cambridge, Durham and Southampton investigating the changing social landscape of the Danish island of Als from the Neolithic to the end of the Viking period (c. 4000 BC to AD 1000).

Archaeology of the British Museum 1996-2000 (Tony Spence)
Project to monitor construction work on the British Museum site during the building of the Great Court in collaboration with Pre-Construct Archaeology Ltd and AOC Archaeology Ltd.

Post-Excavation Projects

Grimes Graves (Gill Varndell and Ian Longworth)
Publication of the British Museum's excavations at the internationally important Neolithic Flint Mines in Norfolk, 1972-1976.

Gozo, Malta (Caroline Malone and Simon Stoddart - Cambridge University)
Excavations of a Neolithic mortuary site took place 1987-1994.

Runnymede (Stuart Needham)
Analysis and publication of the British Museum's excavations at the internationally important Neolithic and Late Bronze Age riverside settlement at Runnymede.

Lockington Hoard (Stuart Needham)
A hoard recovered under archaeological excavation not only offered exceptional contextual information, but also contained three stunningly important metal objects with great implications for the Early Bronze Age.

Springfield Moulds (Stuart Needham)
Analysis of an important group of metalworking moulds found in the excavation of a Late Bronze Age site at Springfield, Essex in the 1980's by the Essex County Archaeological Unit.

Pots in Pits: East Yorkshire Settlements (Val Rigby)
Analysis and publication of excavations of Iron Age settlement features in East Yorkshire to produce a detailed chronology of Iron Age domestic pottery from the region.

Iron Age Cemeteries in the Champagne, France (Ian Stead)
Publication of the British Museum excavations of Iron Age cemeteries from the Champagne region between1971 and 1982.

Hoxne Treasure (Catherine Johns and Peter G

uest - Cardiff University)
Analysis and publication of the major hoard of Roman gold and silver jewellery and objects and coins found at Hoxne, Suffolk in 1992. Publication is in preparation.

Bryther, Isles of Scilly 2000 (JD Hill)
Study and illustration of the metal objects recovered from a unique Iron Age burial with weapons and mirror excavated by the Cornwall Archaeological Unit on Bryther in the Isles of Scilly. The burial will be published shortly.
