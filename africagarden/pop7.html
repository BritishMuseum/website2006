Golden Brown – Restios

With their golden-brown flower-heads, restios are characteristic of the South African fynbos – a heath-like habitat and one of the most florally diverse areas of the world. The grass grows during the cool seasons, relying on summer’s natural burning of the landscape to increase its chances of germination.  Cave sites in South Africa reveal that the earliest human culture, which began in Africa (visit Room 36, case 1), used materials from the fynbos in a number of day-to-day activities. Ever since, the foliage of African reeds and grasses has been used in matting, basketry, clothing, paper-making, fodder and fuel. Visitors can see many examples inside the Museum: from everyday Egyptian shopping bags (Room 64, case 18) to fine Ethiopian story-baskets (Room 66).
