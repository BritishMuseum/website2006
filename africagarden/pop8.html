Dwarf Fan Palm – Chamaerops humilis

The dwarf fan palm is robust in a wide range of conditions, but its main attraction is the colour of its leaves: an intense blue-silver on the upper and lower surfaces. Starting life small, the dwarf fan goes on to produce suckers around the base which each develop a separate trunk. A mature specimen can resemble an octopus, with three to eight trunks branching out of the base. In countries like Sierra Leone, Ghana and Liberia, the hard wood and sweet sap of other fan palms are fermented to make palm wine, a popular drink which lends its name to the lilting folk-guitar music which can be heard in bars and on street corners right across Anglophone West Africa. At the other end of the social scale, the wonderful Ugandan court pots in the shape of fruits from the calabash plant (case 23, African Galleries) would have been used for a more expensive delicacy: banana beer.
