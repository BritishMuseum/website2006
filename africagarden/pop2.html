Baobab tree – Adansonia digitata

The Baobab tree has a legendary status in Africa. With age, it develops a spectacular trunk up to 33 feet in diameter, able to hold pockets of water which will see the tree through the dry seasons. Because of this, the Boabab can live as long as 5000 years and its fruit and seeds are a steady source of fuel, medicine and drink. The Baobab’s large, dark green and glossy leaves usually fall in dry, cold winters but the tree may be evergreen in locations with more moist, warm winters. At its enormous size, the Baobab cannot be moved and is represented in the garden by a fabulous bark sculpture created Daniel and Petra Carstens from South Africa, courtesy of the Eden Project.
