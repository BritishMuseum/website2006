Lily of the Desert – Aloe

From the embalming of the body of Jesus to its use in modern cosmetics, the African Aloe (or Aloe vera) is renowned for its medicinal properties. Although it is cactus-like in appearance, the Aloe belongs to the Lily family and can grow in a number of forms, including trees, shrubs or herbs. It stores water in its fleshy leaves to survive long periods of drought and produces tall, beautiful spikes of red and yellow flowers. One of its active ingredients is mucopolysaccharides (or MPS) – long chain sugar molecules effective in the treatment of inflammation, AIDS and cancer.
