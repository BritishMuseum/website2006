Myles Wickstead knows something about planting things. As British Ambassador in Ethiopia, he helped the Ground Force team surprise Prime Minister Zenawi with a spectacular new garden. Now, as Head of the Secretariat of the Commission for Africa that launched its Report at the British Museum in March, he has taken the idea one stage further.
 
“The great nations of the world,” reads the Commission’s report, “in alliance with their African neighbours… must immediately begin the journey that leads us to the ultimate common destination of a more equitable world…”
 
Journeys, in a way, are like gardens. Their fruits begin with single steps, a handful of seeds. They encourage people to connect with the world in new ways, build new bridges and experience new perspectives. It was with this vision that Wickstead once again met Ground Force producer Carol Haslam with the idea of building another garden – this time at the heart of London’s celebrations of African culture and heritage: Africa 05. When Haslam in turn approached the British Museum as the obvious place to encourage such new journeys, the idea for an Africa Garden was an opportunity the Museum could not refuse…
