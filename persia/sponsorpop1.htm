The National Museum of Iran

Founded in 1937 in Tehran, The National Museum of Iran was originally called Iran Bastan Museum ( Museum of Ancient Iran). The National Museum of Iran is an archaeological and historical museum. Its facade was designed in Sasanian architectural style by Andre Godard after a Sasanian period palace of Sarvestan.

Today the National Museum of Iran includes two buildings, the old building for the collection of Ancient Iran and another for the Islamic period, in addition to having a library and a lecture hall.

The collection from the Museum of Ancient Iran consists of artifacts from the Palaeolithic to pre-historic periods, Elamite civilization, the Acheamenid Persian Empire, Selucid and Parthian to the end of the Sasanian period in 7th Century AD.

The Islamic Museum begins in the 7th century and continues to the end of Qajar Dynasty (beginning of 20th century). The museum includes different departments, such as conservation, pre-historic, Ancient, Islamic. The National Museum of Iran regularly has temporary exhibitions.
