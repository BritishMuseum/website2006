Sir Joseph Hotung

Sir Joseph Hotung is a major patron and sponsor of the British Museum, and a former Trustee. He has funded the Joseph E Hotung Gallery of Oriental Antiquities as well as the gallery within the Great Court. Sir Joseph has helped fund many acquisitions for the Museum and his collection of Chinese jades is currently displayed in the Museum.

As well as being particularly associated with Chinese art, Sir Joseph has also long been interested in the Middle East and Central Asia .  He currently funds a chair in Law, Human Rights and Peace Building in the Middle East at SOAS, University of London .  He has travelled extensively in the region and was Trustee of the Department of the Ancient Near East from 2000, having a special interest in ancient Persian art and the Achaemenid period.  Having visited Iran with Dr John Curtis several times, and seen some of the treasures there for himself, he is delighted to be a sponsor of this exhibition.
