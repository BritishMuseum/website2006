Forgotten Empire
with John Curtis
Wednesday 14 September, 13.15

Discover the wealth of Ancient Persia with exhibition curator, John Curtis, as he discusses unparalleled examples of a culture that has informed and transformed the world for over 2000 years.



Fashion at the Persian Court: innovation and continuation
with Vesta Sarkhosh Curtis
Tuesday 4 October, 13.15

Iconic fashions, unique ideas. Examine the environment of the Persian court and its legacy for Iran and the rest of the Ancient Near Eastern world. Then explore the transmission further in Room 69a’s free, accompanying exhibition Iran before Islam.



Susa: royal city of Darius
with Lorna Oakes
Wednesday 12 October, 13.15

Join writer and academic, Lorna Oakes, in revisiting Susa , the first royal seat of Darius, ‘king of kings’, and explore its influence in Ancient Persia.



The story of Persian Cuneiform
with Irving Finkel
Friday 14 October, 13.15
Friday 25 November, 13.15

Curator Irving Finkel relates the remarkable story of how a handful of scholars deciphered the mysteries of Persian cuneiform in the 19 th century, and explores, through the important descriptions on display, its legacy for our understanding of the Ancient Near East.



Forgotten Empire
in Persian with Vesta Sarkhosh Curtis
Sunday 6 November, 13.15

Vesta Sarkhosh Curtis, British Museum, brings Ancient Persia and the astonishing objects in Forgotten Empire to life, in the Persian language that administered and expressed it.



The Achaemenid Persian Army
with Nigel Tallis
Thursday 17 November, 13.15

“The Persians teach their sons, between the ages of five and twenty, only three things: to ride a horse, use the bow, and speak the truth” (Herodotus)

In the ancient world the nature and structure of army, society, economy and state are directly related and interdependent. Nigel Tallis, exhibition co-curator, examines how the Achaemenid army functioned in war and peace, and how a study of ancient warfare can be a key to unlock the past.




Moulding and casting: the story of the Persepolis casts
with St John Simpson
Wednesday 30 November, 13.15

Alongside rare and unprecedented loans from museums in Iran , as well as the Louvre, Forgotten Empire contains outstanding casts of some of the most striking reliefs that survive at Persepolis . St John Simpson, British Museum , explains how, why and when they were made.





The Persian Empire : a model for Rome?
with Sam Moorhead
Friday 9 December, 13.15
It is widely acknowledged that Roman culture was heavily influenced by Ancient Greece. But in many ways, Roman Imperialism owes more to the Persian Empire. Sam Moorhead, British Museum, explores the similarities between these formidable states and considers how they might have even influenced the British Empire.
