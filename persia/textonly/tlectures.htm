The art of Achaemenid Persia: origins and influences
Thursday 6 October, 18.30

John Curtis, curator of Forgotten Empire, evokes the history and distinctive eclecticism of Persian art and architecture.

£5, concessions £3



Persian Fire
Thursday 27 October, 18.30

Tom Holland, popular history writer, resurrects the first East-West 'clash of empires', when Xerxes, King of Persia, invaded mainland Greece.

£5, concessions £3



Persepolis: new perspectives
Lecture and film screening
Friday 25 November, 18.30

David Stronach, University of California, discusses the diversity behind Persepolis and the complexity at its foundation. Followed by the computer-generated film Persepolis – a reconstruction (Goetz Balonier, Germany 2005).

£5, concessions £3



Islamic Art in Iran: the Sasanian heritage
Thursday December 1, 18.30

Robert Hillenbrand, a leading expert on Islamic art, traces the legacy of the Sasanian Empire in the art and culture of Iran.

£5, concessions £3



Diaspora artists and Iran
Thursday 3 November, 18.30

Amna Malik, Slade School of Art, traverses the diaspora generations, from post-revolutionary poetics to engagement with
Iranian modernity.

£5, concessions £3



‘Alexander’ and Persia: film and reality
Thursday 10 November, 18.30

Robin Lane Fox, best known for his books on Alexander the Great, talks about working on Oliver Stone's film and discusses its historic value.

£5, concessions £3



Iranian poster art
Thursday December 8, 18.30

Peter Chelkowski, New York University, unfolds the political and cultural role of poster art in the identity of modern Iran.

£5, concessions £3
