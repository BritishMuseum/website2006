Turtles can fly
Friday 14 October, 18.30
£5, concessions £3

In a refugee camp between Iraq and Turkey villagers desperately seek satellite television equipment to keep updated on the impending American attack. Horror, stoicism and humour combine in this surprisingly uplifting film.

Bhaman Ghobadi
Iran /France 2005
96 minutes, subtitled



Taste of Cherry
Friday 4 November, 18.30
£5, concessions £3

Legendary Iranian filmmaker, Abbas Kiarostami, won the Palme d'Or at Cannes for this contemplative study of old fashioned grace in modern Tehran . An instant classic returning to the big screen.

Abbas Kiarostami
Iran 1997
98 mins, subtitled



The Apple
Saturday 3 December 14.00
£5, concessions £3

Makhmalbaf’s stunning teenage debut recreates the real-life story of the liberation of two daughters locked up in their father’s house since birth.

Samira Makhmalbaf
Iran 1998
85 mins, subtitled



Persepolis recreated
Friday 16 September, 18.30
Admission free
Tickets from the Box Office

Prepare for an audience with the King of Kings as spectacular computer-generated reconstructions bring the Persian palace at Persepolis to life. Introduced by John Curtis, curator of Forgotten Empire: the world of Ancient Persia.

Producer: Farzin Rezaeian
Iran 2004, 60 minutes
Organised with the Iran Heritage Foundation



Cinema Iran
Friday 7 October, 18.30
£5, concessions £3

Iranian film-makers have produced some of the greatest cinema of recent decades. Cinema Iran tells the remarkable David and Goliath story of a small national film industry which turned the tables on Western Cinema. Picture left, Samira Makhmalbaf, director of The Apple.

UK 2004
60 minutes
