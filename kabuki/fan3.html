A wealth of other commercial and non-commercial activity fuelled the public’s obsession with Kabuki, which represented a strong, new identity for urban Japan. Fans developed chants for their heroes during performances. They bought, learnt and sung from Kabuki songbooks. They graffitied their allegiance or satirical attacks towards actors and musicians on walls. And more often than not, the workers who thronged to the theatre with their spare money would put on their own performance – joking, singing, quarrelling, eating and drinking throughout the play.
