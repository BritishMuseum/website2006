Kabuki films

An Actor’s Revenge 
Saturday 30 July, 15.00

‘…one of the most dazzling and stylistically audacious Japanese films ever made’ Chicago Reader

An Actor’s Revenge tells the story of Yukinojo Nakamura (Kazuo Hasegawa), a renowned 19th century onnagata (female impersonator) consumed with one obsession throughout his life: to avenge his parents’ death. An audacious and fascinating exercise in straddling the cultural past and present, Eastern and Western aesthetics, classical theatre and modern cinema.

Director: Kon Ichikawa, 1963, 113 mins, Japan
Stevenson Lecture Theatre, £3, concessions £2
