In 1868, with the opening of Japan to the West, Kabuki was faced with the important challenge of how to adapt itself to a changing world. It finally began to be acknowledged as something the upper classes could enjoy, but before long the theatre found itself competing with cinema and television. It began to be looked upon as having had its day. Since 1945, American culture, in particular, has flooded the market.
