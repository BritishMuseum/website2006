Bancho Sarayashiki – a tested love

1655, Edo (modern day Tokyo), the cherry blossom season. Harima is a young member of the court of Shogun Tokugawa Ietsuna. He leads a flamboyant life, quarrelling every day with the gangsters in town. But secretly he has fallen deeply in love with Okiku. He is her first lover, and they vow to be married.

Then Harima receives a proposal of marriage by way of his aunt. Okiku gets carried away by her imagination - wild fantasies, one after another, occur to her. In order to ascertain Harima's true mind, she finally breaks one of ten precious plates, heirlooms which have been handed down through many generations of Harima’s family. In those days, whoever broke such an heirloom, whether on purpose or by mistake, would be beheaded without fail. Thinking it to be a genuine mistake, however, Harima pardons her. He insists that she is his only sweetheart. Later, however, when she confesses to him fully, he flies into a rage, saying, "You may be satisfied to have confirmed my devotion, but I am pained that you suspected the purity of my heart." Finally, he breaks the nine remaining plates, kills her, and throws the body down a well.
