Kabuki began around 1600 with the performances of Okuni, a shrine maiden from Izumo Shrine. Crowds flocked to see her sensational dances on the dry river beds in the ancient capital of Kyoto. Soon their scale increased and a number of rival companies arose performing large group dances, while offstage, the performers generally acted as prostitutes. Eventually, the government banned women from the stage in an effort to protect public morals – just one in a long history of government restrictions placed on theatre across the world.
