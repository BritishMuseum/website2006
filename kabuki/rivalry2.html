Rikan and Shikan had performed together in their youth. But when the masterful Rikan became a troupe leader in 1805 he refused to play opposite the more junior Shikan, whom he apparently despised.

While Rikan considered himself the servant of a great Kabuki history, crafting traditional Kabuki poetry and rigorously observing the realist technique, he accused the 'upstart' Shikan of outlandish showmanship. In the same correspondence, revealing perhaps the greater offence, he berates Shikan's market takeover in Kabuki goods - including the branded fabrics, fans and storybooks on display in Kabuki Heroes.
