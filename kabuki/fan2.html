The imperative for commercial printers was to come up with a formula that the public would recognise again and again. In scale and use, their product predated most closely the cult of American baseball cards. Kabuki fans would collect them in their hundreds for scrapbooks with utter devotion to a developing series about their favourite actors and the dramatic narrative in which the designs placed them.
