Shikan and Rikan – A Chronology

 

1798

 

Arashi Rikan I (Kichisaburô II) success in the role of Yojirô, the monkey trainer

1790s

 

Large format (ôban) becomes standard in Edo for actor prints

1801-1860s

 

Illustrated playbook (e-iri nehon) production in Osaka

1802-1830s

 

Period of activity of the artist Hokushû (Shunkô) in Osaka

1805

 

Retirement of the actor Sawamura Kunitarô I; Arashi Rikan I becomes troupe leader

1808-1812

 

Nakamura Utaemon III (Shikan)’s first tour of Edo

1812-1821

 

The great rivalry between Rikan and Shikan

1813

 

Large format print (ôban) becomes standard in Osaka for actor prints

1814-1815

 

Shikan’s second tour of Edo

1817-1819

 

Shikan’s final tour of Edo

1818

 

Edo artist Katsushika Hokusai visits Osaka

1820-1

 

Bandô Mitsugorô III and other Edo actors visit Osaka

1821

 

Edo artist Utagawa Kunisada visits Osaka

1821

 

Rikan takes new stage name ‘Arashi Kitsusaburô I’

1821

 

Reconciliation of Rikan and Shikan, and agreement to perform together

1821

 

Death of Rikan

1825

 

‘Retirement’ of Shikan

1820-5

 

Hokushû produces large head (okubi-e) prints of Shikan

1825

 

 Nakamura Tsurusuke I takes ‘Shikan’ as his stage name and becomes Shikan’s artistic heir
