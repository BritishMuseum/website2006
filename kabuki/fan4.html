In the 20th century the energy that surrounded Kabuki drifted towards the celebrities of cinema and television, partly because the theatre had become, after 1860, a home for the Japanese elite. But in recent years Kabuki has once again become a popular ‘alternative’ to the dominant cultural order. A colourful fanaticism is again sweeping across Japan.
