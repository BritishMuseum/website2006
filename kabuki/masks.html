In Kabuki, looking the part is just as important as playing the part. Different faces and costumes have different meanings – happiness, sadness, anger – which Kabuki actors play out, or reflect on. Similarly, different moods have precise actions which Kabuki actors train hard to perfect (although Rikan famously accused the younger Shikan of not studying these – of ‘empty showmanship’).


Click on the images of Rikan and Shikan to the right. Print and cut out the faces and make your own Kabuki mask. What moods do they convey? Act out one of our top Kabuki plots. Imagine you are Rikan playing the lead part. Try acting with large but precise movements, like a dance. Now imagine you are Shikan. Act with more liveliness, faster sometimes.
