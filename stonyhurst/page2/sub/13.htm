Smoked skin moccasins, single piece, with quillwork and beaded decoration
Iroquois, possibly Seneca, New York, early 19th century. Stonyhurst Collection.

Similar moccasins were collected by Lewis Henry Morgan, founder of anthropology for the New York State Museum. He noted in 1850: 'The moccasin is preeminently an Indian invention, and one of the highest antiquity. It is true to nature in its adjustment to the foot, beautiful in its materials and finish, and durable as an article of apparel. It will compare favorably with the best single article for the protection and adornment of the foot ever invented, either in ancient or modern times.'
