Two bent elk antler wrist protectors or bracelets, Plains Indian
before 1825, Mullanphy collection.

When releasing a bow a hunter would risk hurting his arm with the slap or rebound of the bow string. Articles of this kind were used elsewhere in North America, for instance by Arctic peoples. The more decorated of the ornaments is engraved with a serious of repeated designs. Both guards are made from thin vertical strips of antler, probably cut from immature, and so softer, material and then shaped by boiling.



The central line of circles, each with a triangular forms attached, could perhaps represent a line of tipis seen from all sides.
