Illustration from Joseph-François Lafitau's Moeurs des Sauvages, 1724.

Pictorial Collection Department of Ethnography Library, the British Museum.

Lafitau compares the Native North American world to that of the Classical Mediterranean. Here his print maker depicts Native men pulling toboggan with tumplines, or burden straps. They are shown in summer dress of belted breechclouts, and are wearing classical sandles. Instead the travellers should have been shown wearing fur clothing, and used moccasins and snow shoes to traverse thus equipped, relatively easily, the snow covered landscape.
