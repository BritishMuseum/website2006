Northern Plains hard sole moccasins, c. 1825

Moccasins of this type are made with raw hide soles and tanned skin uppers. This pair is highly decorated with beads, particularly of blue. In the early nineteenth century blue beads were considered 'like gold' by explorers such as Meriwether Lewis and William Clark, who crossed the continent in 1804-6. Most beads in the early nineteenth century were made in Venice. This valuable type, known as 'pony' beads, were made by blowing a thin cane of glass, breaking the cane and then rolling and polishing the finished product.
