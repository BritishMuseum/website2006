Wendat Huron Moccasins

The Wendat Huron are perhaps the most famous moccasin makers. During the 18th and 19th centuries Huron women specialised in porcupine quillwork and moosehair embroidery of the finest quality.

A party of leaders visited George IV wearing moccasins of this kind. They were in England to pursue land claims, but instead found themselves performing the 'War Dance' at Windsor Castle.
