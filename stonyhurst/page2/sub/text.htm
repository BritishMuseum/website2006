Introduction

This exhibition introduces the history of Native America in the Mississippi, Great Lakes region during the period 1775-1825.

It is seen through a collection of Native artworks purchased by the British Museum from Stonyhurst College, Lancashire, in 2003. This includes articles brought to Stonyhurst by a sixteen year old Irish-American schoolboy Bryan, son of John Mullanphy.

The collection also includes items acquired in the nineteenth century by Jesuit missionaries and their associates.

The period was one of exceptional change - of the arrival of French and British colonists, of the American Revolution and of the War of 1812. For Native peoples the arrival of the United States, and the creation of territories and then states, spelled the end of effective autonomy for a group of diverse Nations.

This period was also one of the rapid growth of the cotton plantation economy, the source of the Mullanphy wealth.
