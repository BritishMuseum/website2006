Hardwood 'gunstock' war club, with metal blade
Before 1820, Stonyhurst Collection

Hardwood club, of the type known as 'gunstock', with a metal trade blade.

The incised designs include the Four Sacred Directions or Four Quarters shown as a compass, and a self portrait of the owner with a similar club, and his headless vanquished enemies.

This form of weapon was widely used on the Prairies, around the Great Lakes and in the Woodlands.

"On the Plains four was a sacred number and referred to the four directions. Ritual actions were often repeated in sets of four as were songs. Decorative elements may have four elements in their structure. On the American Plains four is still a sacred number and is used in many ways by modern native people."

JoAllyn Archambault






"The heart is a European folk motif and is part of a whole vocabulary of European folk motifs that Indian people adopted from whites. It appeared on many items traded to Indian people such as jewelry and metal ax heads and Indians came to use the design in other media."

JoAllyn Archambault
