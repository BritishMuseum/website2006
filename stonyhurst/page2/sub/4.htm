Map, of buckskin, probably made by the Wea component of the Miami people
c. 1774-5. Mullanphy Collection








The map represents a vast area of the Mississippi-Ohio drainage, including the modern day states of Indiana and Illinois. There are two or three main characteristics of this map: it is a fair copy, and has a precise and clear linear structure. Each river is shown straightened out. Each can be viewed from the point of view of a canoe paddler travelling in the middle of the stream and illustrating the banks as straight panoramas. The axis of the map, approximating to the line of the spine of the deer, is the Wabash river, which runs through Indiana. The edges of the map are the Mississippi and the Ohio rivers, which are shown running parallel to each other to the Wabash.

It is likely that this map relates to the sale of lands from the Wea (Wia) people to a land speculation company in the 1774-5.
