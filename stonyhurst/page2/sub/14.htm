Skin and cloth baby's moccasins, Iroquois, possibly Mohawk or Tuscarora,
Canada or New York, late 19th century. Stonyhurst Collection.


These are constructed of two pieces, the second a semi-circular vamp at the front decorated with clear glass beads. Conceptually these beads are associated with rock crystal, and with properties associated with heavens, light and the Upper World in Native thought. With the development of the railroad system, and of tourism to places such as Niagara Falls and the Great Lakes, demands for new types of goods arose. In particularly beaded moccasins were produced in large quantities
