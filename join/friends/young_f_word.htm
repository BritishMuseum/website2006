Welcome to the Young Friends of the British Museum! Have fun browsing here and learning more about some of our exciting events and activities on offer. Whether it is sleepovers, behind-the-scenes events with curators, Sunday Clubs or trips, the Young Friends offer something for everyone. Three times a year, ReMus, the special magazine just for Young Friends, brings you news, articles, stories, activities and the chance to sign up for events.

Young Friends was started in 1996 to provide a unique opportunity for young people between the ages of 8 and 15 to enjoy the richness and diversity of the Museum's collection. Since then, hundreds of members have found the Museum is a lot more than a place to go on a school trip. They have pitched their sleeping bag on the floor of the Egyptian galleries for a sleepover, met a curator in a small group, discovered what goes on behind-the-scenes in conservation, helped with tours, and learned about historical figures and food among other things. Boring and repetitive it is not!

In June 2002, the Young Friends sponsored the first ever family fundraising event at the Museum. Young Friends and their families and friends raised over £10,000 to support the conservation of a gigantic foot sculpture for the King's Library. On 26 March 2003 we will be at it again to raise funds to help the Museum acquire fabulous treasure and to celebrate the Museum's 250th birthday.

Being Chairman of the Young Friends is one of the best jobs I have ever had because I get to come to some of the events as well. Check out the rest of our web site. If you are not already a member, I hope you will decide to join; if you are, I will look forward to seeing you at one of the upcoming activities.

Patti White
Chair of the Young Friends of the British Museum
