What do the curators say about the Townley Group?

Neal Spencer, Department of Ancient Egypt & Sudan:
"Kom Firin is a large settlement site in the Western Delta of Egypt, 60km southeast of Alexandria. Despite its size, and strategic location along Egypt's ancient western frontier, the site has never been scientifically excavated. Visible remains on the surface include elements of two temples, massive segments of brick enclosure walls that stand up to 10m high, and even parts of urban structures. At present, the town seems to have been occupied between 1000BC and 600AD. The site is under increasing danger from the encroachment of agriculture: over 50% of its area has disappeared during the 20th century.

Through the generous support of The Townley Group, a first season of fieldwork took place in September 2002, in which a topographical survey was undertaken, enabling an accurate contour map of the site to be produced. Two small test trenches yielded evidence of urban structures in the far northwestern corner of the site, and the intriguing remains of a structure beneath an area of granite blocks, which may represent one of the city's temples. A second season is planned in 2002, in which two temple areas will be excavated, alongside excavation of the town's 'citadel'. Firin represents a rare opportunity to learn more about ancient Egyptian towns and their inhabitants."

For more details, contact Neal Spencer: nspencer@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk

 

Christopher Date, Head of Archives:
"The Museum Archive, a rich historical resource and part of the National Archive, has benefited considerably from the unparalleled interest, encouragement and direct support of the Townley Group. This has influenced considerably our work within Archives and provides much impetus as we plan for the future. It is befitting that during its first year a project to provide a facsimile of the Archive of Charles Townley, antiquarian, benefactor and Museum Trustee, was funded solely by the Townley Group.

As we approach the 250th anniversary of the founding of the Museum, we are about to launch the 'Smirke Plans' project which will conserve a unique collection of historical architectural plans and drawings charting the development of the Museum site. We are delighted that this project will receive the support of the Townley Group, ensuring that this collection is preserved for the future, yet available to the widest possible audience."

For more details, contact Christopher Date: cdate@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk
