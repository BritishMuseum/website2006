Townley Book List 2002-2003

Images of the Greek Theatre
Richard Green and Eric Handley
Examines the history of Greek Theatre through its representation on ceramics, sculpture, mosaics, metalware and gems, looking at images, which informed life and culture.

Ancient Egypt Book of the Dead
R.O Faulkner
Investigates a collection of religious and magazine texts from 'The Chapters of Coming Forth', which discuss how the Egyptians aimed to secure a satisfactory afterlife.

Roman Britain
T.W. Potter and Catherine Johns
A look at the arts and culture of Roman Britain viewed in terms of Britain's place in the empire as a whole.

The Story of The British Museum
Marjorie Caygill
A fascinating look at the history of the museum from its foundation in 1753 to present day.

The British Museum Dictionary of Ancient Egypt
Ian Shaw and Paul Nicholson
A pocket sized edition of the classic British Museum reference book complete with photographs, line drawing, site plans and maps. Covering Egypt and Nubia during the the pharonic and Graeco-Roman periods.

Smile
Introduced by Marina Vaizey
A wonderfully illustrated book, which explores how the smile has been used in art the world over to great effect for many centuries.

Buddha
Delia Pemberton
An accessible guide to the treasures, wisdom and sprituality of the Buddha.

Sex or Symbol? Erotic Images of Greece and Rome
Catherine Jones
Investigates the erotic in Greek and Roman art and how it became an embarrassment to scholars of the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries.

Rebels, Pretenders and Imposters
Clive Cheesman and Jonathan Williams
A fascinating book which explores issues of political legitimacy looking at would-be kings, caliphs, emperors and rebel leaders, from Napoleon Bonaparte to King Anthony of Shropshire.

Catalogue of the Townley Archive at The British Museum
Susan J. Hill
Includes an introduction to Charles Townley's life and a concise guide to research into collecting taste and ideas in the second half of the eighteenth century.
