ROTTEN RECIPES AND REMARKABLE REPAST - THE WEIRD THINGS PEOPLE ATE IN THE PAST

Did you know…

...that the Romans ate most of their food with their fingers?
...that the Romans ate lying down, which gave them terrible indigestion and awfully bad breath?
...that one type of Roman toothpaste actually contained urine… yucky?
...that the Romans would sometimes make themselves sick half way through a meal to make room for the next course?
...that Greek hosts often mixed their wine with water to make sure their guests didn't get too drunk?
...that in Medieval times people sometimes ate fermented grain or grain that had gone off, which made them hallucinate… weird?
...that the Romans ate lots of bizarre foods, including parrots, dormice, porpoises and cockerel combs?
...that bread was sometimes used to pay the wages of farm workers in ancient Egypt?
...that the ancient Egyptians used to bury meals with their dead to make sure they didn't go hungry in the afterlife?
...that Medieval monks would fast for days on end, and that until the 13th century they were not allowed to eat any meat or fish?


Why not try and make some unusual ancient recipes for yourself at home? You may have to go out hunting for strange ingredients or use a little imagination…

Egyptian Glazed Walnuts

2 oz ground almonds
2 oz caster sugar
1-2 tbsp orange flower water
30 walnut halves
2 oz granulated sugar

Mix the almonds, caster sugar and orange flower water to make a paste, then use it to stick the walnut halves together. Put the walnuts on a baking tray, then melt the granulated sugar and pour it over them. The sugar will cool and hold the nuts together. Yummy!

Roman Glazed Dormice

4 dormice (you can normally find them hiding behind doors)
honey
salt and pepper to taste
poppy seeds

Skin the dormice and roast them in a hot oven until they are brown. Then cover them in honey, roll them in poppy seeds and add salt and pepper to taste. Put them back in the oven and cook them until the poppy seeds go brown as well. They may look small, but are surprisingly filling!

Roman Stuffed Dates

6 dates per person
almonds, hazelnuts or pine nuts
salt and pepper
3 tbsp honey

Take the stones out of the dates them fill the space left with the nuts and a little pepper. Roll the dates in the salt, then fry the dates and the honey together in a frying pan. Uummmm…

Medieval Pine Nut Candy

7 oz caster sugar
2 tbsp clear honey
½ cup of water
1 handful chopped pine nuts
3 oz white breadcrumbs
1 tsp ground ginger

Put the sugar, honey and water in a pan and heat until the mixture turns into a syrup. Then turn the mixture into a chilled bowl and beat in the remaining ingredients. Put in a tray to cool, then cut into small pieces.

Garum
A Roman sauce

Rotting fish heads
Rotting fish intestines (apparently foetid tuna gives a particularly good flavour)

Take the fish heads and intestines and leave them to ferment in a hot sun for seven days. Take the foetid mixture and pass it through a sieve. Discard the solid remains, and refine the liquid mixture. The sauce can be used very much like Tomato Ketchup or Daddy's Sauce, as an accompaniment to all dishes.
