27 Aztec Snack Attack 10.00 - 2.00 Imagine a world without chocolate! Or tomatoes or avocados… all these are foods (and words) that we borrowed from the Aztecs. Find out more about their history in the Mexican Gallery and learn how to make some delicious dishes with chef Dave Taylor. Lunch included. (Not suitable for children with nut allergies.)
Meet in the Hartwell Room. 40 places. Tickets: £10

June event time details and location host
1 Behind the Scenes. Amazing Armour! 2.00 - 3.30 A chance for some of our older members to come along to the Department of Medieval and Later Antiquities and go behind the scenes. Find out what it would have been like to be a medieval knight and have a close look at some real armour. Meet in the Front Hall. Ages 11+12. Places. Tickets: £5 With Dirk Breiding
2 Latin American Day 10.00 - 3.00 Have you ever wanted to learn how to salsa the day away? If so, this is your big chance to come along to the British Museum and strut your stuff. There will also be a jewellery workshop where you can get creative with Aztec gold, and an opportunity to find out all about the mysterious Nazca lines and Latin American customs and cultures. Please meet in the King’s Library. 80 places. Tickets: children £12 / adults £6

23 - 24 Sleepover. Egyptian Night 6.45 It’s summer time again, and you know what that means… it’s time for the annual YFBM Egyptian Sleepover! This year you can find out all about Egyptian gods and goddesses, sneak a peek inside an Egyptian tomb, and make your own ancient Egyptian amulet to keep you safe through the night.
Meet in the Front Hall. Children must be accompanied by an adult (maximum 5 children per adult). 250 places. Tickets: children £22 / adults £20

2 Lacquer Lowdown 11.00 - 1.00 Find your way around the Visions of a Golden Land exhibition with the help of the man who put much of the collection together. There will be an opportunity to find out how the lacquer is made, and a handling session, so you can really get a feel for these wonderful objects.
Meet at the North Entrance, Montague Place. 15 places (children only). Tickets: £5 		With Ralph Isaacs
4 Family Evening Opening. Explorers and Conquerors 6.00 - 9.00 Another opportunity to come along to the Museum for our annual Family Evening Opening. This year activities will include story telling in the galleries, Native American crafts and Viking coin making. The British Museum conservation team will be looking at the conservation of treasure to the accompaniment of South American music. Come along and bring the family.
Main Entrance. Tickets: free, but must be booked.		  
8 Drama Workshop. Terrible Tudors 10.30 -1.00 If you could travel back in time to the Tudor period what sort of world would you find? Come along to Xenia Theatre Company’s drama workshop to find out more about life at the Tudor court. Not for the faint hearted, this morning of Tudor related activities will encompass war, conspiracy and betrayal. There will even by a rather surprising tennis lesson…
Meet in the Hartwell Room. Tickets: children £10 / Adults £5 		 
9 Sunday Club senior - for the over 11s. Celtic Art   Take a look at how the Celts and Anglo-Saxons made their intricate knot designs, and find out how to do it yourself.  
22 Archaeological Adventures 10.00 - 5.00 To celebrate National Archaeology Day the YFBM has organised a very special trip to the Iron Age hill fort of Danebury. We will be stopping off along the way to visit the Iron Age Museum at Andover, and bringing along a resident British Museum expert to answer any questions you might have.
Meet at the North Entrance, Montague Place. 50 places. Tickets: children & adults £12
