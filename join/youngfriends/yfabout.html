Our members are sent a copy of ReMus, the magazine of the Young Friends, which is published at the beginning of each term. It is full of puzzles, articles and interesting stories, which make learning about the artefacts in The British Museum lots of fun! In it you can read about the Case of the Perambulating Pot, or the Case of the Fidgety Mummy! There are also competitions, and exhibition reviews in each issue, so you can decide what you want to come and see in The British Museum.

Our events programme is also pretty exciting. Have you ever wondered what it might have been like to be an ancient Egyptian Scribe or a Roman soldier? Or wondered what sort of clothes you would have worn if you were an Elizabethan lady? You might find the answers to these questions at one of our drama workshops or Sunday Clubs. You will also have the chance to talk to the curators and conservators, security guards and scientists to find out what it is like to work in a museum.

Maybe the best part of being a Young Friend of The British Museum is that three times a year, members are offered the chance to come to spend a spooky evening sleeping in the museum itself. Everybody brings a sleeping bag and a midnight feast, and beds down for the evening among the sculptures and statues. In the past we have had Egyptian themed nights, an Anglo-Saxon sleepover, and a Native American evening. (Luckily no-one has ever spotted any ghosts.)
