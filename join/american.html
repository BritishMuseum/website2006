American Friends of The British Museum (AFBM) was established in 1989 in recognition of the British Museum∆s global importance. With hundreds of donors, including members, foundations, and corporate supporters, it is one of the most critical sources of financial and other support for the Museum.

AFBM has raised approximately $30 million since its founding to support a variety of Museum-related projects, including exhibitions, acquisitions, education programs and the Great Court, to which it has thus far contributed approximately $20 million.

AFBM maintains a professional staff in New York and an active board of directors from around the United States. Together, they oversee its promotional, educational and fundraising activities. In January 1990, the Internal Revenue Service ruled that AFBM qualifies as a charitable organization, as described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Accordingly, contributions and bequests made to AFBM, which may be in the form of checks, securities, works of art, and so on, are deductible for U.S. income and estate tax purposes, as provided by law.

American Friends hosts a lively schedule of lectures and events in New York, and select U.S. cities, for members and the public at large. It offers several categories of membership with attractive benefits designed to keep members in close touch with The British Museum.
