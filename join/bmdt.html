The British Museum Development Trust was established in 1994 with overall responsibility for funding the British Museum's Great Court scheme and the Development Programme associated with the Museum's 250th Anniversary in 2003. The £100m campaign for the Great Court (opening in December 2000) was successfully completed by the end of 1999 and the BMDT is now entering the second phase of its fundraising programme encompassing a diverse range of major projects.

The Phase Two campaign aims to broaden public awareness of the Museum's unrivalled collections through the opening of new permanent galleries, to promote access to the collections and understanding of the processes of excavation through the British Museum Study Centre and to facilitate the creation and financing of a wide range of educational and outreach projects.

The restoration of the King's Library to its former glory will bring about the revitalization of one of the grandest public rooms in Europe while the opening of the new Sainsbury African Galleries in December 2000 and of the Wellcome Gallery of Ethnography in 2003 will further add to the rich cross-cultural diversity of the collections. With the introduction of the innovative COMPASS project in the restored Reading Room, the treasures of the Museum will be made accessible to members of the public worldwide via the internet.

The BMDT is also responsible for raising endowment funds for specific areas of the Museum's work, including educational posts, acquisitions, conservation and scientific equipment, archaeological excavations and fieldwork and stone conservation.

The Museum depends on the generosity of individuals, foundations and companies to fund the full range of its activities. There are a variety of ways in which you can help to support the British Museum for more information visit our contact information.
