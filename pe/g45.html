The Waddesdon Bequest

This gallery displays the spectacular Waddesdon Bequest; a collection of around 300 precious objects gathered together in the 19th Century by two members of the Rothschild family and bequeathed to the Museum by Baron Ferdinand de Rothschild in 1898. The collection comprises masterpieces of goldsmiths' work such as silver plate, hardstone vessels and jewellery; painted enamels; glass and ceramics; and sculpture and small carvings in wood. 
Baron Ferdinand's aim was to  rival those collections which had been put together from the Renaissance by rulers and princes. Provenance was important to Baron Ferdinand, and the Bequest  includes pieces from  famous collections such as the Holy Thorn Reliquary belonging to Jean, Duc du Berry (died 1416) and hardstone vessels from the collection of the Dukes of Devonshire.
