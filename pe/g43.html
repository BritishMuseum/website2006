The Medieval Tile and Pottery Room

This gallery is closed for redevelopment until 2008

Room 43 is arranged on two levels with the ground floor devoted principally to pottery production in England from the 7th century to the 15th century.  Display case 7 features examples of European pottery imported into England during the course of the Middle Ages, while cases 8-10 explain the manufacturing processes of  late medieval pottery and tiles. The upper space continues the discussion of medieval tile production and design in England with reference to the Canynges Pavement (the largest surviving English, medieval, secular pavement) displayed on the lower level.
