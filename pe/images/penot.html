New evening course

The discovery of Britain's past: a history of archaeology in Britain from the sixteenth to the twentieth century

Tutor: Jill Cook
(Deputy Keeper, Prehistory & Europe)

8 Monday evenings from 5 January, 18.00 - 20.00
Clore Education Centre
£80, BM Friends £72, concessions £65
Book through the British Museum
Box Office 0207 323 8181

Archaeology is about understanding the remains of the past. These remains might be earthworks or artefacts such as tools, weapons, jewellery and pots. They are used to reveal the activities of past societies that may never have been written down. The practice of archaeology goes back to the late seventeenth century. This course will trace the origins and impact since that time. Each of the eight sessions will include talks with slides and, where appropriate, objects for handlings, as well as time for questions and discussion. Starting from week 2, some sessions will begin in either the King's Library or one of the Prehistory & Europe galleries.

No specialist knowledge of archaeology or history will be assumed or required.
