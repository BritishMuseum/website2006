Projects

Prehistory

Ancient Human Occupation of Britain (Nick Ashton with colleagues from the Natural History Museum, University of London and Durham University)
A five year project funded by the Leverhulme Trust to examine the changing occupation history of Britain from at least 0.5 million years ago to the advent of farming, 6000 years ago.

Middle Thames Population Project (Nick Ashton and Simon Lewis - Queen Mary College, London)
A survey of artefacts on the Middle Thames river terraces suggests a population decline and absence of humans in Britain during the Middle Palaeolithic. The project assesses the reasons for absence with the first publication in 2002. Further fieldwork was conducted in late 2003 and more will take place in 2005.

Modelling and Theorising Metal ‘Flow' (Stuart Needham)
A project challenging the received wisdom on the life-cycle of metal, from initial distribution and flow through the networks to the question of ritual versus utilitarian deposition. Several papers published.

Bronze Age Fleshhooks (Sheridan Bowman (Conservation, Science & Documentation) and Stuart Needham)
A very rare artefact type peculiar to Atlantic Europe. The British Museum examples are for the first time the subject of thorough analysis and technological examination, with significant results. Conclusion of research and submission of paper expected in 2005.

Bronze Age Value Systems (Stuart Needham)
Leverhulme-sponsored research 1998-9 initiated a project aimed at synthesising all major artefact categories of the Copper and Early Bronze Ages in north-west Europe. Value systems and mechanisms of cultural transmission will be central. Publication of a number of papers has been completed and is ongoing.

Iron Age Pottery and Society in East Anglia (JD Hill)
Long term research on Iron Age societies in East Anglia closely tied to rescue excavations in Cambridgeshire and surrounding counties. This project is producing an ongoing series of reports and publications.

Swords and Scabbards (Ian Stead)
The first detailed study since the 1950's of Iron Age swords and scabbards known from Britain. A catalogue and study is expected to be published in 2003/4.

Roman

Roman Medical Instruments (Ralph Jackson)
A long term study of Roman medical instruments from Roman Britain the Roman Empire, currently involving a major catalogue of the Greco-Roman medical instruments in the British Museum.

Cosmetic Grinders (Ralph Jackson)
A catalogue and study of a group of Late Iron Age and Romano-British cosmetic grinders. Publication is expected in 2003/4.

Roman Glass (Jenny Price - University of Durham)
A catalogue of the glass objects from Roman Britain held by the British Museum due for completion in late 2003.

Byzantine

Late Roman and Byzantine Weights, Steelyards and Counterpoise Weights in the BM (Chris Entwistle)
A catalogue of the over 800 copper-alloy, glass and lead weights and other weighing equipment in the BM. Forthcoming 2004.

Early Medieval

Anglo-Saxon wire inlay (Sonja Marzinzik)

An investigation of silver wire inlay on iron objects (mainly belt equipment), its relationship with continental inlay and with garnet cloisonné, which ocurs in similar patterns.

The Celtic Collection (Susan Youngs)
A catalogue of the medieval Celtic collection (ca AD 400 - 1150) in the British Museum.

The Anglo-Saxon Horse and its Equipment (Angela Evans)
Research project re-examining the potential of the horse in Anglo-Saxon England taking as a starting point recently excavated horse burials and horse tack at Sutton Hoo and Lakenheath, together with individual finds of bridle fittings.

Patterns in the Use of Garnet in Early Anglo-Saxon Jewellery (Angela Evans)
An overview of the rise and fall in the production of gold and garnet jewellery and fittings in the late sixth and seventh centuries.

High Status Sword Fittings from Early Anglo-Saxon England (Angela Evans with Noël Adams)
A survey of sword fittings in precious metal, principally finds recorded since the introduction of the Treasure Act in 1997.

The Berthier-Delagarde Collection (J. Andrási)
A catalogue of the Berthier-Delagarde Collection of antiquities from the Crimea, publication due in 2004/5

Early Medieval Antiquities from the Carpathian Basin (J. Andrási)
A catalogue of early medieval antiquities from the Carpathian Basin in the British Museum, publication due in 2005/6.

Princely Treasures from Medieval Kiev (L Pekarska)
A catalogue of princely treasures from medieval Kiev in the British Museum and the Metropolitan Museum of Art, publication due in 2004/5.

Medieval

Lewis Chessmen (James Robinson)
James is writing a book described as 'a lively look at the many theories surrounding the ownership of the pieces, why they were hidden and how exactly they were discovered, and places them in the wider context of the ancient game of chess and secular art in the Middle Ages.'

Seal-dies (James Robinson)
Research on the use of seal-dies by women and also those used by goldsmiths.

Post Medieval

Thomas Tompion (Jeremy Evans)
A long term study of the life and work of the outstanding clock and watch-maker Thomas Tompion, as well as the work of his successor George Graham, together with Daniel Quare and Daniel Delander.

Black Forest Clock-Making (Paul Buck)
A long term study of all aspects of Black Forest clock-making.

Gunnery Instruments Between Arms and Maths (Silke Ackermann)
Research project examining the construction and use of pre-1700 so-called 'gunnery-instruments' and their role in the European Renaissance.

Perpetual Calendars (Silke Ackermann)
Ongoing reseach on the construction, production and use of perpetual calendars and in particular on perpetual calendar-medals in the collections of the Departments of Prehistory & Europe and Coins & Medals. A catalogue is in preparation.

Ottoman Science in the 19th Century (Silke Ackermann)
The study of an extraordinary 19th-century Ottoman astrolabe-quadrant in the context of contemporary European and Ottoman instrument-making.

Multi-Period

Istanbul Rescue Archaeological Survey
A joint project with the University of Reading, the University of Istanbul and the Turkish Ministry of Culture to record and publish all unpublished/threatened material pre-1500 within the survey area. Third season.

The Star of Bethlehem (Silke Ackermann)
Object-based research project looking at the representation of the 'Star of Bethlehem' in the context of the possible astronomical and astrological explanations for its appearance.

P&E Library and Historic Archive

A.W. Franks and A.Way Archive Project (Eva Oledzka)
19th century antiquaries and collectors in the light of archival material in the Department of Prehistory and Europe. Expected to be published in 2004.

Rare Books Project (Eva Oledzka)
A catalogue of holdings and preventive conservation project of rare books and archival notebooks in the Department of Prehistory and Europe. This includes a separate project on A.W. Franks' rare books collection presented by him to the Department of British and Medieval Antiquities (now Department of Prehistory and Europe) in late 19th century.
