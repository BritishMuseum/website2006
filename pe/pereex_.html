Excavations

Excavations at Ringlemere, Kent
 The discovery and careful pinpointing of a fabulous Bronze Age gold cup in November 2001 by Cliff Bradshaw has led to repeated investigations of the site near Ringlemere. The work has been conducted by Keith Parfitt of the Canterbury Archaeological Trust in collaboration with this Department and with further support from the Dover Archaeological Group, English Heritage and the BBC.

A completely unknown Early Bronze Age barrow cemetery has been revealed by aerial photography, geophysics and excavation. The mounds are almost ploughed flat and the gold cup - which is similar to the Rillaton cup found in 1837 - appears to have been dragged from its original context by agricultural machinery. Nevertheless, the gold cup barrow has yielded important finds of Early Bronze Age and Late Neolithic date and has been shown to have a large encircling ditch. Intriguingly, however, no burials have yet been located! Further seasons of exploration are planned.

Wetwang Chariot Burial
In March 2001archaeologists and conservators from the British Museum excavated the grave of an Iron Age woman buried with a ‘chariot' at Wetwang in East Yorkshire, England. The burial is about 2300 years old. BBC Television's Meet The Ancestors filmed the excavation and later study of the finds from the grave. Meet The Ancestors also commissioned a ‘revolutionary' full size working reconstruction of the Iron Age chariot. The finds from the burial have been generously donated to the British Museum by Hogg the Builders.

Hoxne Palaeolithic Project (2000) - ongoing (Nick Ashton)
A sampling and coring programme using micro-fauna to date the Lower Palaeolithic site at Hoxne, Suffolk, in collaboration with Queen Mary, University of London and the Natural History Museum.

Roman Shipwrecks Project (2001) - ongoing (JD Hill)
A three year project with the Centre for Maritime Archaeology, University of Southampton, searching for Roman shipwrecks in British waters. The current focus of the research is the Pudding Pan Rock/Pan Sands Samian cargo(?) in Herne Bay, Kent.

Post-Excavation Projects

Elveden Palaeolithic Research Project (Nick Ashton)
Pist-excavation work on a project in Suffolk examining 400,000 year old flint knapping scatters on the edge of an ancient river channel, in collaboration with Queen Mary, University of London.

Grimes Graves (Gill Varndell and Ian Longworth)
Publication of the British Museum's excavations at the internationally important Neolithic Flint Mines in Norfolk, 1972-1976.

Runnymede (Stuart Needham)
Analysis and publication of the British Museum's excavations at the internationally important Neolithic and Late Bronze Age/ earliest Iron Age site at Runnymede Bridge on the bank of the Thames west of London. The current phase of research, on the Riverside zone of the site, is proving to be highly instructive on the nature of 'ritual' practices in a 'domestic' setting around the eighth century BC.

Pots in Pits: East Yorkshire Settlements (Val Rigby)
Analysis and publication of excavations of Iron Age settlement features in East Yorkshire to produce a detailed chronology of Iron Age domestic pottery from the region.

Iron Age Cemeteries in the Champagne, France (Ian Stead)
Publication of the British Museum excavations of Iron Age cemeteries from the Champagne region between1971 and 1982.

Hoxne Treasure (Catherine Johns and Peter Guest - Cardiff University)
Analysis and publication of the major hoard of Roman gold and silver jewellery and objects and coins found at Hoxne, Suffolk in 1992. Publication is in preparation.

Bryther, Isles of Scilly 2000 (JD Hill)
Study and illustration of the metal objects recovered from a unique Iron Age burial with weapons and mirror excavated by the Cornwall Archaeological Unit on Bryther in the Isles of Scilly. The burial will be published shortly.

Glaston Early Upper Palaeolithic Site 2000 (Jill Cook)
Excavation of a 28,000 year old ‘leaf point' site in Leicestershire with a rich fauna in collaboration with the University of Leicester Archaeological Service and English Heritage, to be published shortly.

The Monastery of St Lot, Deir 'Ain 'Abata, Jordan
Since the late 1980s the British Museum has been excavating at the Monastery of St Lot, Deir 'Ain' Abata, Jordan in tandem with the Jordanian Ministry of Tourism and Antiquities. The excavations revealed an Early Byzantine monastic complex with a cave dating back to the Early Bronze Age. Additional graffiti at the site has led to the theory that the monastery was founded around the cave used by Lot and his daughters after fleeing the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah. A catalogue devoted to a study of the monastic complex together with finds of mosaics, plaster, metalwork, glass, pottery, animal and skeletal remains will be published next year.

Ickham Mill Excavation
Cataloguing, evaluation and dating of late/post-Roman official-issue belt-fittings from the excavation at Ickham Mill, Kent, for publication in the report by Canterbury Archaeological Trust for English Heritage.

The Lakenheath Horse Burials (Angela Evans)
Post excavation assessment of finds made during the excavation of a sixth century Anglo-Saxon cemetery at Lakenheath, Suffolk by the Suffolk County Archaeological Service.

Sutton Hoo Excavations 2000 (Angela Evans)
Post excavation assessment of the finds from the early Anglo-Saxon cemetery excavated by the Suffolk County Archaeological Service on the site of the Sutton Hoo Visitor Centre, Suffolk.

Dover Buckland II
Cataloguing and study of swords excavated from graves in the early Anglo-Saxon cemetery of Dover Buckland II, for the report by English Heritage, in collaboration with Conservation Laboratory staff on the organic remains of the hilts and scabbards, and with Reading University on the pattern-welding of the blades.

Swords from Saltwood
Cataloguing and study of swords excavated from graves in the early Anglo-Saxon cemetery at Saltwood, Kent, in collaboration with Reading University on the pattern-welding of the blades, for the report by English Heritage.
