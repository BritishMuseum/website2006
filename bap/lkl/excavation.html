Lak Largai: excavation

From the sections that have been exposed by gullying on the north and south sides it is clear that although there are substantial cultural deposits, the apparent mound consists predominantly of sterile deposits of sand and silty clay.

A complex stratigraphy of nearly 3 meters was exposed during a small-scale rescue excavation in 1986, containing pits, ashy lenses, and floor levels, but no clear architectural features.
