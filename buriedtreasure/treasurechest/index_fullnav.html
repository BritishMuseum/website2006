Congratulations: You've found the Treasure Chest!

It's full of priceless coins, cups and glittering jewels from the exhibition for you to discover. Some of them date all the way back to the Bronze Age, while the most recent date from the late Middle Ages.

There are ten special objects from the Buried treasure exhibition in our treasure chest. Each one tells a different story; of status and society, war, death and of course everyday life in ancient times.

Why were the objects buried? They were either placed in a grave to accompany the dead, or hidden for safekeeping from greedy hands. Some of them were simply lost at the time, by accident.

Imagine finding such a treasure yourself! Most of these stunning pieces were uncovered by ordinary members of the public, usually working with a metal detector, rather than by professional archaeologists.

Take a look at the Winchester hoard: It contains a large gold necklace, called a torc, and is an especially fascinating piece.
