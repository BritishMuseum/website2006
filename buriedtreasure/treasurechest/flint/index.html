Name Flint handaxe
Time period Palaeolithic
Institution Norwich Castle Museum
Place found Norfolk

This might be the oldest man-made object in British history. It was found by Mike Chambers walking his dog on a Norfolk beach. It's a handaxe made from flint. Handaxes were made by the first humans to live in Europe. They could be used for many things such as cutting meat, scraping skins, chopping wood, digging holes and hammering bone. Archaeologists think it is extremely old, made between 700,000 and 500,000 years ago.
