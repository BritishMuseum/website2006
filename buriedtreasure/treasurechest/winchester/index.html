Name Winchester hoard
Time period Iron Age
Institution British Museum
Place found Hampshire

Discovered as recently as 2000 by retired florist Kevan Halls, the Winchester hoard is one of the most important discoveries of Iron Age jewellery ever made. It consists of two sets of jewellery, probably for a man and woman, including two unique necklaces of exquisite craftsmanship. Gold brooches and bracelets complete the sets. The necklaces, if not the other pieces, were almost certainly made outside of Britain, and may have been a gift from a powerful Roman living around the time of Julius Caesar or the first Roman emperor Augustus.
