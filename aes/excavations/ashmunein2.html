The Temples of Thoth

Page 2 of 5

The great temple-complex of Thoth at the site was altered and adapted over the centuries. The Museum's excavations shed light on these different stages from the New Kingdom ( 1567-1150 BC) down to the Roman monuments of the first century AD. The temples of all periods had suffered from having been quarried for stone since early Christian times, but a considerable amount of masonry remained in place. In the Thoth-temple of the New Kingdom the foundations of the great pylon gateways built by kings Horemheb (1323-1295 BC) and Ramesses II (1279-1213 BC) were built from re-used blocks of stone brought from the destroyed temples of Akhenaten's capital at El-Amarna on the opposite bank of the Nile. Similar re-use was observed in the construction of the subsidiary temple of the god Amun and another small temple at the south end of the site, also built by Ramesses II with later additions made under the emperor Nero.
