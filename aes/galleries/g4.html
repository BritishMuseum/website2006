The Egyptian Sculpture Gallery
Free Admission

A gallery displaying Egyptian Sculpture has been part of the Museum since the earliest days. The northern part of the present gallery was ready to receive objects in 1834, while the southern part had to wait until 1854, Various rearrangements have been made over the years, the most recent being in 1981 when the Sculpture Gallery was completely redesigned.

The present arrangement runs chronologically from south to north, with the exception of a group of star objects, including the Rosetta Stone, at the entrance to the gallery.

Clicking on the small images of objects will take you to a larger view. * marks objects which, although normally displayed here, are presently on loan.
