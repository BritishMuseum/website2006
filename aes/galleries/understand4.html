Illuminating the Collection - Fieldwork and Recent Research

Page 4 of 5

Current excavations and surveys in the Nile Valley and Delta and surrounding desert yield important evidence about ancient Egyptian culture. This adds to our understanding of the Museum's collections.

The British Museum actively supports excavation projects, both through its own fieldwork, and by assisting other institutions, including the Supreme Council of Antiquities in Egypt.

Another aspect of curatorial research focuses on material which has been outside Egypt for many years. A spectacular example in recent years was the identification of a statue in the basement of a museum in Southampton, where it had lain for over a century.

The piece dates to the 25th dynasty, and represents a god, possibly Amun, wearing an unusual cap and holding the crook and flail. The facial features reflect those of the reigning king, possibly Taharqa (690-664 BC).
