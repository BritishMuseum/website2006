The Roxie Walker Galleries of Egyptian Funerary Archaeology
Rooms 62 and 63
Free admission

The Roxie Walker Galleries of Egyptian Funerary Archaeology were made possible due to the generous sponsorship of the Bioanthropology Foundation, with additional support made available by the British Museum Society. They were opened to the public on 14 May 1999.

Egyptian funerary remains have always been one of the most popular attractions in the Museum, but the display had not significantly changed in the 20th century. The new arrangement puts the objects into their original tomb contexts, while explaining the background and significance of the individual elements of burials. Modern research on these objects features heavily, in particular investigations into the human remains, X-rays, CAT scans and the like of mummies are on show.
