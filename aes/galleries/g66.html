Coptic Egypt
Room 66
Free admission

An interim display is currently being prepared in Room 66, due to open in January 2004. This will feature material from both Egypt and Ethiopia (drawn from the collections of the Department of Ethnography). The Christian churches of the two countries were closely linked until the middle of the 20th century.

The Egyptian (Coptic) material will include finely decorated textiles, but also architectural elements, sculpture, ceramics and other objects from towns, tombs and monasteries. While many of the objects feature explicitly Christian iconography, the display aims to highlight the diversity of Egyptian culture between the 4th and 8th centuries AD. Themes from Greek mythology are depicted in many of the textiles, while Classical architecture influenced on monastery and tomb design. Elements of pharaonic iconography also persist, most notably in the use of the ankh-cross.

Christian material from Nubia is displayed in Room 65, including objects from the burial of Bishop Timotheos at Qasr Ibrim.
