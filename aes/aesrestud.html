The collection includes several million objects, of which a representative selection are on display in the Museum's galleries. The remainder forms a study collection, which can consulted by appointment in the Department's Study Room. The breadth of this collection, with its variety of material for comparative study, both intact and fragmentary, makes it an invaluable resource for scholarship.

The Study Room is open on weekdays (10am - 1pm, 2pm - 4.30pm). Visitors wishing to work on objects which are in the reserve collections should contact the Department. Space is limited and visitors are requested to make an appointment, preferably at least two weeks in advance, giving the inventory numbers and details of the objects they wish to view. It would be helpful if visitors could give some indication of the length of their stay. We regret that it may be necessary in certain circumstances to limit the number of objects viewed.

Find out about the stone store in the King Edward Basement, cataloguing the collection and the numbering systems in use. Applications for admission should be supported either by a member of the academic staff of the Museum or by evidence of identification (passport, driving licence), and by a letter of recommendation.
