The Treasures of Tutankhamun

The majority of the finds from the tomb of Tutankhamun are in the Egyptian Museum, Cairo. Several items, including the gilded cow head and two model boats, are in the Museum of Egyptian Art at Luxor. The remains of Tutankhamun are in a gilded coffin in his tomb in the Valley of the Kings.

 

 

A selection of the treasures from the tomb of Tutankhamun was displayed in the British Museum from 30 March to 30 Sept in 1972 (the admission charge eas then 50p). More than 1.6 million visitors came to see the exhibition, some queueing for up to eight hours; it was the most popular exhibition ever in the Museum, and paved the way for further major exhibitions on a range of subjects.

Most of Howard Carter's papers about his discovery of the tomb are in The Griffith Institute Oxford, which also contains a set of the photographic negatives taken during the clearance of the tomb.

 

For further information see Nicholas Reeves, The Complete Tutankhamun (Thames and Hudson, London 1990).
