Pyramid Theories

In addition to the standard works cited in Further Readings on Pyramids there is a great deal of literature both published and unpublished which concentrates specifically on the areas of constructional techniques, harmonic design and astronomical alignments.

Much of this material is highly speculative and not always supported by evidence, but some valid points on design are noted in:

W.M.F. Petrie, Pyramids and temples of Gizeh, (London, 1883), 220ff.

and in several articles by John Legon:

"The Giza Site Plan Revisited", in Göttinger Miszellen 124 (1991), 69-78

"The Geometry of the Great Pyramid", in Göttinger Miszellen 108 (1989), 57-64

"The Design of the Pyramid of Khaefre", in Göttinger Miszellen 110 (1989), 27-34.

An important web site which deals with pyramid theories is http://www.cheops.org
