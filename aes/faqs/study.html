How do I become an Egyptologist?

For a career in Egyptology it is essential to do research in the subject as well as a first degree. Full time undergraduate courses in Egyptology are available in the UK at:

  • Oxford (Oriental Institute, Pusey Lane, Oxford OX1 2LE)
  • Cambridge (Faculty of Oriental Studies, Sidgwick Avenue, Cambridge CB3 9DA)
  • University College London (Gower Street, London WC1E 6BT)
  • Liverpool (School of Archaeology, Classics and Oriental Studies, PO Box 147, University of Liverpool, Liverpool L69 3BX)
  • Birmingham (Dept of Ancient History and Archaeology, Edgbaston, Birmingham B15 2TT)

  • Swansea (Dept. of Classics and Ancient History, University of Wales Swansea, Singleton Park, Swansea SA2 8PP)

Several of these concentrate on language and texts rather than practical archaeology or objects. Many of the most important books, including the main hieroglyphic dictionary, are in German, so a reading knowledge of this is often encouraged in university applicants.
