Further Reading on Pyramids

There is a vast amount of information available on the Egyptian pyramids. The best way to find what you want is to begin with some of the books which give an overview of the whole subject, and then use the bibliographies in these books to find other sources for any more material you wish to locate.

The books with which you can begin are easily available and contain all the information that most enquirers will ever need, but for advanced matters it will be necessary to consult specialist publications, which might be obtained through a good library. In case of difficulty, it is possible to obtain photocopies from the National Lending Library at Boston Spa (best to apply through a library located in your area). However, amongst the extensive bibliography on pyramids, the essential works are:

M. Lehner, The Complete Pyramids (Thames & Hudson, 1997).
A highly-detailed study of all the Egyptian pyramids, with additional sections on the pyramids in the Sudan and the interpretation of the pyramid form in modern architecture. The book is extensively illustrated with photographs, drawings and computer reconstructions and contains very up-to-date information.

I.E.S. Edwards, The Pyramids of Egypt, (revised edition, Penguin Books 1991).
This is readily available through bookshops. An extensive bibliography of the subject is available at the back of the book.

The bibliographies in these works contain hundreds of references to other volumes on pyramids for those who wish to explore the subject in depth. Among these publications, the very useful compilation on the pyramids of the Old Kingdom contained in the following series of eight volumes, is particularly valuable for the great number of large-scale plans, sections and elevations:

V. Maragioglio and C. Rinaldi, L'Architettura delle Piramidi Menfite, Volumes I-VIII (Great Pyramid in Vol IV).
Text in English & Italian. Large numbers of plans, sections and elevations. Turin & Rapallo, 1963-77.

Websites
