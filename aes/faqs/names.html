Egyptian Names and Letters

Modern writers have always faced great difficulties in finding a consistent system for conveying the ancient names, above all because the ancient Egyptian scripts record the consonants but not the vowels of words.

These are the consonants as conventionally read from hieroglyphs by Egyptologists, and in the phonetic sequence (left to right) accepted since the late nineteenth century, with a rough approximation of the sound, and below this are the letters suggested to denote that sound in English text.
