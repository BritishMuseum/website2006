Stone Store

One part of the study collection is housed in our new Stone Store in the basement of the King Edward Building. This is a specially conditioned store for porous stone in the Museum, which allows the collection of sculpture, reliefs and stone vessels to be more easily accessible to scholars and students (by appointment).

 The material ranges in size from very small pieces, to fragments of colossal statues weighing several tons. The collection ranges in date from 10 000 BC to the 12th century AD. It is arranged in the Store in two sequences, one for stelae and reliefs, and the other for sculpture in the round. These are in chronological order, starting from the Old Kingdom material at the eastern end.

The idea of a special store for porous stone had been in existence for a while, and planning was already underway then the Great Court Millennium Project started. This provided the major impetus for the installation of the store, as one of the consequences of the new project was that the Department would lose much of its basement storage, which primarily housed stone objects. These basements were emptied, and thus the vast majority of the stone collection is now together for the first time.
