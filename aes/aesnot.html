  The Wendorf Collection

The Wendorf Collection consists of more than six million prehistoric artefacts from excavations between 1963 and 1997 in Egypt and the Sudan conducted by Fred Wendorf, Emeritus Professor of Prehistory at the Southern Methodist University in Dallas, Texas. This important collection of material has been given to the Department of Ancient Egypt and Sudan at The British Museum.

Information about the Collection's contents and availability will be found here.

The Green Diaries

Frederick William Green (1869-1949) worked for a number of years in Egypt. His diaries are among the archives
which belong to this department.

A collection of Green's other papers, 1895-1949, mainly comprising manuscripts, notebooks, correspondence, maps and drawings is held in the Faculty of Oriental Studies, University of Cambridge. They relate to excavations and surveys at Hierakonpolis, el-Kab, the Bucheum and in Nubia, the Eastern Desert, the Fayum and the Western Desert. A collection level description is available on the Faculty's website and it is hoped to mount the full list on the website in due course.

Readers interested in the BM material should contact the department. Those interested in the Cambridge material should contact the Faculty of Oriental Studies in Cambridge directly.
