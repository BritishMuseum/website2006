Loans

Loans permit people around the world to see objects from the collection, most of which it is not possible to display in the Museum at the present time. We have a number of objects on long-term loans. Several of these are Sekhmet statues, which are in Memphis (Tennessee), Stockholm, Heidelberg, and Baltimore. Present major exhibitions on loan include:

Eternal Egypt (USA, 2001-2004)

'Eternal Egypt' organised by the American Federation of Arts in conjunction with The British Museum, is a major exhibition of masterpieces from the Department of Egyptian Antiquities which will be travelling to eight venues in the United States between March 2001 and January 2004.

No fewer than 144 objects from the Department's collections will form this exhibition. Many of these have never before left the Museum, and while some are on display to the public, many others are little known treasures from the Study Collection which have not been exhibited for many years. The guest curator for the exhibition is Edna R. Russmann of the Brooklyn Museum, one of Egyptology's leading historians of art.

The choice of pieces illustrate all the major periods of Egyptian art from the First Dynasty down to the Roman Period, with particular concentrations of objects from the Middle Kingdom, the New Kingdom and the Late Period. Certain areas of the exhibition will follow particular themes, such as 'The mummy as statue' and 'Gold, substance of the Gods'.
