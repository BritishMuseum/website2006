New pathways to Africa in 2005
 
 
Supported by DCMS and the British Council, the Museum’s third Africa  season brings to a finale UK-wide events which have profiled the wealth, history and spirit of Africa: 2 million BC to 2005.
 
• Alongside key partners including the International Council of African Museums, interpretation of African Galleries – the greatest collection of African objects outside the continent – has increased.
 
• Some 4 million visitors have been meeting artists from Nigeria, Mali and the Caribbean, as Ground Force’s Africa Garden explores different African environments onsite and via broadcast.
 
• Senegalese pop stars, Ghanaian bishops and the UK’s
Africa Commission have been offering new perspectives on contemporary politics.
 
And in April the Throne of Weapons tour inspired cross-border workshops among teenagers in Belfast – just three months before an IRA declaration of a permanent commitment to peace.
 
It’s the unique power of the Africa at the British Museum: inspiring the world to see that it is one.
