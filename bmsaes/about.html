About BMSAES

British Museum Studies in Ancient Egypt and Sudan (BMSAES) is an initiative taken by the Department of Ancient Egypt and Sudan to encourage electronic publication of research.

The growth and increasing acceptance of the Internet within the academic community means that it is now possible to publish the results of research in media other than paper. There will always be a place for the conventional publication in book form for many types of work, but there are some areas in which the more rapid appearance of article-length papers offered through the World Wide Web will be of increasing importance. To give just a few examples, consider the following:

  • Electronic publication offers a rapid turnaround of a publication
  • It permits much more extensive use of colour at no extra cost
  • Certain types of 'new media' digital documentation (panoramas, animation, video) can be published no other way

Thus the Department is now offering colleagues the chance to publish scholarly research through The British Museum's web server. Topics can cover any of the subject areas normally associated with Ancient Egypt and the Sudan and related issues. There does not have to be a connection with The British Museum in the article for it to be considered for publication. We will not publish an article about material which may be considered as looted.

Access to BMSAES is free.

Format of the Published Papers

We have decided to make papers available as PDF (Portable Document Format) files, which can be read by the free Acrobat Reader (version 4 and above) made by Adobe Systems. We have decided against using HTML for most submissions. PDF has the following advantages:

  • It permits better control of formatting of text
  • It prints as it appears on screen. Web browsers often produced output formatted differently to what is seen on screen. We anticipate that many readers will want to print articles which are useful to them
  • For articles with numerous graphics, both screen-only and printing versions of the PDF file will be given
  • Like HTML, PDF permits linking and external URLs

HTML will be reserved for anything which need special effects which cannot be created in a PDF file.

Frequency of Appearance

Online publication does not need the high critical threshold of print material to make an issue economical. Thus we propose to publish an issue in any month when as few as one or two articles are ready.

The first issue went online in January 2002. We look forward to hearing from you.
