How to Submit

General enquiries about submissions should be directed to Nigel Strudwick (email nstrudwick@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk).

Guidelines about format of submissions, with a stress on the technical aspect, are available here.

When a paper is received the files submitted will be checked for their readability, and an acknowledgement will be sent as soon as possible thereafter. It is preferred, in the spirit of this project, that correspondence should be by email wherever possible. We hope to be able to review the paper and give a verdict within approximately one month, unless circumstances prevent it.

The reviewing process: To maintain a speedy process, we will as far as possible review papers internally within the Department or The British Museum. However, there are bound to be cases where the necessary expertise cannot be found here, as with, for example, certain scientific aspects of papers. A suitable reviewer will then be found outside The British Museum, and this may mean that it takes a little longer to obtain the verdict.

We propose three possible verdicts:

  • Accept as is, perhaps with minor changes to English.
  • Accept with minor changes such as the addition of certain references.
  • Reject, where more substantive changes are needed. Resubmission may be suggested.

For an accepted paper, the author will be informed of the likely publication date, and asked to make any minor corrections as the formatting process proceeds. A PDF file will be sent for proof corrections.

Corrections: Once a paper has been published, to maintain academic integrity no changes can be made to the original file. We will, however, be happy to publish corrections or additions.
