More Information & Events

An gallery guide leaflet (Available at the entrance to the gallery)

A handlist of the objects on display (available for reference from the Information/Handling Collection desk in the gallery.

A catalogue of the books from the House of Commons Library which are incorporated into the exhibition (available on the computer terminals in the Information Centre in the Round Reading Room of the British Museum).

A beautifully illustrated book entitled Enlightenment. Discovering the World in the Eighteenth Century, edited by Kim Sloan (British Museum Press, 2003) (for sale in the Museum Bookshop, from the British Museum Press website or from booksellers).

Two free trails and a study pack are available from the Information Desks in the Great Court and Reading Room or by post from the Department of Education and Information.

Eighteenth century to eBay is a family trail round the Enlightenment Gallery. It is about why people collect things, how they can arrange them and what these things can tell us about the world. Suitable for families with children 7+.

Carry on collecting is a cross-Museum trail, starting in the Enlightenment Gallery, which introduces you to famous collectors from the past and from other cultures, and compares different ways of looking at objects: in the18th century they contributed to universal knowledge, whilst today we are more interested in objects in their cultural context.

Accessing Enlightenment is a study guide for students at A level and above, studying museology, consumer studies or 18th century cultural history. It links the experience of being a museum visitor now and in the 18th century: what sort of person visited museums, what was their educational background, how easy was it for them to get in and what did they learn while they were here? The study guide costs £8.99 and is a new type of learning resource produced by the Department for Education and Information; comments from users are warmly welcomed.

A range of books about the Enlightenment, the architecture of the British Museum, the history of the British Museum and the cultures that produced the objects on display in the exhibition are available in the Information Centre in the Round Reading Room of the British Museum.
