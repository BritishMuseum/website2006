European Association of Southeast Asian Archaeologists 10th International Conference
Tuesday 14 September to Friday 17 September, 2004

The 10th International EurASEAA Conference will be held at The British Museum, Great Russell Street, London, WC1 3DG in association with the Department of Asia, the Department of Education and the Institute of Archaeology, University College London. The conference will run from Tuesday morning 14th September to Friday 17th with the following weekend left for optional visits and excursions which will be announced in due course.
Because of limitations of space, attendance at the conference will be restricted to a maximum of 150 persons.
As in the previous EurASEAA conferences, papers on all aspect of Southeast Asian archaeology are invited, from prehistory to art history and studies of architecture and ceramics and other materials of the historical period. However research on ethnographic and modern materials will not generally be admitted unless they are closely related to archaeological problems.
Research papers on South or East Asia will only be considered if they closely relate to Southeast Asian themes. However, we consider that much of southern China before the Han expansion can be regarded as 'Southeast Asia'. Presentations should not exceed 20 minutes, and priority will be given to presentations and posters that report new research results or new methodologies.

International Association of Buddhist Studies
planned for September 2005

Hosted by The British Museum, The British Library, and the School for Oriental and African Studies

IABS is the largest international scholarly organisation for Buddhist studies, attracting delegates from Europe, Asia and America to its tri-annual conferences. The 2005 conference, will run for 5 days and feature a host of panels on religion and practice, philosophy, texts and manuscripts, material culture and contemporary issues.

For further information contact the Organising Committee, c/o Dr Michael Willis, Oriental Antiquities.
