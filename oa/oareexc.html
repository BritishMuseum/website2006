The Department actively carries out its own field research, in addition to supporting from time to time excavation and fieldwork undertaken by other bodies.

The Bannu Archaeological Project
The Bannu Archaeological Project has been carrying out fieldwork in the North-west Frontier of Pakistan since 1985. It is currently focused on the early historical site of Akra, believed to be the capital city of the Achaemenid satrapy of Thatagush or Sattagydia.

The Vidisha Project
The aim of this project, directed by Dr. M. Willis, is centred about the ancient city of Vidisha in central India. It is to examine this great site near the famous Buddhist stupa complex at Sanchi, through historical and human geography, religion, archaeology, epigraphy, architecture, vernacular building and other facets of material culture.
