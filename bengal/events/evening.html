Tagore Music Evening

Friday 3 November, 18.30
BP Lecture Theatre

The leading South African soprano Sally Silver will be joined by the renowned Medici Quartet and world class tabla player Sanju Sahai for a special concert. The poetry of Tagore has inspired many composers including Naresh Sohal whose Three Songs from Gitanjali for soprano, string quartet and tabla will be performed in addition to further settings for cello (played by Anthony Lewis) and soprano. The programme will also include a tabla solo and Ravel’s String Quartet.
Supported by the Tabor Foundation
£7, concessions £5

Modernism and the East

Thursday 9 November, 18.30
BP Lecture Theatre

A panel of writers and critics including Amit Chaudhuri (author), Pankaj Mishra (author), Mark Ford (UCL) and Eliot Weinberger (poet) discuss the influence of ideas of Easterness in the Western Modernist canon and explain how they took hold. They will also consider the simultaneous and earlier development of a non-European Modernism in India and Bengal.

Faith, nation, culture:
The Guardian/British Museum Public Forum:
What Bengal's history tells us about living with multiple identities?

Friday 1 December, 18.30
BP Lecture Theatre

Bengal has arguably the longest history of engagement between East and West, stretching back over several centuries of settlement, with Calcutta once the capital city of the British in India. For Bengalis, the British were just one chapter in a long history of cultural exchange and accommodation. That history has seen a cultural heritage shared across faiths (in particular, Hinduism and Islam) and then split, in the twentieth century, across two nations: India and Bangladesh. How does this story of multiple identities - of faith, nation, culture - shed light on the challenges of globalisation in the twenty-first century as many Bengalis migrate across the sub-continent and across the globe? How do those diaspora identities, whether in Tower Hamlets or Delhi, refashion their past and what insights can history can offer for the increasing primacy of religious identity?

£10, concessions £8
