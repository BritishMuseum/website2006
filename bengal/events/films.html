The Goddess (Devi)

Saturday 16 September, 14.00
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

In 1860s Bengal a widowed feudal landlord decides his daughter-in-law Doya is an incarnation of the goddess Kali.

Director: Satyajit Ray
Bengal, 143 mins, 1960

Utsav (Festival)

Friday 22 September, 18.30
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

Drama about family legacy in Calcutta, based on a story by Tagore. Introduced by Sangeeta Datta.

Director: Rituparno Ghosh
Bengal, 124 mins, 2000

Tagore on film

Saturday 30 September, 11.00 – 17.00
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

Andrew Robinson, biographer of both Rabindranath Tagore and Satyajit Ray, gives an illustrated discussion on the two great Bengali artists. Followed by screenings of Ray's film work.

£15, £10 concessions

36 Chowringhee Lane

Friday 20 October, 18.30
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

In post-independence India, a lonely Anglo-Indian school teacher allows a former student to have romantic tryst in her apartment.

Director: Aparna Sen
Bengal, 122 mins, 1981

The Cloud-Capped Star (Meghe Dhaka Tara)

Friday 27 October, 18.30
BP Lecture Theatre

A dark melodrama set in late 1950s Calcutta about a refugee family from East Bengal and the struggle of their eldest daughter to keep them afloat.

Director: Ritwik Ghatak
Bengal, 122 mins, 1960

In Search of Famine
(Akaler Sandhaney)

Friday 3 November, 18.30
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

Director: Mrinal Sen
India, 125 mins, 1980

Letters from an Ordinary Girl

Saturday 4 November, 13.00
BP Lecture Theatre

With special guest Sangeeta Datta.
Based on Tagore's Sadharon Meye this short film revolves around the letters a girl in Calcutta sends to a famous writer in England, later found and responded to by a girl in London. Followed by a discussion.

Director: Sangeeta Datta
UK, 27 mins, 2006
Admission free, no pre booking

Devdas

Friday 10 November, 18.30
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

 

Director: Bimal Roy
India, 159 mins, 1955
In Hindi with English subtitles

Singing Pictures – Women Painters of Naya

Friday 10 November, 13.00
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

For generations the Patua (Chitrakara) communities of West Bengal have been painters and singers of stories depicted in scrolls. The film follows the daily lives of the Muslim Patua women from the Naya near Calcutta who formed a scroll painter's co-operative.
Directors: Lina Fruzzetti and Ákos Öslöu
USA, 45 mins, 2005
Admission free, no pre-booking

Sand in the Eye(Chokher Bali)

Friday 17 November, 18.30
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

Director: Rituparno Ghosh
India, 167 mins, 2003

The Apu trilogy

Friday 24 November
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

Pather Panchali 14.00
India, 1955, 115 mins

Aparajito 16.15
India, 1956, 113mins

The world of Apu 19.00
India, 1959, 106 mins

Satyajit Ray's most celebrated works, these three films follow the fortunes of Apu, a young boy in rural Bengal through to urban life in Calcutta and the tragic results of his marrying.
With special guest Derek Malcolm

Director: Satyajit Ray
A ticket is required for each film

Bangladesh film day

Saturday 25 November, 14.00 – 18.00
BP Lecture Theatre

Double bill of The Clay Bird (Matir Moina), 2002 and Homeland (Ontorjatra), 2006, with directors Tareque Masud and Catherine Masud.

£15, concessions £10

Durga: The Goddess Comes To London

Thursday 11 January, 13.00 – 14.00
Stevenson Lecture Theatre

To celebrate the completion of the Voices of Bengal season, the Museum will screening MayaVision's Channel 5 documentary on the Durga: Creating an image of the goddess. Michael Wood, from Mayavision, will introduce the film, and there will be an opportunity for discussion following the screening.

Admission free
