The room is named after King George III and was built shortly after his death in 1820 to house his library. The books were removed to the new British Library in 1999 and the room is now being restored as one of London's finest neo-Classical interiors. It will re-open in 2003, the year of the Museum's 250th anniversary, and house a new exhibition Enlightenment: Discovering the World in the 18th Century. This will look at a period which saw the development of a modern, systematic approach to how people understood the world of nature and human achievement, and saw the founding of the British Museum itself. The exhibition will also provide an introduction to the Museum and its collections, and highlight the way that our understanding of much of human history has changed.

The new display is being developed in partnership with the Natural History Museum and the House of Commons Library, and further material is kindly being provided by the Science Museum, the National Maritime Museum, the British Library, the Society of Antiquaries of London, the Victoria and Albert Museum, the Linnean Society of London, the Royal Collections Trust and the Ashmolean Museum.

Major funding towards the project is being generously provided by the The Wolfson Foundation and Simon Sainsbury; further support is being given by the Heritage Lottery Fund, the Pidem Fund, the British Museum Friends, The John Ellerman Foundation and others.
