The Great Court will also encompass new permenant gallery space. In 1970, The British Museum's Department of Ethnography was moved to the Museum of Mankind off Piccadilly because of the lack of space at Bloomsbury. Since then the collections have been displayed in a series of temporary exhibitions. As a major part of the Museum's development plans, the Ethnography collections have been brought back to Bloomsbury to be displayed in new permanent galleries in the Great Court (Sainsbury African Galleries). The return of the Ethnography collections is of vital importance to the Museum's aim of portraying a complete picture of human achievement and enables the works of recent non-western societies to be seen in their proper context among other world cultures, past and present. An important element of the Sainsbury Galleries is to offer a dedicated area for changing displays, focusing particularly on contemporary works. The Sainsbury African Galleries link to planned new galleries for American, Asian, European and Pacific cultures on the north side of the Museum.
