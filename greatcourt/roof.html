As part of the Great Court project, the 1870s extension to the Museum's Weston Great Hall has been removed and a new southern portico has been reinstated using over 1,000 tonnes of stone.

As well as housing lifts providing access to the upper floor, the new South portico has three large portals through which the majority of visitors gain their first view of the Great Court.

Integral to the project on the South side of the site is the re-landscaping of the Museum forecourt to provide a fitting threshold to the refurbished Weston Great Hall and the Great Court. The Museum's Montague Place entrance has been similarly enhanced by the reinstatement of the railings removed at the time of the Second World War. Faithful copies of the original cast iron gates and railings were specially commissioned and now create an impressive feature on the north side of the Museum This sequence of linked spaces forms an important part of a new cultural route from The British Library at St Pancras to Covent Garden.

The Great Court's magnificent glass and steel roof was designed by computer. Work on the roof began in September 1999, and the 478-tonne steel structure, which supports 315 tonnes of glass has been built like a giant jigsaw puzzle. The installation of the glass panels was completed in July 2000. The 11kms of roof structure has been built to an astonishing accuracy of 3mms.
