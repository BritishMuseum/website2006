Currently on show until 2007:
Hadrian's Army of Britain coin from the G. Cope collection
One of the most detailed and visually impressive Roman coins known which specifically commemorates the soldiers who served in the province of Roman Britain. The front of the coin carries a portrait of the Emperor Hadrian (AD 117-38) and is recognisably similar to his statues. The reverse shows Hadrian (who visited Britain in AD 122) raised on a platform in front of a group of soldiers. The three soldiers hold two legionary standards and a flag indicating that they are at the head of a vast legion (which is out of the picture) mustered to hear the emperor's address. Although the emperor is unrealistically large in order to indicate his status, his features are recognisable and the dress and equipment of all the figures are carefully depicted. Below the figures is written EXER(citus) BRITANNICVS (the army of Britain).
