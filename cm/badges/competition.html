What’s the best badge you’ve ever owned? Was it an object of beauty, did it state a cause close to your heart, or maybe it just made you laugh?

We would like to invite you to be a part of the ‘Status Symbols’ exhibition by designing your own badge. It can be a slogan or logo stating a cause close to your heart, or something that describes you, or your friends or family.

We will make your design into a badge and send a copy of it out to you for free. Your badge will then be displayed on the badge wall in the exhibition, as well as on the online Badge Gallery



Designers of the 6 best badges when the exhibition closes in January 2005 will receive:

3 Adults: a copy of the book ‘Badges’ by Philip Attwood
3 Children (14 and under): a badge-making machine each



1. Download the blank badge template and entry form. Draw or write your design onto the template within the dotted line. Please use flat decoration such as pencil, pen, ink or felt tip. Any raised/3D decoration such as collage, glitter or even paint will not work in our badge-making machine.

2. Fill in the short entry form on the same page. You will be asked for a sentence explaining what your badge is about, or why you chose it. We want to know the story behind your design, and the competition will be judged on this as well as on the design itself.

3. Send in your design and entry form with a sae
(so that we can send you your badge)

to:



Rules

1. Only one design/entry per person. Children under 14 must get the signed permission of a parent or guardian.
2. Please use pencil, pen, ink or felt tip to complete your design. Any raised/3D decoration such as collage, glitter or even paint will not work in the badge-maker.
2. Last entries to be received on or before 15 January 2005. Winners will be notified by 31 January 2005.
3. Please allow 14 days to receive your badge. We will let you know when we send it out to you when it will be displayed online and in the gallery.
4. The Museum reserves the right to reject designs it does not feel are appropriate.
