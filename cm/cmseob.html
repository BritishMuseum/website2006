Staff are please to assist with the identification of objects relating to the Department's collections.

No charge is made for this service, although donations are always welcome.

Enquiries can be made in person, by telephone, post or email. Enquiries in person should be made at the Department's reception area in Room 69a during Department opening hours Monday to Friday
10.00 - 13.00 pm (except Wednesdays)
14.15 - 16.00

A receipt will be given for any objects handed in for examination. Whenever possible, enquiries will be dealt with on the same day, but it may be necessary to leave objects to be collected later or returned by post.

Postal enquiries containing objects should be sent by registered post, with the cost of return registered postage enclosed, to:
The Duty Curator Department of Coins and Medals
The British Museum
Great Russell Street
London WC1B 3DG UK

To ensure a fair service for everyone, no more than ten objects are normally accepted for examination at any time, whether brought to the Department or sent by post.

Neither the Trustees of the British Museum nor members of staff can accept liability for opinions given on obejcts submitted for examinations, or for information provided. They cannot give valuations and do not provide certificates of authenticity.

Anyone seeking advice on valuations, or on buying and selling coins, medals or bank notes, should consult a dealer or auctioneer. Names of dealers and auctioneers can be obtained from the Business Telephone Directory, or from the British Numismatic Trade Association
