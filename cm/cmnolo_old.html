Long-term loans
The Department also assists local and national museums by making longer-term loans of material. These range from a loan of medals to complement the permanent collection at Tate Britain, to involvement in a major project with The Manchester Museum to develop a new Money Gallery.

The new gallery is part of a 19 million pound redevelopment of The Manchester Museum that will provide a number of new displays as well as a discovery centre, lecture theatre, new entrance, shop and café. The Money Gallery represents an unprecedented collaboration between The British Museum and a regional partner. The two museums have worked together to develop the gallery which will benefit from the long-term loan of a large number of objects drawn from the Department's collections, with additional objects coming from the Departments of the Ancient Near East, Greek and Roman Antiquities, and Oriental Antiquities. The opening of the new gallery in 2003 is one part of a British Museum initiative to expand its work with partner institutions around the country, providing unparalleled levels of access to its collections.
