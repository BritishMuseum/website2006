This rare box, one of only eight remaining in the world, was made to contain sacred Buddhist scriptures. The lacquered wooden box is inlaid with mother-of-pearl, tortoise shell and twisted wire and densely covered with a design of chrysanthemum and peony scrolls.

The box dates from the thirteenth Century, when Buddhism became very important in Korea. At this time the skills of lacquer-working reached their peak, and Korean craftsmen developed special methods for inlaying the lacquer with wire-work as part of the design.
