The layout of traditional Korean houses reflected the Confucian principle of separating the sexes and keeping women in the inner rooms. One of the main rooms, the sarangbang, was used by the gentlemen of the house as a study and for receiving visitors, while the women¹s room, the anbang or inner room, was associated with domestic activities such as sewing.

This reconstructed sarangbang was built by Korean craftsmen according to traditional specifications and building techniques including wooden brackets and no nails.
