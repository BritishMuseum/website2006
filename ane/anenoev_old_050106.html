EXHIBITION
Forgotten Empire: the world of Ancient Persia
Until 8 January 2006
A spectacular special exhibition of Achaemenid objects, many loaned from Tehran.


Refurbishment of Rooms 51 – 52

A refurbishment is planned for these galleries, which will be closed from December 2005. Many of the Achaemenid and Sasanian objects have already been placed on temporary display in the two British Museum exhibitions Forgotten Empire: the world of Ancient Persia  (9 September 2005 – 8 January 2006) and Iran before Islam: Propaganda and Religion 224-651 (30 June 2005 – 8 January 2006). A selection of the most important objects will remain on display elsewhere in the museum. We expect to re-open the permanent gallery displays for Ancient Iran and the Alexander to Islam periods in the Near East in early 2007.


SEMINAR FOR ARABIAN STUDIES
International Conference
27 – 29 July 2006

This major international conference returns to The British Museum in Summer 2006.

A wide range of papers are expected on the archaeology, history and languages of the Arabian peninsula, with particular emphasis on new research. In recent years these have included spectacular archaeological discoveries in Saudi Arabia and dramatic new evidence for Nabataean trade.

Further details on the published proceedings, conference and how to book are available on www.arabianseminar.org.uk/


CULTURAL CONNECTIONS OF THE RED SEA
International conference
27 – 28 October 2006

This is the third international Red Sea conference held at The British Museum and jointly organised with the Society for Arabian Studies.

The aim is to focus on this region to better understand connections, both ancient and historic. The focus this year builds on previous themes of Trade and Travel in the Red Sea Region (2002), and People of the Red Sea (2004). The proceedings of both previous conferences have been published.

Details on these and the new conference are available on www.societyforarabianstudies.org
