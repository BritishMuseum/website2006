Although the collections centre on Mesopotamia most of the surrounding areas are well-represented.

A large quantity of Bronze Age pottery from a cemetery at Yortan near Troy in western Turkey was acquired in 1921. The Iranian collection was enhanced with the addition of the Oxus Treasure, a magnificent collection of gold and silver bjects dating from the Achaemenid period (5th-4th century BC). This hoard was found by local villagers on the north bank of the River Oxus, in modern Tadjikistan, between 1877 and 1880. It was bequeathed to the British Museum in 1897 by Augustus Wollaston Franks (1826-97), together with some magnificent pieces of Achaemenid and Sasanian silver plate. A miscellaneous collection of Iranian antiquities was purchased from the German scholar Ernst Herzfeld (1879-1948) in 1936, and the Museum received a share of material from the work of Sir Aurel Stein (1862-1943) in Iran.
