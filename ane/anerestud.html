Closure of study room
12 June - 7 July 2006

We regret to announce that the study room is closed for this period, both for students and personal enquiries. This is due to a shortage of staff, many of whom will be involved at this time in the preparation of a major loan exhibition to China. 

All the material in the collection, whether on exhibition or in storage, is made available to researchers in the departmental Study Room.

The Department's Study Room is located in the historic Arched Room. Access is via the large double doors at the north end of the Egyptian Sculpture Gallery (room 4), at the bottom of the West Stairs. Members of the public can be directed by information staff or find the Arched Room through the Visit Guide, which is available throughout the Museum.

Both archaeological finds and cuneiform tablets can be examined here, but there is a particularly strong demand for tablets. Scholars from all around the world come to use this facility, with the result that this resource has become one of the main international centres for the study of the Ancient Near East.

Opening times and conditions
The Study Room is open on weekdays
10.00 -13.00 and 14.00 -16.00

Visitors wishing to look at objects which are in the study collections or on display should apply to the Department. Space is limited and visitors are requested to make an appointment as far in advance as possible, giving details of the objects. It would be helpful if visitors could give some indication of the length of their stay.

Applications for admission must be supported either by a member of the academic staff of the Museum or by evidence of identification (passport, driving licence) and by a letter of recommendation from an academic institution or a person of recognised authority.
