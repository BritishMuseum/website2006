The British Museum Research Publications are published by the Trustees of the British Museum. The series was launched in 1978 as the Occasional Papers, and some 140 titles have been produced. The aim is to make available as widely as possible research and information on different aspects of the Museum's collections.

Between 6 to 8 titles are published each year, the subject matter ranging from conference proceedings to specialist monographs on classes of object, from archaeological excavation, scientific research and conservation reports to small exhibition and collection catalogues. Some titles are published as hard copy books, but others are produced as electronic web-publications (see below). All titles are peer reviewed prior to publication.

As of 1 April 2004 the series, whether electronic or hard copy, is to be renamed the British Museum Research Publications.

The Research Publications editor can be contacted by e-mail.
