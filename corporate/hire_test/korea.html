The Korea Foundation Gallery (Room 67) explores the rich traditions of Korean art through its ceramics, sculpture, paintings, illuminated manuscripts and textiles. This gallery is a light, airy space, perfect for more intimate receptions of less than a hundred guests. The centrepiece of the gallery is a reconstructed Sarangbang, or gentleman's study; a fine example of Korean craftmanship and architecture.

Receptions in the Korea Foundation Gallery
Maximum capacity: 100 standing
Smoking is not permitted anywhere within the Museum. Dancing and recorded/amplified music are not permitted in the galleries but may be permitted in the Great Court by arrangement.

Please note that red wine is not permitted in the Korea
Foundation Gallery.
