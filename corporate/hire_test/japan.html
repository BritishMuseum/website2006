Due to the fragile nature of many of the objects in the collection, the Japanese Galleries (Rooms 92-94) host a changing programme of exhibitions throughout the year. These temporary displays address the arts of Buddhist and secular Japan, from prints and printed books to exquisite metalwork, including swords and sword furniture. The subtle lighting in this gallery creates a wonderful atmosphere for canapé receptions. Please telephone the Corporate Services Office for information on current and forthcoming exhibitions.

Receptions in the Japanese Galleries
Maximum capacity: 350 standing
Smoking is not permitted anywhere within the Museum. Dancing and recorded/amplified music are not permitted in the galleries but may be permitted in the Great Court by arrangement.
Please note that red wine is not permitted in the Japanese Galleries
