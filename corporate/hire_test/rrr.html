For 150 years the Reading Room has been home to scholars and revolutionaries, writers and poets, students and eccentrics. Located at the centre of the Great Court, the Reading Room remains a working library, open to scholars and researchers from across the world. The breathtaking azure, blue and cream domed ceiling has recently been restored to its original splendour, making this an elegant and imposing setting for receptions. Up to 200 guests can gather in the library's viewing area to admire the room as it first appeared in 1857, providing
one of the most memorable sights in London.
