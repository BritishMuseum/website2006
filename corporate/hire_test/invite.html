1. A proof of your invitation must be sent to the relevant Event Manager in the Corporate Services Office prior to printing and distribution.

2. Please ensure that the guest entry point into the building (ie North or South Entrance) is included on your invitation.

3. Please ensure that details of the exact location of your event are included on the invitation (including Gallery or Room number).

4. Depending on the venue, it may be necessary that guests are requested not to wear sharp heels that may cause damage to the fabric of the building. This is particularly important for events taking place in the Kings Library. The Event Manager will notify you if this applies to your event.

5. The British Museum logo may only be used with prior written permission of the Corporate Services Office, within the guidelines of the Museum's design directive. Details available from the Corporate Services Office.

6. Images of The British Museum may only be used if they are digitally unaltered, and with prior written approval of the Corporate Services Office.

7. The British Museum can supply a Venue plan to include on invitations. For details please contact the Corporate Services Office.

8. Please ensure that your invitation includes the words "Entry to The British Museum will only be guaranteed on presentation of this invitation" or words to this effect. This is particularly important for guest entry before 9.00am or after 6.00pm.

9. When registering attendees, please remember to ask your guests whether they have any special dietary requirements (eg vegetarian or allergies). Caterers often allocate these meals on an individual basis.

10. When registering attendees, please note that parking for disabled drivers is usually available by prior arrangement (usually up to a maximum of 4 cars). Please contact the Corporate Services Office to book.

11. Clients are strongly advised not to send out invitations to guests until the event contract has been signed by the client and the deposit invoice has been paid to confirm the booking.
