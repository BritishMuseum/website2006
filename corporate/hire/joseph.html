The Sir Joseph Hotung Gallery of Oriental Antiquities (Room 33) is one of the Museum's largest galleries. The gallery walls are embellished with gold leaf, and objects are displayed in their original mahogany cases. This sets a magnificent scene within which to view the largest collection of South and Southeast Asian antiquities in Europe.

The gallery is divided into two parts. The first, Chinese in theme, houses tomb figures, lacquerware, Buddhist deities and important porcelain objects. The second section includes many Southeast Asian objects of religious significance, including early Buddhist sculptures from Ghandara and Amaravati, and exquisite South Indian bronzes. In the centre of the gallery are gilt-bronze images and ritual objects from Tibet and Nepal, making this a wonderful venue for large receptions and small, intimate dinners alike.
