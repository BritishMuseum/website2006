The Pamela Hartwell Room is located in the Trustees' Suite, overlooking the Museum's Forecourt and Colonnade, with private access via the Director's Entrance. This stately, carpeted room has a high ceiling, a 100 year-old Bacarrat chandelier and imposing oil portraits of kings and statesmen, and makes a beautiful setting for receptions and seated dinners. It is available throughout the week for daytime and evening private functions

Private Dining and Receptions in the Pamela Hartwell Room
Maximum capacity: 80 standing 50 seated

The Hartwell Room has furniture to accommodate 30 seated guests. For larger dinners additional furniture will be required.
Please note that the Hartwell Room is also available for breakfasts, and that a discounted rate is negotiable for part day hire of the venue.
