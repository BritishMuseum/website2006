The John Addis Gallery of Islamic Art (Room 34) contains exhibits that date from the 7th century AD, from Spain to India. The displays in the gallery cover science and magic, arms, armour and coinage, and showcase some of the best examples of Islamic pottery outside the Islamic world. Highlights of the gallery include treasures from the Mughal Empire and beautiful decorative tile work. A perfect venue for receptions of up to 250 guests.

Receptions in the John Addis Gallery of Islamic Art
Maximum capacity: 250 standing
Smoking is not permitted anywhere within the Museum. Dancing and recorded/amplified music are not permitted in the galleries but may be permitted in the Great Court by arrangement.

Please note that red wine is not permitted in the John Addis Gallery of Islamic Art.
