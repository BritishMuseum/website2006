Reception and Dinner facilities

The Museum offers an exclusive venue with a range of elegant settings for entertaining guests among some of the finest antiquities and works of art in the world. An evening event provides a unique and prestigious opportunity to enjoy the Museum and its treasures after public visiting hours. The Museum can accommodate banquets, buffet and drinks receptions, combined with private views of special exhibitions if desired. Each occasion is planned by the Museum's experienced Corporate Affairs staff according to the client's special requirements.

For further details contact: corporate@thebritishmuseum.ac.uk

Capacities
 

Maximum capacities of each of the galleries vary.Using the largest spaces, receptions for up to 1000 guests, and dinners for up to 270 guests can be accommodated.

Rooms
  Receptions and dinners can be based in a wide range of the Museum's galleries.

One of the most frequently used is the Nereid Room. The room takes its name from the Nereid Monument, built around 400 BC, displayed within it. Clients may also have access to the Egyptian Sculpture Gallery and the Roman Room.

The Joseph E Hotung Gallery of Oriental Antiquities, the Prints & Drawings Gallery and the Japanese Galleries at the north side of the Museum may also be used for receptions.

A full description of galleries available can be supplied.

Private views of special exhibitions can also be arranged.
Insurance / indemnity
  Clients are required to complete a form of indemnity covering their event. Caterers employed by clients are also required to complete a form of indemnity.
