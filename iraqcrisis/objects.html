Objects from Iraq in The British Museum

View objects from Iraq in The British Museum
on the Museum's objects database COMPASS.

The objects below have been chosen because they are similar to objects looted or damaged in the Iraq Museum, Baghdad.


Crushed female skull with jewellery. From the Great Death Pit within the Royal cemetery at Ur, c2600 B.C. Find out more.
Lyre decorated with lapis lazuli inlay and a golden bull's head. A similar example held in the Baghdad Museum was stripped of its gold decoration. Find out more.
Carved ivory plaque showing a lioness attacking a man. From the Assyrian capital, Nimrud, 9th-8th century B.C. Find out more.
Colossal statue of a winged human-headed bull designed to protect Assyrian palaces from demonic forces. From the North-West Palace of Ashurnasirpal II Nimrud, c.883-859 BC. Similar examples have been damaged during looting at sites within Iraq. Find out more.
