The Iraq Crisis - British Museum Statement, 13 June 2003

The British Museum, UNESCO and the international museums and academic community stand ready to help their Iraqi colleagues make good (as far as possible) the losses and damage caused by the recent war and the long period of sanctions. As soon as the occupation authorities have decided who are the proper people to lead the Iraqi museums and antiquities services, we shall start planning for the future.

In the meantime Sarah Collins, an Arabic-speaking curator from the Department of the Ancient Near East, is already in Baghdad working with ORHA and providing curatorial expertise in the Iraq Museum, and a team of curators and conservators led by John Curtis departed for Baghdad on 11th June. The intention of this team is to undertake a detailed assessment of curatorial and conservation needs in the museum with a view to sending out to Iraq a team of conservators in the autumn. It is intended that this team should include conservators drawn from the international community.

A full report of John Curtis's team's visit will be made to the meeting of international Assyrian specialists to be held at the British Museum from 7th to the 10th July. We hope many colleagues from Iraq will be present.
