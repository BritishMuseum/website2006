International Conference organised by the British Museum and UNESCO to agree International Support for Museums and Archaeological Sites in Iraq.

Tuesday 29th April 2003, British Museum

Dr Donny George, of the Iraq Museum, Baghdad, representing the Iraq Department of Antiquities, reported on the state of the Iraq Museum and on what was known about the other museums and archaeological sites in the country.

The meeting, at which all the major centres of expertise in Mesopotamian antiquities were represented unanimously made the following resolutions:

  • the cultural reconstruction of Iraq should be led by the Iraq Department of Antiquities, drawing on the expertise and support of the international community.

  • the coalition forces in Iraq should take all possible steps to prevent further export of antiquities from Iraq

  • the Security Council should impose sanctions on trade in antiquities illegally exported from Iraq as requested by the Director-General of Unesco.

Dr George made the following recommendations, which were endorsed by all present:

1. Staffing

i. Staff salaries to be paid (in the first instance by ORHA), and an urgent review of salary scales to be undertaken by the Iraq Department of Antiquities.

ii. Staff numbers in State Board of Antiquities and Heritage to be restored to full strength, i.e.

c. 400 archaeologists
c. 1600 guards
c. 600 technicians
c. 2600 staff in whole country

2. Equipment

New equipment and facilities needed (ORHA?)

Cars
Computers and printers
Scanners
Cameras
Furniture throughout museum
Structural repairs in Iraq Museum
Showcases
Security systems
Climate control in museum
Controlled storage
Conservation laboratory
Photographic studio
Email network

3. Surveys/Conservation

i. Check of sites and monuments to be undertaken by staff of Iraq Department of Antiquities with whatever international assistance they request and all damage to be recorded in a six-month period. In cases of war damage, results to be verified by ORHA nominees. Results of survey to be published.

ii. Lists of objects stolen from sites and museums to be drawn up by staff of the Iraq Department of Antiquities with appropriate assistance.

iii. Conservation assessment of objects in Iraqi museums to be undertaken by senior conservator nominated by the British Museum, working with Iraqi and international colleagues.

iv. Conservation materials to be ordered and supplied.

v. Teams of conservators to be sent to Iraq to work with local conservators.

vi. Small group of curators to be sent to Baghdad to assist with audit of objects in storerooms.

vii. Training programmes to be established in conservation and archaeology.

To carry out the tasks of survey and conservation, the Iraqi colleagues must be able to call on the best expertise available, anywhere in the world. All the institutions represented pledged their support. Many other offers of help and of funding have already been received. At the request of Dr George and of Unesco, it was agreed that:

The British Museum should act as a clearing-house for offers of foreign assistance working in consultation with Unesco, and in close collaboration with the Iraq Department of Antiquities.

  • Relevant books and publications to be donated to Iraqi institutions.

  • The journal Sumer to resume publication as soon as possible.

  • Resumption of archaeological and ethnographic work by Department of Antiquities.

  • Resumption of archaeological work by foreign teams, with the fullest involvement of Iraqi colleagues, following the model established in 1980s.

  • A separate plan of action to be drawn up for libraries and archives by the Iraq Department of Antiquities.

The British Museum, after discussion with Dr George and Unesco, would circulate details of how the clearing house of mechanism would operate.
