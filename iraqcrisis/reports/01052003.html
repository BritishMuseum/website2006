On 1 May 2003 British Museum curators, Sheila Canby, Venetia Porter, Irving Finkel and Sally Irving met with Antiquities, Islamic art and coin dealers and collectors to discuss the looting of the Iraq museum.

Following the Meeting on the Iraq museum on 29th April it was thought it was advisable to have a meeting to inform these dealers and collectors about what had taken place in Baghdad and to make known to them the existence of the 'RED LIST' and the UNESCO initiatives regarding the database of objects. They were also provided with website details (Art Newspaper, Chicago etc.) and given the ongoing list of publications on the contents of the museum made by Chicago. They were shown images of the looting and information about the forthcoming Private members bill on stolen antiquities.
