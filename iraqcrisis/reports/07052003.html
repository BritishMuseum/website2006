Report from International Council of Museums (ICOM) Working Group: Emergency Red List of Iraqi Antiquities at Risk. 7 May 2003, Interpol General Secretariat, Lyons by David Lewis, Department of the Ancient Near East.

The purpose of this meeting was to generate an ICOM Red List which would aid the identification of stolen cultural objects from Iraq. The Red List will be produced for both print and web. The current ICOM Redlist homepage is http://icom.museum/redlist/ . The Iraqi information is yet to be added.

**************UPDATE*****************************************************************
The Red List is now available at http://icom.museum/redlist/irak/en/index.html

To be of most use it was agreed the Red List needed to be of generic types of objects. A list was produced which outlined the objects types most at risk. At the end of the day this list was still considered to be too long to be of practical use. It was left to a small working party to slim line the document to make it as useful as possible to end users, particularly law enforcement agencies and customs officials. The document will be illustrated using examples of the object types from other collections. This is a summary of the list. Sculpture and Reliefs and Vessels both had six or so sub categories.

1. Cuneiform tablets and other object with cuneiform text on
2. Cylinder seals
3. Stamp seals
4. Ivory
5. Sculpture and reliefs
6. Vessels
7. Jewellery
8. Weapons and armour
9. Coins
10. Manuscripts
11. Architectural and furniture fragments

The academic community's opinion was greatly aided by Interpol and Brian Hanley, an Australian security specialist, in making the terminology as broadly useful as possible.

In addition to the main discussion, I presented an illustrated list of stolen objects compiled at the British Museum. A copy of this information was given to ICOM and Interpol.
