Head of Sokrates

This is a Roman copy of a lost Greek original.
The nose had been lost from this head and it was decided that it should be restored before being put on display. Other portrait heads of Sokrates are known and so it was possible to be reasonably sure of the appearance of the original nose.

The nose was restored using a synthetic resin and filler mixture and it was painted to match the colour of the surrounding marble.

Restoration of stone objects is not normally done for cosmetic reasons, but on this occasion the loss of the nose marred the appearance of this head.
