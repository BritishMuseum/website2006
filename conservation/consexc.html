Involvement in excavation

Conservators take part in excavations, helping to devise lifting processes or carrying out essential stabilisation as objects emerge from the ground. Rapid conservation of freshly excavated material is sometimes essential for objects and assists archaeologists to asses the site.

The conservation of previously buried material requires a special approach. The prescence of salts from the surroundings may mean that objects are especially prone to chemical or mechanical damage. As traces of soil are removed mineralised organic remains may be seen by the experienced conservator.
