Cleaning

This marble foot of a colossal statue comes from Alexandria in Egypt. The statue probably dates from the 2nd century AD and may have represented the Egyptian god Serapis.

With some exceptions, visitors are not allowed to touch objects on open display. This object is featured on the Egyptian Touch Tour (No9) for the visually impaired. The results of repeated handling can be clearly seen.

The conservator is using a steam cleaner and a sponge to remove the accumulated grime to reveal the original white marble.
