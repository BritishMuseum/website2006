The primary role of the Department of Conservation is to conserve (to care for and preserve) the collections of the British Museum, for the benefit of present and future generations.

In pursuit of this, the Department aims to be recognised, both nationally and internationally, as a centre of excellence for the conservation of archaeological and ethnographic artefacts and of fine and applied art.

The British Museum has a very long history of employment of conservators and research into conservation. Today, conservators are involved in many aspects of museum life, being involved in excavations, mounting exhibitions, as well as research and performing conservation on objects.
