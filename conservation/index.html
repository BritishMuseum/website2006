Objects in the Museum may need to be conserved so that they can be accessible, exhibited and studied safely. Conservators can help to provide them with good storage and display conditions to minimise deterioration.Objects may also need to be cleaned, reconstructed, restored and stabilised. Research is performed to identify and control the causes of deterioration and to develop new conservation techniques.The study of objects while being conserved and their deterioration processes contributes to a better understanding of the Museum's collections.

Our work can be seen not only in the galleries but also at public events and in our publications.
