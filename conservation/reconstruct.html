Reconstructions

Greek Vase

A ship, HMS Colossus, carrying a collection of Ancient Greek Vases from the Mediterranean to England, sank off the Isles of Scilly in 1798. Approximately 300 vases were a part of the cargo. Private salvage procedures, begun in the 1970's, recovered thousands of sherds of the red and black ware. The collection was subsequently brought to the British Museum for conservation treatment and study.

Initially, the sherds were put through a desalination process to remove the soluble salts left by the sea water and then they were left to dry. Sherds were numbered and sorted looking for similar patterns and joins allowing them to be grouped into individual vessels. Where enough sherds existed to form a profile of a vase, reconstruction began. In some instances, there was substantial amounts of loss to the vases. An elaborate process for assembling the sherds was devised. Sherds were joined with an adhesive and positioned using clamps, and strips of dental wax. Missing areas were filled using a fine grade of casting plaster. This set quickly and was easily shaped using spatulas and abrasive papers.
