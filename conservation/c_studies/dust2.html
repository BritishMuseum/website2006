Dust Deposition

Technique

Dust levels can be measured either as total particulates in a given volume of air or as the amount of particulates that deposit under a given set of environmental conditions. The latter is of most interest in museums and therefore the chosen method measured dust deposition by equating the amount of dust deposited to the loss of surface reflectance of a horizontally mounted glass slide.

Glass deposition slides are cleaned and numbered before exposure. The initial surface reflectance is measured and then the slides are exposed horizontally for a period of one week. After exposure, the surface reflectance of the slide is re-measured and the percentage loss of gloss is calculated. Results are expressed in soiling units per week, where one soiling unit is defined as a one per cent loss of gloss on a slide exposed for a period of one week. All measurements were made using equipment at Queen Mary and Westfield College, London.
