Dust Deposition

Results

Background deposition levels are typically 5 - 15 soiling units per week in Gallery environments.Values of 0 -5 soiling units per week are more typical for storage and staff areas.

The major cause of dust deposition in gallery areas is the high number of visitors. The higher deposition levels observed in these areas can be
attributed to the generation and circulation of particulates caused by visitor movement. Deposition levels increased when circulation routes were altered in the Museum (due to the British Library moving to a new site and consequent closure of the King's library); those galleries with increased visitor numbers saw deposition levels increase by on average 25 - 30%.

The sealing measures introduced to separate the Great Court building site from the rest of the Museum site have been effective and have contained the vast majority of dust generated by the work to the localised environment. After building work commenced, a minor increase in deposition levels was observed in storage and staff areas, though overall levels remained low.

Further work
Dust levels will be monitored throughout the entire course of the building works. Research will also begin on assessing the contribution of particulates to the deterioration of artefacts.
