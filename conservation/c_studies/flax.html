Preventative

Black-dyed Maori textiles

Traditional Maori textiles were made almost exclusively of fibre extracted from New Zealand flax (Phormium tenax). Usually the natural fibre was used undyed, but sometimes it was dyed. By far the most common colour was black. The black yarn was used for decorative borders and tassels on cloaks. Today the black-dyed fibre presents conservators with great problems as it deteriorates much faster than the undyed fibre.

Objects with black dyed yarn are prone to damage and are difficult to handle. Some cloaks with tassels or tags have only black tufts to show where the attachments had once been placed. Research has revealed the deterioration mechanism and methods for slowing down the rate of degradation.
