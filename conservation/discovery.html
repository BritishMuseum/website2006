Egyptian wall painting

Conservators examine objects very carefully before they begin work. This assessment helps to determine the conservation treatment, but it can also reveal technical information about the
object.

This wall painting had to be removed from display in advance of the Great Court works. Assessment using a microscope showed that the surface was very fragile and needed consolidation before the painting could be moved. The detailed examination also revealed for the first time that the eye of the cat is gilded ie: gold leaf has been used to produce the yellow colour.

Egyptian wall painting 'Fowling in the marshes'
EA 37977. From the tomb of Nebamun
