Today is a good day: remembering Chief Dan George

Directed by Loretta Todd, 1998, 48 mins
Chief Dan George became a symbol of Indian determination to challenge stereotypes at the centre of American movies. His reality – as a member of a First Nations community and as an actor of extraordinary depth and commitment – came to be as important as the roles he played. Loretta Todd’s film is a compelling exploration of the history and images that shaped Chief Dan George’s life as a maker of new kinds of history and real forms of image.
