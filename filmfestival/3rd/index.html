Feast and Transformation -
from the North Pacific Coast

Friday 3 – Sunday 5 February 2006

£20, concessions £15 (or £10 per day, concessions £7.50)

BP Lecture Theatre, Clore Education Centre (CEC)

This year’s festival takes as its inspiration the potlatch of the British Columbia First Nations: the traditional giftgiving ceremony of wealth, community and identity.

The programme includes classic documentaries by Western filmmakers, such as Edward Curtis’ In the Land of the Head-Hunters (1914), as well as showcasing work by contemporary Native directors and producers. Sir David Attenborough opens the festival with The Crooked Beak of Heaven, his classic study of the relationship between art and life for the Gitskan, Haida, and Kwakwaka’wakw First Nations.
