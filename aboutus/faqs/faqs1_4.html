The British Museum's collections include many famous treasures, but we are often asked about objects in other museums and galleries. These include Tutankhamun, the dinosaurs, the Magna Carta and Venus de Milo.

Object Location
Where is Tutankhamun? In the Cairo Museum, Egypt. The exhibition 'Treasures of Tutankhamun' was held at the British Museum March-December 1972.
Where are the dinosaurs? The British Museum's collection now forms the Natural History Museum, London.
Where is the Koh-i-Noor diamond? The Tower of London
Where is the Venus de Milo? Musée du Louvre, Paris
Where is Michelangelo's David? Galleria dell'Accademia, Florence
Where is Hammurabi's Law Code? Musée du Louvre, Paris
Where is the Magna Carta? There are three copies: at the British Library, London, and Salisbury and Lincoln Cathedrals
Where is the Alfred Jewel? Ashmolean Museum, Oxford
Where is the Book of Kells? Trinity College Library, Dublin
Where is the Domesday Book? Public Record Office, London
Where is the Winged Victory of Samothrace? Musée du Louvre, Paris
Where is the Babbage computer? Science Museum, London
Where is Trajan's Column?

Rome. There is also a cast in the Victoria & Albert Museum, London
Where is the head of Nefertiti?

Egyptian Museum, Berlin
There is a copy in the Wellcome Museum of the History of Medicine, Science Museum, London
Where is the Pergamon Treasure? Pergammon Museum, Berlin
Where is the Mappa Mundi? Hereford Cathedral, England
Where is Montezuma's Crown? Museum of Ethnology, Vienna
Where is the Moabite Stone? Musée du Louvre, Paris
Where is the Bayeux Tapestry?

Musée de la Tapisserie Mathilde a Bayeaux, France
