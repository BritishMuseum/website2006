See over 100 of the world's greatest historic step in the heart of London. Captured over 40 years, the aerial
photography of Swiss photographer Georg Gerster will take visitors on a tour of five continents. Gerster has
flown over archaeological sites in 111 countries to compile a photographic portfolio of epic revelation.

The exhibition will be themed by continent and objects from the Museum's permanent collection will feature
alongside Gerster's photographs to provide an added insight into the people behind these extraordinary
locations. Some of the sites included are the astonishingly vast Great Wall of China, the sacred aboriginal
site of Uluru (Ayres' Rock) in Australia, and the ancient cities of Babylon (in modern-day Iraq), Leptis Magna
(in modern-day Libya) and Teotihuacan (in modern-day Mexico). Gerster's photographs even show the hidden
similarities across seemingly unrelated cultures, such as the Nasca Owl Man from Peru, the Blythe
Man in California, and the Cerne Abbas Giant in Dorset.

As well as focusing on the wonders of these archaeological sites, the exhibition also serves as a reminder
of the fragility of many of these important locations and that positive action is required to preserve our common
cultural heritage. This is your chance to view the world from a truly different perspective.
